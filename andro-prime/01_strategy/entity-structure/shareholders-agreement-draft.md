## 3. Voting and decision-making

3.1 The **B Ordinary Shares carry no right to receive notice of, attend, speak or vote** at any general meeting of the Company, save as set out in clause 3.3.

3.2 Accordingly, control of all shareholder decisions (ordinary and special resolutions) rests with the holder of the A Ordinary Shares (Keith) for so long as he holds a majority of the voting shares.

3.3 Notwithstanding clause 3.1, the B Ordinary Shares retain the right to vote (and the consent of the holder shall be required) **only** on a resolution to **vary, abrogate or adversely affect the class rights of the B Ordinary Shares**. This reflects the statutory protection of class rights (sections 630–640 Companies Act) which cannot be excluded.

3.4 The Shareholders acknowledge that Ewa retains all **non-excludable statutory rights** as a member, including the right to petition under section 994 (unfair prejudice) and to apply for just and equitable winding up, none of which is waived by this agreement.

> **DRAFTING NOTE:** 3.3 and 3.4 are deliberately included for honesty and enforceability — you cannot fully contract a member out of class-rights votes or s.994. Leaving them out would not give Keith more control; it would just make the document look one-sided and easier to attack.

## 8. Drag-along and tag-along

8.1 **Drag-along.** If Keith wishes to sell his shares to a bona fide arm's-length third party, he may require Ewa to sell all of her shares to that buyer **on the same terms per share**, by serving a drag notice.

8.2 **Tag-along.** If Keith proposes to transfer shares that would result in a change of control, Ewa shall have the right to require the buyer to purchase her shares **on the same terms per share** before that transfer completes.

> **DRAFTING NOTE:** Tag-along (8.2) is the main exit protection a non-voting minority has, and including it materially strengthens the fairness/enforceability of the whole agreement. I recommend keeping it.
