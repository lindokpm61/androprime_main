## The "Voy £29 collision" - verified

The £29 figure is unverified and appears wrong. Voy's finger-prick testosterone test standing (already-discounted) price is **£33.95, down from £54.99 (~38% off)** - a permanent sale anchor. Markers on the finger-prick entry test are Total + Free testosterone (the 5-marker/venous work is the £49.95+ enhanced step). **Replace "£29" in prior docs with "£33.95 (from £54.99)".** The collision vs the £99 Kit 1 still stands (a ~£65 gap on a materially similar entry panel), but the number was wrong.

## White-space synthesis (honest)

**NOT white space (do not claim):**
- "Interpretation + longitudinal tracking + trust" is TAKEN (Thriva owns it; Forth credible). Kill that claim.
- Best accreditation story is Medichecks' (UKAS ISO 15189 + CQC + GMC). Andro Prime can match "UKAS-accredited lab" via its supplier but cannot out-trust Medichecks on paper.
- Biological-age / longevity packaging is contested and off-limits under Phase 0 anyway.
- Provider-agnostic upload is only partially open (Thriva accepts external results, manual only) - a feature, not a moat, and it fights Thriva on home turf.

**Where genuine, defensible space exists.** The category splits into two camps with a gap between:
1. **Men's-health testosterone brands (Numan/Voy/Manual/Hims)** = treatment funnels. Their interpretation is structurally conflicted - they profit when your result is "low."
2. **Diagnostics generalists (Thriva/Forth/Medichecks)** = honest brokers that don't prescribe, but are NOT men's-health-positioned; testosterone is one panel among hundreds.

**Nobody occupies: a male-positioned testosterone brand that is deliberately NOT a treatment funnel.** That is the slot Andro Prime's constraints force it into - the constraint is the moat.

### The single most defensible position

> **"The men's testosterone test with no reason to sell you testosterone."**
> A named GMC-registered GP reviews your result; a low result routes to your NHS GP, not a subscription; a UKAS-accredited UK lab; one UK data controller; your data is never sold.

Why it is most defensible:
- The treatment-funnel incumbents are commercially barred from the claim - their revenue IS the upsell; the honest-broker position would break their model.
- The generalist diagnostics players don't make it because they aren't men's-health brands, so there's no low-T anxiety narrative for them to disarm.
- It converts every constraint into a selling point: no-prescribe to "conflict-free"; GP-referral-not-upsell to "we route you out, not up"; single UK controller + no data sale to a privacy contrast against funnels that harvest low-T/ED intent.
- It is Phase-0 legal: wellness-only, no clinical claims, supplements deferred, Vitall never named ("UKAS-accredited UK lab" suffices).

**Two honesty caveats:** (1) do not assert competitors sell data (unverified) - frame as Andro Prime's positive commitment plus the structural observation that funnel businesses ingest low-T/ED intent for targeting. (2) "Named GMC GP" is a thin differentiator (rivals also use GMC reviewers) - lead on "conflict-free / no product to sell"; treat the named GP as supporting texture.
