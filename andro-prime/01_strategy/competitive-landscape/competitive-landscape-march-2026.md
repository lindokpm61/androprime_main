## 1. Market Consolidation Summary

The UK private TRT market has undergone significant consolidation since the V6.1 model was written. Two acquisitions have fundamentally changed the competitive dynamics:

**Acquisition 1: Manual → Voy + Optimale absorption**
Manual rebranded to Voy and absorbed Optimale's patient base. Optimale's existing patients are being directed to Voy accounts. Combined entity now claims 1M+ members across UK, Germany, and Brazil. Voy retains Dr Jeff Foster as Medical Director and is hiring aggressively — a Growth Marketing Manager with a seven-figure experimentation budget and an influencer marketing team building a UK creator network.

**Acquisition 2: Medichecks acquires Leger Clinic**
Medichecks (UK's largest private blood testing company, 300+ tests, decade-plus track record) acquired Leger Clinic (founded 1987, CQC "Outstanding" rated, 30+ years clinical TRT expertise under Dr Doug Savage). This creates a fully integrated diagnostic-to-treatment pipeline — the exact model Andro Prime is building. Medichecks can now funnel low-testosterone blood test results directly into Leger's TRT service.

**Regulatory tailwind (US, but globally relevant):**
In December 2025, an FDA expert panel recommended expanding TRT approved indications to include age-related low testosterone and removing its Schedule III controlled substance designation. While US-specific, this signals a global shift toward TRT normalisation that will increase media coverage, public awareness, and addressable market size in the UK.

## 3. What Consolidation Means for Andro Prime

### 3.1 Threats

**Diagnostic-to-treatment pipeline model is now proven.** Medichecks buying Leger validates exactly what you're building. But it also means the biggest blood testing brand in the UK is now executing the same strategy with existing traffic, trust, and SEO authority. Your Product 1 test kit competes directly with Medichecks' consumer testing products, and they can now convert those results into their own TRT service.

**Price pressure intensifies.** Voy at £99/month all-in is nearly half your planned £185 ARPU. Leger (now within Medichecks) and One Day Tests both undercut you significantly. The price gap between Andro Prime and the cheapest credible alternative is now £86/month — that's £1,032/year. Your positioning must justify that premium convincingly or patients will choose the cheaper option.

**Marketing spend asymmetry widening.** Voy is hiring a Growth Marketing Manager with a dedicated seven-figure experimentation budget. Medichecks has years of SEO authority. You have a LinkedIn following and a content engine. The paid acquisition battlefield is now structurally unfavourable for a bootstrapped new entrant.

### 3.2 Opportunities

**Consolidation creates churn.** Every acquisition creates disrupted patients. Optimale patients migrated to Voy. Leger patients integrating into Medichecks Group. Some won't like the corporate transition. Leger is already actively poaching patients from other providers with a £59 transfer consultation. You can do the same once live.

**The personal touch gap widens.** Voy with 1M members cannot speak like a real person. Medichecks Group is a diagnostics corporation bolting on clinical services. Neither can offer what Andro Prime proposes: a personal brand founder who's been through TRT himself, a named GP who patients actually know, and communication in customer language rather than clinical marketing speak. The bigger they get, the more impersonal they become, and the wider your differentiation becomes.

**Customer language gap remains unaddressed.** Despite consolidation, none of the major players have shifted their messaging. Voy still says "Feel healthier, happier and stronger." Medichecks says "simplify access to testing, treatment, and expert-led care." Balance My Hormones says "optimise your hormone balance." Nobody is saying "knackered all the time," "not myself anymore," or "done with the NHS runaround." The Reddit research gap is still wide open.

**NHS gap targeting is uncontested paid search territory.** Men with testosterone 8-12 nmol/L rejected by the NHS are a high-intent audience actively seeking private alternatives. The V6.1 model identified this as a distinct acquisition channel (£80-120 CAC). Paid search targeting "GP said testosterone normal," "rejected for TRT NHS" captures men who are already educated and motivated. None of the major players are running targeted campaigns on these terms.

**The FDA tailwind grows the market.** Expanded TRT awareness driven by US regulatory changes increases the total number of men thinking about testosterone testing. More awareness = more top-of-funnel demand for your £39 test kit, regardless of downstream TRT competition.

### 3.3 Strategic Implications

**Pricing needs re-examination.** The V6.1 Base case ARPU of £185 was positioned as mid-market between BMH (£149-189) and Manual (£199). But Manual is now Voy at £99, and Leger (within Medichecks) offers TRT from £80. The "mid-market" has shifted downward. Either you need to reposition as explicitly premium (justifying £185 through service quality, personalisation, and clinical relationship) or you need to reconsider whether £185 is sustainable given the new competitive floor.

**Product 1 lab partner selection is now strategically critical.** Medichecks is no longer a viable white-label partner — they're a direct competitor with their own TRT pipeline. Any lab partner with TRT treatment ambitions (One Day Tests) carries conflict-of-interest risk. Neutral infrastructure providers (Thriva Solutions, Vitall) are now the only safe choices. See `05_partners/labs/lab-partner-rankings-addendum.md`.

**Content and personal brand are your primary moat.** In a market where the funded players compete on price, SEO, and paid acquisition, your only sustainable advantage is the personal brand and authentic voice. Keith's story, Ewa's credentials, customer language — these cannot be replicated by corporate competitors regardless of budget.

**Two-entity structure matters more now.** With Medichecks proving that diagnostic platforms command premium acquisition multiples, Prima Medical Group's value as a separable platform entity is strengthened. A buyer in Year 7 would see Prima as analogous to what Medichecks built — a diagnostic and marketing infrastructure that can serve multiple clinics.

## 5. Recommended Actions

1. **Revisit pricing strategy.** Model a scenario at £145-165 ARPU and assess whether unit economics still work at V6.1 COGS of £59 + £30 GP + £21 ops = £110. At £150 ARPU, EBITDA per patient drops to £40/month (26.7% margin). Still positive, but tight.

2. **Accelerate content engine.** In a market where paid acquisition favours funded players, organic content is your competitive weapon. Every week you're not posting is a week Voy and Medichecks are building SEO and paid acquisition dominance without you in the conversation.

3. **Position explicitly as the anti-corporate alternative.** Don't try to look like Voy or Medichecks. Look like the opposite. Personal, transparent, plain-speaking, founder-led.

4. **Lock in a neutral lab partner immediately.** The Medichecks/Leger merger means the window for neutral white-label partnerships may narrow further. Thriva Solutions is the priority. See `05_partners/labs/thriva/`.

5. **Monitor for further consolidation.** If Balance My Hormones or The Men's Health Clinic gets acquired, the independent tier collapses entirely and Andro Prime's positioning as "the last personal TRT provider" becomes even more differentiated — or the market signals that only scaled players survive.
