**Single riskiest assumption to bet on:** C1: that longitudinal tracking is open whitespace. It's whitespace on Reddit's power-user forums (who reject the existing tools); it is not whitespace commercially (Forth/Thriva/Medichecks already ship it).

1. First-party prevalence + WTP: does the mainstream £99 buyer want a dashboard, or "tell me I'm fine"? (quiz drop-off, post-purchase survey, Van Westendorp). Everything rides on this and nobody has it.
2. The competitor teardown FILE 1 promised but never did: Forth/Thriva/Medichecks/Numan/Function on tracking+interpretation+trust+price, plus the still-unanswered Voy £29 collision. Confirm the lane is open before building for it.

## Executive verdict

1. **Biggest gap:** no real competitor teardown was done, and the one Fable ran refutes the core thesis: Forth (tracking-first), Thriva (trend dashboard) and Medichecks (clinician interpretation) already occupy the "open lane," so the roadmap's headline moat is contested ground, not whitespace.
2. **Best new revenue idea:** the prepaid baseline + 90-day retest bundle (B1): turns the files' own "retest is the emotional payoff" insight into a real SKU that lifts AOV and pre-sells through the days-15-45 churn window, directly answering the founder's "these are free tools, not revenue" concern.
3. **Conclusion to challenge:** that "own longitudinal tracking" is the top move: the most capital-intensive build, on the most contested ground, for a Vitall reseller with the weakest hand to win it.
4. **Cheapest de-risker:** the map is labelled "sized" but the flagship hubs (`low testosterone`, `testosterone test`, `ED blood test`) are unsized and `brain fog` (14,800) is missing entirely: one Google-Ads-volume backfill fixes the prioritisation.
5. **The real edge is the cheap one nobody's building:** a named GMC GP over an algorithm, no data sale, single UK controller: constraint-compatible, incumbent-resistant, and currently filed as a compliance chore instead of the brand's headline.
