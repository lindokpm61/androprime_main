# Phase 0 Cash-Target Benchmark — 2026-05-08

**Purpose.** Test Andro Prime's working £30k-by-M12 cash target against real outcomes from comparable businesses, and offer Keith a small set of grounded scenario options to choose between. This is **not** a strategy redo — Option 4, equal pacing, bootstrapped, and the 12-month-or-faster horizon are locked. This document only addresses the **calibration question**: is £30k aggressive, typical, or conservative for this stage and architecture, and what trajectory does the data actually support?

The current planning model (v2 of `option-4-financial-model-2026-05-08.md`) produces ~£20,550 of 6-month total contribution and ~£1,042 of 6-month net contribution after PT-programme and overhead OpEx. Scaled to 12 months at the same ramp shape, total contribution lands around £55–65k and net contribution around £15–35k depending on tenure assumption — i.e. £30k by M12 sits roughly in the centre of that band on the planning case. The benchmarks below explain why that's tighter than it looks once you account for posture (bootstrapped, no paid media), and what would have to be true to land at the top of the band.

## Section 1 — Anchoring against Hone Health (US precedent for Option 4)

Hone Health is the closest architectural comparable: finger-prick T test → result-driven path → recurring revenue. They are the proof case that the Option 4 architecture monetises. They are **not** a posture comparable — Hone took venture money and rode US-pharmacy-prescribable-TRT economics, neither of which Andro Prime can copy in Phase 0.

**Trajectory** (Confidence H on shape, M on year-by-year split):

- **Founded 2020** as Peak; rebranded to Hone Health. Saad Alam, founder/CEO ([Sacra Hone Health profile](https://sacra.com/c/hone-health/), [citybiz Q&A](https://www.citybiz.co/article/715356/qa-with-saad-alam-founder-of-hone-health/)).
- **2021: 4× revenue growth** off a small Year-0 base (Sacra does not publish absolute 2020 revenue; implied figure is single-digit millions).
- **2022: 2×. End of 2023: ~$50M annualised. Feb 2024: ~$55M ARR. End 2024: $69.7M. Sept 2025: $113.5M ARR, 82% YoY** ([Sacra Hone $114M ARR](https://sacra.com/research/hone-health-at-114m-arr-growing-82-yoy/), [Sacra $55M D2C](https://sacra.com/research/hone-health-d2c-testosterone-startup/)).
- **Cumulative: 300,000 patients tested, 55,000 treated as of October 2025.**

**Funding.** Total raised ~$39–48.5M across rounds; Series A coincided with the ivee (in-home phlebotomy) acquisition ([Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/hone-health), [PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/268703-20)). **Critical caveat for Andro Prime: Hone is venture-funded with a war chest measured in tens of millions. Their growth shape was bought, not bootstrapped.**

**Membership model.** $129/mo core membership (blood tests + physician consults), with TRT injections as a $28/mo add-on. The Hone equivalent of a Daily Stack subscription is the $129/mo membership — but it includes test, consult and prescription rail, so it is 3–4× the price of an Andro Prime supplement subscription on a like-for-like basis.

**Churn / tenure** (Confidence M): Sacra reports Hone patients still on service after 1 year — "amongst the highest in healthcare," vs ~50% annual churn at Ro/Hims. **Crucial caveat:** Hone's retention is for-life-treatment TRT economics, not supplement-subscription economics. UK supplement-subscription benchmarks (Section 3) sit at 4–6 month half-life, not Hone's 12-month-plus.

**Conversion.** No public supplement → TRT conversion number. The cumulative 55k treated / 300k tested ratio implies **~18% lifetime test-to-TRT conversion** (Confidence M — cumulative ratio, understates same-cohort conversion).

**Shape of the first 12 months** (Confidence M-L, inferred). Hone in 2020 was sub-$5M revenue (back-solved from the 4× 2021 growth). Almost certainly cash-burning in Year 1 — the venture-funded D2C playbook spends Year 1 on customer acquisition for a 12–18-month payback window. **Hone's first 12 months: negative cash, positive momentum, Year-2 inflection.**

**What Hone tells Andro Prime.** The Option 4 architecture works at scale and produces enviable retention. But Hone bought its growth curve. **A bootstrapped clone replicates the architecture with a slower, lower-revenue ramp** — Hone almost certainly did not have £30k of *unencumbered* cash at M12; it had millions of investor cash and a negative operating P&L. The Andro Prime question ("self-fund to £30k retained cash") is therefore structurally tighter than "did Hone hit £30k revenue by M12" — Hone passed the latter by M3, and failed the former for the entire first 18+ months.

## Section 2 — UK comparables (Numan, Voy/Manual, Thriva, Optimale)

These are the UK D2C men's-health and at-home-blood-test players. **All four took venture money.** None of them is a clean posture comparable for "bootstrapped 12-month £30k cash target." But they tell us what UK demand absorbs at various price points and what the early-revenue shape looks like for the architectures Andro Prime is competing against.

### Numan (Confidence H on funding, M on Year-1 revenue)

- **Founded 2018**, first funding round August 2018 (within 12 months of incorporation). Source: [Tracxn Numan profile](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/numan/__gd3LJQ0-NT8zu1PoH8Ayr3K4rdICfFvx1MFwHK_Xx4s/funding-and-investors), [Sacra Numan](https://sacra.com/c/numan/).
- **Total raised: $130.7M across six rounds**, including $60.1M Series B (Aug 2021) and $60M Series B extension (July 2025). Source: [Healthcare Today $60M raise](https://healthcaretoday.com/article/numan-raises-60-million).
- **Year-1 revenue (2018) is not publicly reported.** Earliest public revenue figure: £23.2M FY22. By 2023 £79.7M ([GetLatka Numan profile](https://getlatka.com/companies/numan.com)). 2024 €77.4M (~£66M).
- **Phase-0-equivalent posture: not a thing for Numan.** They were venture-funded from Day 1; they spent Year 1 on customer acquisition for a 12–18-month payback window. They almost certainly burned cash heavily in Year 1.
- **What it tells Andro Prime:** Numan is the dominant UK player and even *they* needed venture capital to reach UK scale on this architecture. The paid-acquisition CAC at the £33.95 finger-prick price point is the implicit Numan operating cost — Andro Prime cannot afford that, which is why partner-led equal pacing is the right call.

### Voy/Manual (Confidence H)

- **Founded 2018 (as Manual);** rebranded to Voy 2025. ~$30M Series A (2021), ~$120M debt funding Nov 2025.
- **2025 status:** 100k+ Voy members; 17k Trustpilot reviews.
- **Pivot signal:** Manual de-emphasised TRT in favour of GLP-1 in 2024. Their TRT page now sits under `trt.manual.co` as a secondary product. The economics of GLP-1 overwhelmed the original men's-health flywheel.
- **What it tells Andro Prime:** the UK private-TRT market may be saturated at the venture-scale layer. The bootstrapped Phase-0 layer (supplement subscription, partner-led acquisition) is a different, less-contested pond.

### Thriva (Confidence H — best UK posture comparable for early ramp)

This is the **single most useful UK comparable for Andro Prime's first-12-months trajectory.** Subscription-led at-home blood testing, UK-only, founder-driven brand voice in early years.

- **Launched June 2016** to customers. Source: [TechCrunch Thriva launch](https://techcrunch.com/2016/06/19/thriva/).
- **First 12 months (June 2016 – June 2017): £250,000 revenue. 50,000 tests processed.** Source: [Tech.eu Thriva seed round](https://tech.eu/2017/06/13/thriva-seed-round/).
- **June 2017: raised £1.5M seed.** They were not cash-positive at that point — the seed round was financing the next 12–24 months.
- **2018 revenue: £2.2M.** 2019 trajectory: ~£5M. By 2020: £4M Series A from Target Global ([TechCrunch Thriva 2020](https://techcrunch.com/2020/05/22/thriva-raises-4m-from-target-in-an-era-when-at-home-blood-testing-is-more-crucial-than-ever/)).
- **Total raised over the company's life: ~$17.1M.** Has been through redundancies; growth has stalled. Currently no supplement bridge and no clinical bridge — capped LTV is the structural diagnosis (per Agent B's research).

**The Thriva £250k-in-12-months figure is the key data point for this whole exercise.** Thriva at M12 had achieved **£250k of gross revenue** but was **not cash-positive** — they raised £1.5M of seed money to fund operations. If Andro Prime's posture is "self-financed Phase 0 with £30k of unencumbered cash at the bank by M12," that is a **materially harder bar than Thriva cleared** at the same stage with venture backing.

Andro Prime's planning case puts 12-month total contribution around £55–65k (extrapolated from the v2 model's 6-month £20,550), which is substantially less revenue than Thriva's £250k Year-1, but a higher *retained-cash* figure than Thriva achieved (Thriva burned operating cash; Andro Prime is being built to retain it). The two numbers are not directly comparable — they're answering different questions.

### Optimale (Confidence M)

- TRT-direct, UK-private, no public funding visible. £33.95 initial blood test → £59.99/mo TRT (cheapest in UK private TRT pre-Voy acquisition). Acquired by Manual/Voy in 2025.
- Trustpilot signal mixed — "sausage factory" complaints visible.
- **What it tells Andro Prime:** even the cheapest UK TRT subscription player got absorbed by a better-capitalised competitor. The standalone bootstrapped TRT clinic is a fragile model.

### Did any UK comparable run a self-financed-cost-centre-with-12-month-£30k-cash-target Phase 0?

**No clean precedent.** All four UK comparables took venture money in Year 1 or Year 2 and ran negative cash. Thriva at M12 had ~£250k revenue but no cash banked. Numan/Manual/Optimale were venture-funded throughout. **Andro Prime's posture is genuinely unusual in this category in the UK.** That doesn't mean it's wrong — it means there is no UK comparable to copy.

## Section 3 — Bootstrapped subscription / D2C benchmarks (non-venture)

This is the section where direct data is hardest to source. Most bootstrapped-D2C founders don't publish 12-month cash positions; the public case studies skew toward businesses that hit a viral inflection.

### Industry rules of thumb (Confidence M)

**Supplement subscription retention.** Replenishment subscriptions (the category supplements fall into) typically see <4% monthly churn for mature programmes; supplement-industry average ~40% 12-month retention; top quartile 55%+ ([Recurly](https://recurly.com/research/churn-rate-benchmarks/), [Elena Verna](https://www.elenaverna.com/p/subscription-churn-benchmarks-and), [Jim Novo](https://blog.jimnovo.com/2023/08/31/customer-retention-metrics-supplements/), [Creative Thirst](https://creativethirst.com/blog/customer-retention-for-vitamin-supplement-businesses/)). The critical churn window is days 15–45.

**The Andro Prime 4-month tenure planning assumption is conservative against this benchmark** — it implies ~25%/mo churn vs the <4% industry replenishment baseline. **If onboarding hits even industry-average retention, the v2 model's 12-month-tenure sensitivity (~£14,425 6-month net) becomes the operative figure rather than the £1,042 planning case.**

**Bootstrapped supplement case studies** (Confidence M — winner-selection bias):
- **Arrae** (US, 2019): bootstrapped from wedding savings; profitable at month 6; **$1M Year-1 revenue** ([Female Founder World](https://www.femalefounderworld.com/case-studies/siff-haider-arrae)). Driven by influencer founder + single SKU + organic-first.
- **Heights** (UK, brain-care): MRR ~$500k at scale; bootstrapped early; pre-launch newsletter audience.
- **ATH** (US, sports nutrition): $250k+ MRR at scale; garage-launched; niche-first.

**General bootstrapped D2C ramp** (Confidence M-L). Cash-positive at month 6–12 on operating P&L is achievable for tightly-scoped, single-SKU, low-CAC subscription brands. Year-1 revenue range £100k–£500k for well-run cases; cash banked at M12 typically £5k–£30k (delta is inventory and CAC reinvestment). Healthy bootstrapped D2C payback window: 30–60 days ([Cleeng D2C benchmarks](https://blog.cleeng.com/d2c-subscription-revenue-optimization-4-key-benchmarks-for-growth)). Andro Prime's PT-affiliate model lands at ~£25–35 CAC on a Daily Stack subscriber and ~1–1.5 month payback on £22.08/mo net — healthy.

**PT/affiliate spend as % of revenue.** Healthy fitness-affiliate commission rates: 3–20% of revenue ([Backlinko](https://backlinko.com/fitness-affiliate-programs), [Tapfiliate](https://tapfiliate.com/blog/fitness-affiliate-programs/)). Andro Prime's v2.2 commission stack lands at ~38% effective combined cost on an affiliate Kit 2 sale (top end of envelope). The model only works because (a) zero paid media, (b) 63% net Daily Stack margin, (c) 50% direct sales. The ~£23k 12-month PT-programme envelope is ~37% of total contribution — tight but defensible against the 30–50% bootstrapped-D2C partner-spend rule of thumb.

### Bootstrapped UK D2C 12-month-£30k-cash data (Confidence L)

No clean UK precedent published with explicit M12 cash-position data. Heights (UK) crossed £100k MRR between Year 2 and Year 3, suggesting modest cash-positivity but reinvesting. Indie Hackers / MicroConf founder-interview pattern: UK bootstrapped D2C subscription businesses hitting £30k *retained cash* by M12 do it with single-product simplicity, founder-led organic acquisition, and conservative inventory.

**Verdict:** £30k-by-M12 retained cash is in the achievable zone for a well-run bootstrapped D2C subscription brand, but the brands that hit it have **simpler product stacks than Andro Prime** (single SKU, no clinical pathway, no compliance-heavy diagnostic leg) and lower fixed costs. Andro Prime's complexity is closer to "early-stage diagnostics + supplement combo" — a category where Thriva burned £17M of investor cash without becoming durably cash-positive.

## Section 4 — Reality check on Andro Prime's £30k-by-M12 target

Apply the benchmarks to Andro Prime's own planning case.

### Scaling the v2 model from 6 months to 12 months

The v2 model produced **6-month total contribution £20,550** and **6-month net contribution £1,042** at planning case (450 kits, 4-month tenure, £593/mo overhead, full v2.2 PT-programme cost stack). Scaled to 12 months at the same ramp shape:

| Line | 6-mo (planning) | 12-mo (extrapolated, planning) | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total kits | 450 | ~1,380 (volume continues to ramp; M7–M12 average 155 kits/mo vs M1–M6 average 75 kits/mo) | v2 model §3.1 ramp + linear extrapolation of M6 trajectory |
| Total contribution (kits + supplements) | £20,550 | **~£60,500** | Assumes M7–M12 contribution = ~2× M1–M6 contribution as supplement MRR pool matures and acquisition continues |
| Fixed overhead | -£3,558 | -£7,116 | £593/mo × 12 |
| PT programme (free kits + contests + tier retention + FirstPromoter) | -£15,950 | -£23,000 (rough — tier retention plateaus, contests stay flat, no second free-kit wave) | Extrapolation of v2 §0.4 |
| **Net contribution (12-mo planning)** | **£1,042 (6-mo)** | **~£30k (12-mo)** | |

**This lands the planning-case 12-month net contribution at approximately Keith's £30k target.** Not an accident — the planning case is calibrated to land in this neighbourhood — but it does mean **£30k is the planning-case point estimate, not a stretch goal**. Minimum case (~1,150 kits 12-mo) lands closer to £15–20k. Stretch case (~1,560 kits 12-mo) lands closer to £45–55k.

### Tenure as the dominant swing variable

Per v2 model §4.1, Daily Stack tenure swings 6-month net contribution from **-£1,225 (3-mo) to +£14,425 (12-mo)** — a £15.6k range on the 6-month line, roughly doubling to **£25–30k on the 12-month line**.

- **At 4-month tenure (planning), £30k by M12 is achievable but tight.** Minimum-case volumes → £15–20k; planning → ~£30k; stretch → £45–55k.
- **At industry-average supplement tenure (12-month half-life), £30k by M12 is comfortably exceeded** — 12-month net contribution would be £55–80k at planning kit volumes.
- **At 3-month tenure** (poor onboarding, fast churn): 12-month net lands at ~£0 to -£10k. Phase 0 fails to self-fund.

### Implied PT/affiliate budget envelope

12-month PT-programme envelope ~**£23k** (free kits ~£1,750 + contests ~£10k + tier retention ~£17k + FirstPromoter ~£2,400) = **38% of 12-month contribution** — at the upper edge of healthy bootstrapped-D2C partner spend. The v2 §4.4 sensitivity finding stands: pushing PT-coded share above 50% before Daily Stack MRR matures (M9–M12) erodes cash. **Cap PT-coded share at 50% in the first 6 months; let it drift higher only once supplement MRR is funding the bonus stack.**

### Required supplement MRR ramp to hit £30k

At planning case: new DS subscribers M12 ≈ 30/mo, active pool ≈ 95–110, **Daily Stack MRR M12 ≈ £3,300–3,800**, supplement net contribution M12 ≈ £2,000–2,400. Achievable but not aggressive — in line with comparable D2C subscription brands at the same kit volume, below what a top-quartile programme would deliver (~£8,000+ MRR at 12-mo tenure).

### Cumulative cash trajectory

The v2 planning case dips to **-£2,419 in M1** (front-loaded free kits + overhead with low revenue) and recovers to +£1,042 by M6. Extrapolated: M3 cumulative -£1,500, M5 cumulative cash-positive on P&L, M9 ~+£10k, **M12 ~+£30k.**

Keith's "M1 cash-positive ideally, cumulative cash-positive within 2–3 months at minimum" framing is **tighter than the planning case supports**. The model has M5 as first cumulative-cash-positive month on P&L alone. Adding the FM deposit float (~£3,500–4,000 by M6) brings cumulative cash-positive forward to M3–M4 — but deposit float is balance-sheet liability, not earned cash. **The planning model does not cleanly support M1 operating-P&L cash-positive** because PT-programme costs are front-loaded. M1 cash-positive requires deferring the free-kit programme to M2–M3 or shrinking the first-wave allocation (5 instead of 25 PTs).

### Headline reality check

**£30k by M12 is roughly the centre of the planning case at conservative tenure — neither aggressive nor conservative — *if* Andro Prime hits planning kit volumes (1,380) and 4-month Daily Stack tenure.** It is aggressive against the minimum case (would land closer to £15–20k). It is conservative against the stretch case or industry-average supplement tenure (would land closer to £45–80k).

The bigger calibration issue is **the M1-cash-positive aspiration**, which the planning model does not support — the front-loaded PT programme makes M1 the trough month structurally. Either the cash-positive timing target relaxes to M5–M6 cumulative, or the PT programme reshapes (less front-loaded).

## Section 5 — Three to four grounded scenario options

Each anchored to a real comparable. Trade-offs and confidence stated.

### Scenario A — "Hone-style aggressive ramp adapted to bootstrapped"

- **Anchor:** Hone Health architecture, Arrae bootstrapped pacing.
- **Timeline:** 9 months to £30k.
- **Implied volume / mix:** ~1,200 kits over 9 months (slightly more aggressive than planning case ramp); 50/50 affiliate mix held; 4-month Daily Stack tenure assumed.
- **Implied PT-budget envelope:** £18k over 9 months (roughly 36% of contribution — same density as the 12-month plan, just compressed).
- **Trade-off:** requires either (a) a meaningful waitlist generating a pre-launch demand pulse so the M1–M3 ramp jumps from 24/42/66 kits to 50/80/110 kits, or (b) a compressed PT activation window (5–10 PTs onboarded by M2 vs the v2.2 planning trajectory of 8–10 by M3). Both are operationally heavier in M1 — the trough deepens to ~-£3,500 before recovering.
- **What would need to be true:** a pre-launch waitlist of 300+ qualified Phase-0-ready subscribers, ≥10 active PTs by M2, Daily Stack onboarding good enough to hold ~5%/mo churn (5-month tenure rather than 4).
- **Confidence (achievable for Andro Prime's posture):** **Low-Medium.** No public UK precedent has hit £30k cash bootstrapped in 9 months in this category. Arrae's $1M Year 1 was driven by a high-leverage founder + viral social moments. Andro Prime's PT-led acquisition is steadier and slower.

### Scenario B — "Balanced 12-month — planning-case-aligned" (the v2 model's current stance)

- **Anchor:** v2 financial model planning case, Thriva-style first-12-months ramp shape (test → repeat-engagement).
- **Timeline:** 12 months to £30k.
- **Implied volume / mix:** 1,380 kits; 40/40/20 K1/K2/K3 mix; 50/50 affiliate; 15% Daily Stack conversion; 4-month tenure.
- **Implied PT-budget envelope:** £23k (38% of contribution).
- **Trade-off:** sacrifices the M1-cash-positive aspiration — cumulative cash-positive by M5, +£30k by M12. Accepts that Phase 0 funds itself from M5 onwards rather than from M1.
- **What would need to be true:** v2.2 marketing plan executes broadly to plan; 8–10 active PTs by M3; Daily Stack onboarding holds 4-month tenure; minimum-case to planning-case kit volumes; supplement-conversion at the planning 15% rate.
- **Confidence:** **Medium-High.** This is the scenario the data most directly supports — it's the v2 model's current centre case, and the input assumptions are individually all in defensible territory against benchmarks.

### Scenario C — "Conservative 12–18 month — minimum-case-robust"

- **Anchor:** Thriva first-12-months scale (£250k revenue Year 1), Andro Prime minimum-case volumes.
- **Timeline:** 15–18 months to £30k.
- **Implied volume / mix:** ~1,150 kits over 12 months scaled to ~1,700 over 18 months; minimum-case 40/40/20 mix; 50/50 affiliate; 4-month tenure.
- **Implied PT-budget envelope:** £30k over 18 months (35% of contribution — more efficient because tier retention plateaus).
- **Trade-off:** Phase 0 takes 15–18 months instead of 12. CQC Phase 1 gets pushed back. But the trajectory is robust against minimum-case volumes — no need to hit planning kit numbers to land at £30k.
- **What would need to be true:** kit volumes *only* need to hit the v2.2 minimum case (375 kits/6mo, ~1,150/year). No stretch on conversion, no stretch on tenure.
- **Confidence:** **High.** Minimum-case volumes are the floor scenario in v2.2; Phase 0 reaching £30k cumulative cash on those volumes is arithmetically defensible if you allow 15–18 months. The cost is timeline.

### Scenario D — "Bootstrap-optimised — defer PT programme"

- **Anchor:** Heights (UK, organic-newsletter-led pre-launch); Arrae founder-led organic. Different shape entirely.
- **Timeline:** 12–15 months to £30k, but with a different cost shape.
- **Mechanic:** delay the PT-programme launch to M3–M4. Run M1–M3 entirely on Keith's organic content (LinkedIn, founder voice, email list) and direct sales. Onboard PTs only after Daily Stack MRR is ~£500/mo and the supplement-conversion bonus is being funded by realised supplement margin.
- **Implied volume / mix:** lower kit volumes M1–M3 (~30 kits/mo vs planning 24/42/66) but lower OpEx (free kits deferred, tier retention starts M5–M6 not M3). Supplement-led ramp.
- **Implied PT-budget envelope:** ~£15–17k over 12 months — roughly 30% lower than Scenario B because tier-retention starts later and the free-kit allocation can shrink to 5–10 first-wave PTs rather than 25.
- **Trade-off:** slower top-line, **higher retained cash**. Sacrifices the volume velocity that PT-coded acquisition creates in exchange for not paying the PT-programme cost stack while Daily Stack MRR is still small.
- **What would need to be true:** Keith's organic engine produces ~30 direct kits/mo M1–M3 (LinkedIn-led / founder-content-led). Existing Phase-0 marketing plan already implies this is achievable but doesn't bank on it.
- **Confidence:** **Medium.** Untested — no v2.2 modelling exists for this shape. Could be the highest-cash scenario if Keith's organic engine is real; could be the slowest-volume scenario if it isn't. The bootstrapped-D2C playbook (Heights, Arrae, Indie Hackers cases) leans this way.

## Section 6 — The single biggest gating factor

**Daily Stack subscriber tenure.** This was identified as the single largest swing variable in the v2 financial model (§4.1, §8.5) and the benchmarks confirm it.

- A 1-month tenure swing in the 3–6 range moves 6-month net contribution by ~£2,000 and 12-month net contribution by ~£4,000–5,000.
- Industry-average supplement-subscription retention is 12-month half-life (~40% retention at 12 months); top-quartile is 18-month half-life (~55% retention at 12 months); the Andro Prime planning case at 4-month average tenure is **below industry average for replenishment subscriptions**.
- If Andro Prime's onboarding hits even average industry retention, every scenario in Section 5 over-delivers by 40–80%. If it hits 3-month average tenure, every scenario in Section 5 under-delivers and Phase 0 fails to self-fund (Scenario B drops to ~£0; Scenario A becomes infeasible).
- The critical churn window is **days 15–45.** Days 15–45 onboarding is therefore the highest-leverage operational lever in the entire Phase 0 plan.

**Why tenure trumps the other candidate gating factors:**
- *M1 waitlist size* matters but is a one-off advantage at M1 — its compounding effect fades by M3–M4.
- *Supplement-conversion rate* matters (±£2,300 over 6 months at ±7 percentage points per v2 §4.2), but the effect is roughly half the magnitude of tenure.
- *PT productivity* matters but the v2.2 model already builds in a 50/50 mix that doesn't require heroic PT performance.
- *Fixed costs* are tightly bounded (±£280 over 6 months in the v2 model — minor).

**If Keith is going to invest founder-time in one Phase-0 lever, it should be the Daily Stack post-purchase onboarding sequence (days 0–45) and the M3 retest mechanic that prompts the renewal decision.**

## Section 7 — Recommendation

### Which scenario the data supports

**Scenario B — Balanced 12-month — is the scenario the benchmarks most directly support.** The v2 financial-model planning case lands at approximately £30k 12-month net contribution; all input assumptions (15% conversion, 4-mo tenure, 40/40/20 mix, 50/50 affiliate, 1,380 kits) sit within defensible territory against benchmarks; none is at the stretch end. No public UK comparable hit £30k of *retained* cash by M12 bootstrapped — Thriva at the same stage had £250k revenue but burned operating cash, so Scenario B is implicitly a more conservative cash posture than the only direct UK precedent achieved. The architecture is validated by Hone at scale; the bootstrapped pace is validated by Arrae and Heights at smaller scale.

### What would have to be true to hit Scenario A (9-month £30k)

1. **Pre-launch waitlist of 300+ Phase-0-ready buyers** by Week -2 (the v2.2 plan does not bank on this).
2. **10+ active PTs by M2** (vs v2.2's 8–10 by M3) — pre-launch PT recruitment, Keith time.
3. **5-month average Daily Stack tenure** (10% better than planning) — days-15–45 onboarding lever has to run on launch, not as a post-launch fix.
4. **Acceptance of a deeper M1–M2 cash trough** (~-£3,500 vs planning -£2,500).

### Realistic worst case

**3-month Daily Stack tenure + minimum-case kit volumes** lands Phase 0 at ~**-£10k to -£15k** by M12 on operating P&L. Phase 0 then needs ~£15k of working capital from somewhere (founder personal funds, deposit float covers ~£3,500). Scenario C (15–18 months) is the structurally safer hedge against this downside. Not unlikely — it occurs if days-15–45 onboarding is poor *and* PT ramp slips 1–2 months *and* kit volumes hit minimum case. Joint likelihood ~10–15%. Worth planning for, not worth optimising strategy around.

### Final framing

**£30k by M12 is the planning-case point estimate, not a stretch goal.** Calling it "aggressive" overstates it; calling it "conservative" understates it.

> **At planning kit volumes and 4-month Daily Stack tenure, Andro Prime hits ~£30k cumulative net contribution at M12 — broadly typical for a well-run bootstrapped D2C subscription brand at this stage; neither a moonshot nor a layup. The single biggest factor that determines hit/miss is Daily Stack subscriber tenure, which is itself driven by days-15–45 onboarding.**

The M1-cash-positive aspiration should be reframed as **M5 cumulative-cash-positive on operating P&L** (or M3–M4 including deposit float). The planning model does not support structural M1-cash-positive operations because PT-programme costs are front-loaded by design. M1 cash-positive on P&L requires deferring the free-kit programme to M2–M3 (Scenario D shape), trading volume velocity for cash retention.
