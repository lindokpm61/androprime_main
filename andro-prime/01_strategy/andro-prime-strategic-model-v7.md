V7 is the planning document. V6.1 was the right starting point — single entity, AI-first ops, Phase 0 pre-CQC revenue strategy, three-case exit framework — but it was written in March 2026 and the strategy has evolved in three material ways since:

V7 reflects these changes and recalibrates V6.1's patient counts to defensible ranges. V6.1's exit ambition (£50M+ family outcome at Optimised Base) is preserved as the Upside scenario, not the planning case. The planning case is £30-45M.

V6.1's structural decisions — single entity, AI-first operations, Phase 0 to Phase 1 progression, validation gate discipline, single-line ARPU expansion through add-ons — are largely preserved. V7 extends rather than replaces them.

## 3. The Longitudinal Tracker Thesis (New in V7)

### 3.1 Why this is a new strategic foundation

V6.1 did not contemplate a customer-facing longitudinal data layer. The 2026-05-12 longitudinal tracker decision document established this as a structural retention mechanism, a brand differentiator, and a clinical funnel that no UK competitor currently owns. V7 elevates this from "feature" to "foundation."

### 3.2 What the tracker is

A unified customer-facing dashboard that surfaces the customer's own purchased lab data, supplement subscription history, and (post-CQC) clinical interactions back to them as a longitudinal record. Built in two phases:

- **v1 (passive tracker)** — ships M3-M4 post-launch. Aggregates blood results, reference ranges, supplement timeline. Shows trends as observations. No interpretation, no recommendations. Customer-facing framing: *"your health record, tracked properly."*
- **v2 (intelligence layer)** — ships M13-M14 if gate passes. Adds correlation analysis, marker-driven retest prompts. Gated on engagement criteria (50% dashboard log-in rate, 100+ customers with ≥2 results, 6 months elapsed). If engagement is below 50%, v2 does not get built.

### 3.3 Why this changes the platform thesis

Three reasons.

**Retention.** Affiliate and PT-driven acquisition produces customers whose attachment is borrowed from the referrer. The tracker is the simplest mechanism for converting passive subscription into active relationship. Customer logs in to see their data, not to receive marketing. Tenure is the single largest LTV swing variable in every model built since March 2026 — every additional month of tenure compounds disproportionately.

**Whitespace.** No UK at-home men's health brand currently owns the data-visualisation layer for both wellness and clinical customers. The moat is not "we built a dashboard first" — competitors can build dashboards in 8-12 weeks. The moat is owning the data layer that makes the dashboard valuable, and accruing that data from launch while competitors start from zero.

**Brand positioning.** Traditional UK private clinical practice holds the longitudinal record clinician-side. Patients see piecemeal results at appointments. Andro Prime inverts this: patient view and clinician view are roughly one-to-one. The brand becomes *"we treat you like an adult who can see your own data"* — a positioning claim that operates against the entire conventional private-clinic model, not just against direct competitors.

### 3.4 Compliance posture

The v1 tracker is observation-only. It does not interpret data, does not generate recommendations, does not assert causation. The customer sees their data alongside reference ranges; the tracker does not say "you are deficient" or "your supplement is working." This compliance posture is non-negotiable and is detailed in the 2026-05-12 decision document and the `/03_compliance/CONTEXT.md` rules.

Ewa (clinical advisor) signs off all dashboard copy, trend indicator language, and retest prompt copy before release. The retainer for this work is a Y1 OpEx line item (~£1,000/month, scaling Y2-Y3).

### 3.5 What the tracker is NOT

The tracker is not an "AI health insights" product. It is not a diagnostic aid. It is not a clinical decision-support tool. It is not customer-facing AI labelling. The framing decision is deliberate and is not subject to A/B testing — changing it requires a strategic review, not a marketing decision.

## 5. Multi-Vertical Clinical Service Strategy

### 5.1 Verticals committed in V7

| Vertical | Launch | Rationale | Initial scale assumption |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **TRT** | M13 (post-CQC) | Core thesis, highest ARPU, deepest market knowledge | Lead vertical |
| **Hair loss** | M15-M18 (Y2) | Lowest clinical complexity, large existing market, low CAC via existing customers | Volume play |
| **GLP-1 / weight management** | M18-M24 (Y2) | Fastest-growing UK men's-health segment; higher ARPU than TRT in some configurations | Scale opportunity |

### 5.2 Verticals flagged as options (Y3-Y4)

| Vertical | Earliest launch | Rationale | Status |
|---------|----------------|-----------|--------|
| Sexual health (standalone, beyond TRT add-on) | M25-M30 (Y3) | Tadalafil/sildenafil already in TRT add-on stack; standalone product extends reach | Decision deferred to Gate 2 |
| Cardiometabolic (statins, BP management) | M30-M36 (Y3) | Builds on bloods data; competitive whitespace | Decision deferred to Gate 3 |

### 5.3 Why multi-vertical, not TRT-led-with-add-ons

V6.1 modelled tadalafil, anastrozole, and supplements as add-ons to TRT (incremental £18-25 ARPU per TRT patient). V7 keeps this attach revenue but additionally treats hair loss and GLP-1 as standalone clinical services that can be acquired independently.

Three reasons this matters:

**Customer reach.** A customer who needs hair loss treatment but not TRT cannot be reached by a TRT-led platform. Multi-vertical expansion broadens the addressable market.

**Risk diversification.** TRT-only platforms have all eggs in one regulatory and competitive basket. NHS policy shifts on testosterone, MHRA actions, or major TRT competitor moves can dent a TRT-only business meaningfully. Multi-vertical platforms absorb shocks better.

**Exit positioning.** Strategic acquirers (Hims, Numan, a larger health group) are *expected* to value platforms with revenue diversity higher than single-vertical clinics. The platform multiple is hypothesised to be materially higher (estimated 8-10x EBITDA vs 5-7x for single-vertical), though UK men's-health platform exit multiples are not publicly disclosed at sufficient granularity to confirm this with high confidence. The Hims acquisition of Manual (UK) in 2025 was at an undisclosed multiple; Numan's valuation history is private. V7 treats the platform-multiple premium as an expected but unvalidated thesis, not as a fact.

### 5.4 What multi-vertical does NOT mean in V7

V7 does NOT commit to:
- Launching a vertical before its commercial logic is proven (each vertical has its own validation gate)
- Building bespoke clinical workflows for each vertical from scratch (shared infrastructure, differentiated only where regulatory or clinical reality demands)
- Expanding faster than CQC governance allows (each new regulated activity requires CQC notification or amendment)
- Bidding for share with paid acquisition before unit economics are proven per vertical

The discipline is the same as V6.1: validate then scale, not scale then hope.

### 5.5 Vertical launch gates

Each clinical vertical has a Y1 commercial-proof gate equivalent to V6.1's Gate 1:

- **Hair loss launch gate (M15-M18):** 50+ active hair loss patients within 3 months of launch, CAC <£60, retention >85% at 60 days. Below threshold: pause expansion, investigate.
- **GLP-1 launch gate (M18-M24):** 80+ active GLP-1 patients within 4 months, CAC <£120, retention >75% at 90 days. Below threshold: pause expansion.

Each gate is documented separately when the vertical reaches launch-ready status.

## 6. Cohort Structure (the core V7 model)

V7 abandons V6.1's single-line patient build in favour of a cohort-based structure where wellness, TRT, hair loss, and GLP-1 are modelled as separate cohorts with their own ARPU, churn, COGS, and acquisition economics. The financial model (built next) will instantiate this.

### 6.1 The five cohorts

| Cohort | Description | Y7 ARPU range | Y7 margin |
|--------|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Wellness (standalone)** | Customers on kits + supplements + tracker; no clinical service | £25-40/mo blended | 50-55% |
| **TRT** | Active TRT patients (with add-on attach) | £215-235/mo | 42-45% |
| **Hair loss** | Standalone hair loss customers (finasteride/minoxidil, topical, oral) | £25-45/mo | 55-60% |
| **GLP-1** | Active weight-management patients | £150-200/mo | 35-40% |
| **Multi-service** | Customers active in 2+ clinical cohorts | Sum of components | Blended |

### 6.2 Cohort flow and conversion

```
Phase 0 acquisition → Wellness cohort (default)
                          ↓
        Tracker engagement / flagged result / opt-in
                          ↓
            ┌─────────────┴─────────────┐
            ↓             ↓             ↓
           TRT       Hair loss        GLP-1
        (post-CQC) (post-CQC Y2)  (post-CQC Y2)
                          ↓
                  Multi-service cohort
                          ↓
                 Cross-attach revenue
```

Implication: every clinical patient passes through wellness first. The wellness cohort is not a separate funnel — it is the entry point for everyone. Tracker engagement and longitudinal data accrual happens to all customers, regardless of which clinical vertical (if any) they eventually enter.

- M15-M18: Hair loss launches as second vertical. Lower clinical complexity, faster ramp.

- Year 2 active cohort end: ~2,500-3,500 wellness + 400-600 TRT + 100-300 hair + 50-150 GLP-1.

### 7.2a Year 2 Working Capital — load-bearing assumption

V7 acknowledges a Year 2 working capital requirement that V6.1 did not surface. The cycle of clinical inventory, paid acquisition, and Stripe settlement creates a peak funding gap that Phase 0 cash alone does not cover.

**Year 2 working capital sources of pressure:**

- **TRT medication float.** ~£59/patient/month COGS. Pharmacy invoices Andro Prime before patient payment cycles complete. At 500 active TRT patients by end of Y2, this represents ~£30-50k of inventory float at any given time.
- **GLP-1 inventory float.** Semaglutide/tirzepatide wholesale ~£100-150 per month-equivalent dose. 100 active GLP-1 patients = £10-15k/month of inventory cycling through; storage and refrigeration adds further cost.
- **Blood-test reagent and kit COGS.** Sit between order placement and customer payment receipt.
- **Paid acquisition spend.** Y2 paid media (per V6.1 §11) builds; CAC is paid up-front while customer LTV materialises over months.
- **Clinical infrastructure ramp.** Software (Semble, prescribing platforms), indemnity, pharmacy contracts, GP onboarding costs.

**Estimated peak Y2 working capital need: £50-80k.**

**Phase 0 cash available at M12 per v3 model: ~£39k cumulative.** This does not cover Y2 working capital, Y2 OpEx ramp, and clinical inventory cycles simultaneously.

**Bridge funding gap: £30-60k.** This must be resolved before TRT launch. Options:

1. **Founder capital injection** (Keith, ~£40-60k bridge loan to the business, repaid from Y2-Y3 cashflow). Simplest. No external dependencies.
2. **Revenue-based finance** against TRT MRR once it materialises (M16-M18 earliest, since lenders need 3-6 months of subscription history). Available but expensive (typically 6-10% effective rate plus fees).
3. **Supplier credit terms** with pharmacy partners (NET 30/60 instead of NET 14). Achievable but requires negotiation with partners who may push back from a new entity.
4. **Delay second vertical launch.** If GLP-1 launch is pushed to M22-M28 (rather than M18-M24), TRT cashflow has more time to build before working capital pressure peaks. This trades exit-timeline aggression for working capital safety.
5. **Combination** (most likely actual outcome): some founder bridge + supplier terms + slightly delayed GLP-1.

**V7's planning recommendation: Option 5.** Assume Keith brings £30-40k working capital injection at M11-M13 (just before TRT launch), negotiate NET 30 terms with at least one pharmacy partner, and treat GLP-1 launch as M20-M24 not M18 to give cash one extra month per delay. This keeps the timeline ambitious without creating a cash-flow cliff that could force unfavourable decisions in Y2.

The financial model (built next) will include this explicit working-capital line and flag the peak month for cash position.

#### Gate Exit-Y3 (M36)

**Trigger metric:** Total active across cohorts ≥5,500. EBITDA run-rate ≥£1.5M annualised.

**On trajectory (≥5,500 active, EBITDA on track):**
- Continue as planned. V7 §10 Optimised Base remains the planning case.
- Commit to GLP-1 expansion at full pace (more clinicians, paid acquisition lift).
- Begin documenting playbook for sexual health vertical launch decision (Gate 2 at M48).

**Below trajectory but recoverable (3,500-5,499 active, EBITDA £800k-£1.5M annualised):**
- Identify the failing cohort. Most likely candidate based on launch risk: GLP-1 (GPhC compliance complexity) or hair loss (CAC erosion).
- **Decision branch:** (a) double down on the strongest cohort by reallocating paid media; (b) pause expansion in the failing cohort and consolidate; (c) renegotiate supplier terms to extend working-capital runway.
- Recalibrate V7 planning case to Base (§19-27M family outcome post-CGT).
- 6-month performance review at M42 to assess whether trajectory is recoverable.

**Materially failing (≤3,500 active, EBITDA £400-800k annualised):**
- The multi-vertical platform thesis has not landed in 3 years. Three explicit options:
 - **(a) Concentrate on TRT-only** — shut down or wind down failing verticals, redeploy resources behind TRT plus longitudinal data, accept that exit is now single-vertical (5-7x multiple, smaller exit).
 - **(b) Acquisition by competitor** — initiate conversations with Numan, Voy, or strategic acquirer. Exit at lower multiple (likely £8-15M EV) but recoups Keith's time and capital.
 - **(c) External capital raise** — if validation is right but pace is wrong, raise £500k-£1.5M to accelerate. This contradicts the bootstrapped thesis but may be the rational choice if specific levers are identified.
- V7 is materially rewritten to V8 reflecting reality.

#### Gate Exit-Y5 (M60)

**Trigger metric:** Annual revenue ≥£8M, EBITDA margin ≥40%, founder dependency ≤4 hours/week clinically (Keith) and ≤2 days/week (Ewa).

**On trajectory:**
- Begin Y6 exit-readiness prep: clinical team independence, process documentation, financial cleanup, IP audit (longitudinal data asset specifically).
- Schedule strategic-buyer conversations for M66-M72 with explicit ranges (preferred acquirers, valuation expectations).
- Lock V7 Optimised Base as the live target.

**Below trajectory (revenue £5-7.9M, margin 35-39%):**
- Exit window pushed to Y8-Y9. The business is profitable but not at exit-ready scale.
- **Decision branch:** (a) continue building toward Y8 exit at higher valuation; (b) accept earlier exit at lower multiple now; (c) explore management buyout or partial sale (sell 30-50% to PE, retain operating role).
- Keith income optimisation becomes more important: longer hold means more dividend income over time, less capital gain at exit.

**Materially below (revenue <£5M or margin <35% or founder dependency >6 hrs/wk):**
- The business is a lifestyle business, not an exit business. This is not a failure — but it's a different outcome than V7 planned.
- **Decision branch:** (a) embrace the lifestyle-business outcome (high personal income, no exit); (b) shut down and exit personally (return to consulting at keith-antony.com or alternative); (c) sell to a smaller buyer (5-6x EBITDA at £2-4M EV).
- V7 is retired. New strategic document reflecting lifestyle-business reality.

#### Gate Exit-Ready (M72)

**Trigger metric:** Strategic buyer conversations initiated, due diligence preparation begins, founder discount reducible to 15% or less.

**On trajectory (clear buyer interest, multiple bidders or strong single buyer):**
- Execute exit Y7 per plan. Engage M&A advisor (cost: 1-3% of EV typically). Target close M84.
- Hold cash position robust enough to support 6-12 months of deal conversations without operational compromise.

**Single buyer, weaker terms:**
- **Decision branch:** (a) accept terms and exit; (b) extend hold to Y8 hoping for stronger market or second bidder; (c) renegotiate structure (earn-out, partial cash, equity rollover).
- Earn-out structures common in this space; Keith should expect 60-80% cash on close + 20-40% earn-out over 1-2 years tied to retention.

**No serious buyer interest:**
- The business is profitable but not strategic. Most likely cause: founder dependency too high, or longitudinal data asset insufficiently differentiated, or revenue diversification still concentrated in TRT.
- **Decision branch:** (a) extend hold to Y8-Y9 building toward stronger position; (b) accept that no high-multiple exit exists, pivot to dividend-extraction strategy; (c) operational sale to roll-up at lower multiple.

If any exit-track gate is materially missed, V7 is updated against evidence. The gates are not pass/fail — they are decision points that route to explicit next actions.

## 11. Founder Outcomes (V7) — Honest Reconstruction

V6.1 §13 produced family-outcome totals that double-counted operating income and exit proceeds without properly modelling cash retention. V7 §11 corrects this. The headline number anyone should care about is the **exit-only family outcome** (§10). The lifetime total is interesting but methodologically harder to defend precisely.

### 11.1 Why this is a rebuild, not an inheritance

Three corrections apply to V6.1's framing:

1. **Not all EBITDA becomes distributable cash.** EBITDA → corporation tax (25%) → retained earnings reinvested in growth (probably 30-50% of post-CT profit in Y1-Y5) → dividends taken. So distributable to founders is materially lower than EBITDA × (1 – CT) × (1 – dividend tax).
2. **Reinvestment is the price of growth.** The trajectory in §7 requires capital re-injection across Y2-Y5: clinical launches, working-capital float (§7.2a), paid acquisition expansion, hires. The cash that funds growth is not the cash that goes to founders.
3. **Ewa's operating income and buyout are not both at full value.** If Ewa is bought out before exit (V6.1 framing), she stops drawing dividends from the operating business at the point of buyout. The two lines compete; V6.1 treated them as additive.

### 11.2 Realistic operating-income window

## 14. The Honest Read

V7 is a more conservative document than V6.1 was. V6.1 promised £50-79M family outcome at Optimised Base. V7 §11.4 lands the lifetime family outcome at **£28-37M** at planning case. The reason is not pessimism — it is recalibration against UK comparable data and against realistic cash-distribution mechanics. Bootstrapped UK men's-health platforms in Numan/Optimale/Leger range have not hit V6.1's Y7 patient counts within 7 years. And operating income cannot be drawn at full margin while the business is also growing — reinvestment competes with distribution. V6.1 conflated these. V7 separates them.

V7 is also a more ambitious document in one specific way: it commits to multi-vertical platform thesis as the planning case, not as upside. V6.1 treated multi-vertical as undefined future expansion. V7 names the verticals (TRT + hair + GLP-1), sequences their launches, gates them with explicit decision branches, and acknowledges the working capital cost of that ambition. This is harder to execute than TRT-with-add-ons. But it is also the path that justifies the platform multiple at exit (if the platform-multiple thesis itself proves true — §5.3 flags this as expected but unvalidated).

The £50M+ stretch ambition is preserved. It is preserved as stretch, not as plan. The Keith Position C answer (£30-45M family outcome as planning target) is reconciled in §11.6: V7's planning case brushes the lower edge of Position C's range with no headroom. Getting into the middle of Position C requires Upside execution across multiple structural conditions — not impossible, but not the default trajectory either.

V7 is also a more honest document than V7.0-initial was. The first draft buried working capital in §12 as a footnote and produced family-outcome totals that didn't survive arithmetic scrutiny. The current V7 surfaces the working-capital bridge gap as a planning input (§7.2a), rebuilds the family-outcome maths with realistic cash retention (§11), and gives the exit-track gates explicit decision branches rather than measurements (§8.4). This is the V6.1 discipline (Base case decision-making, validation gates, assumption transparency) applied more rigorously.

V7 is not the final document. It is the current document. As Phase 0 data arrives, as CQC lands, as vertical launches succeed or fail, V7 will be updated. The discipline is to update V7 against evidence, not against ambition.

The number to keep in mind: **£28-37M lifetime family combined at Optimised Base planning case.** Not £50M. Not £80M. If a buyer in 7 years offers £50M+, that is a stretch outcome above plan. If they offer £30M, that is on plan. If they offer £20M, that is below plan and triggers the decision branches in §8.4. The number is the discipline.
