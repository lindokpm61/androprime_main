## Compliance Rules

- **Ashwagandha is a silent ingredient.** Do not mention it in any copy, email, social post, affiliate brief, or influencer talking points. It appears on the label because it is in the formulation. It cannot be sold on. See root `CLAUDE.md` for the full rule.
- All copy claims must use the exact EFSA-approved wording or a plain-English derivative that does not exceed the approved claim.
- Do not claim this supplement will raise testosterone levels — only that Zinc "contributes to the maintenance of normal testosterone levels."
- **Magnesium is no longer in the formulation.** Do not reference Magnesium in any Daily Stack copy. The "fatigue" angle is now driven by Active B12's energy-metabolism claim.
- "Active B12" is the marketing label for the Methylcobalamin form. The label and copy must indicate Methylcobalamin (active form) where ingredient transparency is required.
