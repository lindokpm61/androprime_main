# Supplement Formulation — RCT Evidence Review

**Created:** 2026-07-02 | **Owner:** Keith | **Status:** Internal R&D. Dose/form optimisation evidence. No change approved — recommendations require Ewa + manufacturer sign-off.
**Scope:** Human RCTs / meta-analyses for every ingredient in the live range (Daily Stack, Joint & Recovery Collagen) and the three pipeline loops (Omega-3, Thyroid, Homocysteine B-complex).

> **Read first — this is not marketing input.** External claims remain governed by the EFSA authorised-claims list regardless of what these trials show. Nothing here relaxes a claim. Ashwagandha stays a silent ingredient externally.

> **The strategic thread:** for zinc, vitamin D, and B12 the RCT benefits appear almost entirely in *deficient* subjects and are null in replete people. This **validates the test-first model** — we recommend each ingredient only to the men whose kit shows the matching deficiency, i.e. exactly the subgroup where the evidence holds. It also constrains copy: we cannot promise a felt benefit to a general (mostly replete) audience.

## 1. Daily Stack (capsules)

| Ingredient | Our spec | Evidence verdict | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zinc | 30 mg gluconate | Raises testosterone only in **zinc-deficient** men; null in replete/unselected men (FAZST RCT, n≈2,370). 30 mg exceeds EU supplemental UL of 25 mg. | **Drop to 25 mg** (under UL) and **add ~1 mg copper** (30 mg zinc long-term depletes copper). Gluconate form fine. |
| Vitamin D3 | 4,000 IU | Muscle-strength benefit concentrated in deficient/older subjects; null in replete athletes (Beaudart 2014 meta SMD 0.17; VITAL/D-Health null in replete). Testosterone: rise only in deficient men (Pilz 2011, confounded by weight loss); **null in replete men** (Lerchbaum 2017). D3 > D2 (Tripkovic 2012), but the gap is small for daily dosing. | **Keep 4,000 IU** (= IOM UL, safe, no monitoring implied). **Best-evidenced cofactor is magnesium, not K2** (Dai 2018 RCT — Mg modulates 25(OH)D; every vit-D enzyme is Mg-dependent). **K2 is speculative/marketing** — AVADEC 2022 even hinted at harm; never claim K2 is needed for D3 safety. ⚠️ Mg-cofactor conflicts with our V7.2 decision to *remove* Mg (no kit trigger) — that was a marketing-logic call, not a formulation-science one; flag for reconciliation. |
| Active B12 | 1,000 mcg methylcobalamin | 1,000 mcg is adequate (Markun 2021 meta, n=6,276 — **null on energy/cognition/mood in replete people**; Kuzminski 1998 — high-dose oral corrects deficiency). 5,000 mcg = more absorbed mass, no demonstrated benefit (passive absorption ~1.2%, Berlin 1968). Methyl vs cyano: better retained, **no proven clinical superiority** (Adams 1971). | **Keep 1,000 mcg methylcobalamin.** Don't upgrade to 5,000 mcg. Keep "active form" positioning but never claim clinical superiority over cyanocobalamin. |
| Ashwagandha KSM-66 | 600 mg | **600 mg/day is the validated dose** — Salve 2019 dose-ranging (250 vs 600) showed 600 mg clearly superior; cortisol strongest claim, T modest & population-dependent (~15%, only in overweight/older/newly-training men). | **Keep exactly 600 mg KSM-66.** ⚠️ **New safety flag: hepatotoxicity.** NIH LiverTox rates ashwagandha a "probable" cause of liver injury (score C); 2023 case series exist. Trials only ran 8–12 wks. Add a liver caution + consider a duration/cycling note. Silent externally (no claim), but the liver risk is a real product-safety issue for Ewa. |

**Interactions to note:** metformin depletes B12 (supportive rationale for metabolic audience); zinc–copper antagonism (hence the copper add); ashwagandha may raise thyroid hormone and lower glucose/BP (additive with those meds), and is contraindicated in pregnancy.

## 2. Joint & Recovery Collagen (powder) — biggest fixes here

| Ingredient | Our spec | Evidence verdict | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hydrolysed collagen (I & III) | 10 g | Analgesic efficacy in knee OA confirmed by RCT meta-analysis; evidenced range 5–15 g. | **Keep 10 g** (if we keep the hydrolysed route — see combination flag). |
| UC-II | 40 mg | **40 mg is the exact evidenced dose** — Lugo 2013 (healthy active adults with exercise-induced knee discomfort — *our* population), Lugo 2016 OA (n=191), Kumar 2023 meta (WOMAC −8.9). | **Keep 40 mg** if used — but see combination flag. |
| Vitamin C | 80 mg | Adequate collagen-synthesis cofactor (tissue saturation ~100 mg, Levine 1996; Shaw 2017 effect on ~50 mg). Role is catalytic, not dose-hungry. | **Keep 80 mg.** |
| MSM | 500 mg | **Sub-therapeutic.** Every positive OA/recovery RCT used 1.5–6 g/day (typical 3 g). No RCT supports 500 mg total/day. | **Raise to 3 g** (trivial in a powder) or drop. |
| Hyaluronic acid | 5 mg | **Sub-therapeutic.** Oral HA trials use 80–240 mg (Tashiro 2012, 200 mg). 5 mg has no oral RCT support. | **Raise to ~80–120 mg** or drop. |

### Combination flag (decision required)
The **only human RCT of UC-II + hydrolysed collagen combined** (Sci Rep 2025, n=68) was **null vs placebo** — and it under-dosed UC-II at 20 mg. Every *positive* UC-II trial used **UC-II 40 mg alone**. Mechanisms differ (UC-II = oral tolerance at mg scale; hydrolysed = gram-scale substrate) so they're not antagonistic, but there is **zero evidence the combo beats UC-II monotherapy**, and pairing them tells two contradictory stories (keep-intact vs broken-down).

**Pick one lane:**
- **A. UC-II standalone** — UC-II 40 mg small capsule. Best-matched evidence for "active men, exercise-induced joint discomfort," and cheap. Drop hydrolysed collagen.
- **B. Hydrolysed route** — 10 g hydrolysed collagen powder + fix MSM (→3 g) + HA (→~100 mg); remove UC-II.

Do **not** ship both at current doses — that's the one configuration with a failed trial behind it.

### Free upgrade
Shaw 2017: collagen + vitamin C taken **~1 h before exercise** drives tendon/ligament collagen synthesis. Add a "take before training" usage instruction — evidence-based, costs nothing, differentiates.

## 3. Pipeline loops

| Product | Evidence verdict | Action / flag |
|---|---|---|
| **Omega-3** (2 g EPA/DHA, rTG) | rTG absorbs better than ethyl-ester (Dyerberg 2010: rTG 124% vs EE 73%; Neubronner 2011). ~1,250 mg/day reaches the 8% Omega-3 Index target; 2 g comfortably above (Flock 2013). Primary-prevention CV trials **null** (VITAL, ASCEND); high-dose split (REDUCE-IT positive EPA-only vs STRENGTH null). EPA-predominant ratio aligns with the mood evidence (Liao 2019). | Keep rTG + 2 g + EPA-forward ratio. Frame around triglycerides/index-correction + mood, **not** heart-disease prevention. ⚠️ **AFib safety flag:** omega-3 raises incident atrial fibrillation dose-dependently (Gencer 2021 meta, HR 1.25; >1 g/day HR 1.49). At 2 g the risk is intermediate but non-zero — add a label caution for anyone with prior AFib/palpitations. Bleeding risk fine to ~5 g (EFSA). Vegan algal SKU: bioequivalent for DHA (Arterburn 2008) but source EPA-containing algae to hold the ratio. (Rawcreation can't make this — softgel.) |
| **Thyroid (Se + I)** | Selenium reduces TPO antibodies (Hashimoto meta, SMD −0.96, n=2,358); **selenomethionine > selenite**. Iodine has a U-shaped risk curve — excess can trigger/worsen autoimmune thyroiditis, the exact target population. | Use **selenomethionine ~100 mcg** (long-term diabetes signal at 200 mcg). **Reconsider iodine** — high-dose iodine here is a real safety liability. **Ewa gate mandatory.** |
| **Homocysteine B-complex** | Folate lowers homocysteine ~25% (plateau ~0.8 mg), B12 adds ~7%, **B6 ≈ nil** (HLTC 1998). Hard endpoints **null** — HOPE-2, NORVIT, VISP, SEARCH, VITATOPS, Cochrane 2017 all null for CV events/mortality. Real benefit only in narrow subgroups: primary stroke in **low-folate/unfortified** populations (CSPPT, 21% RRR) and cognition in **high-homocysteine** subgroups (VITACOG, FACIT). | Use **methylfolate (5-MTHF) 400–800 mcg** (genotype-agnostic, no unmetabolised folic acid) + **B12 methylcobalamin 250–500 mcg**; keep **B6 at a token dose**. Marker/retest play only — don't stand behind a clinical outcome. ⚠️ **Two men's-product safety flags:** (1) **B6 neuropathy** — EFSA cut the UL to **12 mg/day (2023)**; keep total B6 well under it (the 25–50 mg used in old trials is unsafe for chronic use). (2) **Folic acid → prostate-cancer signal** (Figueiredo 2009, HR 2.63) — a specific reason to use **5-MTHF at modest dose, never high folic acid** in a male product. Also: folic acid masks B12 deficiency — dose B12 adequately. |

---

## Priority changes ("make it the best product")
1. **Collagen: resolve the UC-II vs hydrolysed lane** (biggest single decision) and **fix MSM (→3 g) + HA (→~100 mg)**.
2. **Daily Stack: zinc 30→25 mg + add ~1 mg copper; add K2 100 mcg** with the D3.
3. **Keep ashwagandha 600 mg and B12 1,000 mcg methyl exactly** — already optimal.
4. **Thyroid: selenomethionine, minimal/no iodine** — Ewa safety gate.
5. **Homocysteine: methylfolate over folic acid, low B6**, marker/retest framing only.
6. **Add "take before training" to collagen** (Shaw).

## Full citations (PMIDs verified)

**Zinc**
- Prasad 1996, *Nutrition* 12(5):344–8, PMID 8875519 — restriction crashes T; ~30 mg/day zinc gluconate raised T in marginally-deficient elderly men.
- Netter 1981, *Arch Androl* 7(1):69–73, PMID 7271365 — T rise only in low-baseline-T subgroup.
- Te 2023, *J Trace Elem Med Biol* 76:127124, PMID 36577241 — systematic review; effect depends on baseline status.
- FAZST 2020 (folic acid + zinc RCT) — null on T/sperm in unselected men.
- Barrie 1987, *Agents Actions* 21:223–8, PMID 3630857 — picolinate out-absorbed gluconate (single small study).
- Yadrick 1989, *AJCN* 49:145–50 — 50 mg/day lowered copper status → basis of the 40 mg US UL (EFSA UL 25 mg).

**Vitamin D3**
- Pilz 2011, *Horm Metab Res* 43(3):223–5, PMID 21154195 — T +25% in deficient men (weight-loss confounded).
- Lerchbaum 2017, *JCEM* 102(11):4292, PMID 28938446 — **no T effect in replete men**.
- Abu-Zaid 2024 meta, *Diseases* 12(10):228, PMID 39452471 — small total-T bump, no free-T effect, high heterogeneity.
- Beaudart 2014, *JCEM* 99(11):4336, PMID 25033068 — strength SMD 0.17, driven by deficient/older.
- VITAL (Manson 2019, NEJM, PMID 30415637) / D-Health (Neale 2022) — null in replete.
- Tripkovic 2012, *AJCN* 95(6):1357–64, PMID 22552031 — D3 > D2 (gap small for daily dosing).
- Dai 2018, *AJCN* 108(6):1249–58, PMID 30541089 — **magnesium modulates 25(OH)D** (cofactor RCT). K2: AVADEC/Diederichsen 2022, *Circulation* 145:1387–97 (no benefit, possible harm).

**Active B12 (methylcobalamin)**
- Markun 2021, *Nutrients* 13(3):923, PMID 33809274 — 16 RCTs, n=6,276; **null on energy/cognition/mood in replete**.
- Kuzminski 1998, *Blood* 92(4):1191–8, PMID 9694707 — oral high-dose corrects deficiency.
- Berlin 1968, *Acta Med Scand* 184:247–58, PMID 5751528 — ~1.2% passive absorption.
- Adams 1971, *Scand J Gastroenterol* 6(3):249–52 — forms absorbed similarly; methyl retained slightly better, no clinical-outcome edge.

**Ashwagandha KSM-66**
- Chandrasekhar 2012, *Indian J Psychol Med* 34(3):255–62, PMID 23439798 — 600 mg/day, cortisol −27.9%.
- Salve 2019, *Cureus* 11(12):e6466, PMID 32021735 — **250 vs 600 mg dose-ranging; 600 mg superior**.
- Wankhede 2015, *JISSN* 12:43, PMID 26609282 — T + strength (untrained men, training-confounded).
- Lopresti 2019, *Am J Mens Health* 13(2), PMID 30854916 — T +14.7% (Shoden, overweight/older men).
- Bonilla 2021 meta, *JFMK* 6(1):20, PMID 33670194; Akhgarjand 2022, *Phytother Res*, PMID 36017529.
- **Safety:** NIH LiverTox (NBK548536) — "probable" hepatotoxin (score C); Ashwagandha liver-injury case series, *Hepatol Commun* 2023.

**Hydrolysed collagen**
- Clark 2008, *Curr Med Res Opin* 24(5):1485–96, PMID 18416885 — **10 g/day**, athletes, joint pain (Gelita-funded).
- Zdzieblik 2017, *Appl Physiol Nutr Metab* 42(6):588–95, PMID 28177710 — 5 g bioactive peptides, active knees.
- Kviatkovsky 2023, *JISSN* 20(1):2243252, PMID 37551682 — 10 vs 20 g in active adults 40–65 (no added benefit at 20 g).
- García-Coronado 2019 meta, *Int Orthop* 43(3):531–8, PMID 30368550 — WOMAC/VAS benefit **but** contested by von Hippel 2021, PMID 34636929.

**UC-II**
- Lugo 2013, *JISSN* 10:48, PMID 24153020 (PMC4015808) — **healthy active adults, exercise-induced knee discomfort, 40 mg**.
- Lugo 2016, *Nutr J* 15:14, PMID 26822714 (PMC4731911) — n=191 OA, 40 mg beat glucosamine+chondroitin.
- Crowley 2009, *Int J Med Sci* 6(6):312–21 (medsci.org/v06p0312).
- Kumar 2023 meta, *Am J Transl Res* 15(9):5545–55, PMID 37854210 — WOMAC MD −8.9.
- **UC-II + hydrolysed combo null RCT:** *Sci Rep* 2025;15, PMC12405528 (n=68, null vs placebo; UC-II under-dosed at 20 mg).

**MSM**
- Kim 2006, *Osteoarthritis Cartilage* 14(3):286–94, PMID 16309928 — 6 g/day.
- Debbi 2011, *BMC Complement Altern Med* 11:50, PMID 21708034 — ~3.4 g/day.
- Usha & Naidu 2004, *Clin Drug Investig* 24(6):353–63, PMID 17516722 — 1.5 g/day (lowest positive total dose).
- Brien 2008 review PMID 18417375 / 2011 meta — positive but preliminary. **No RCT supports 500 mg/day.**

**Vitamin C**
- Shaw 2017, *AJCN* 105(1):136–43, PMID 27852613 — gelatin + ~50 mg vit C, ~1 h pre-exercise, doubled collagen-synthesis marker.
- Lis & Baar 2019, *IJSNEM* 29(5):526–31, PMID 30859848 — ~15 g threshold for the acute synthesis signal.
- Levine 1996, *PNAS* 93(8):3704–9, PMID 8623000 — tissue saturation ~100 mg (80 mg adequate as cofactor).

**Hyaluronic acid (oral)**
- Tashiro 2012, *ScientificWorldJournal* 2012:167928, PMID 23226979 — **200 mg/day**, 12 mo.
- Kalman 2008, *Nutr J* 7:3 — lowest-HA trial ~40 mg/day (Mobilee matrix). Oe 2016 review PMID 26818459 — trial range 48–240 mg, modal 200 mg. **No trial near 5 mg.**

**Omega-3**
- Flock 2013, *JAHA* 2(6):e000513, PMID 24252845 — dose-response to Omega-3 Index (1,800 mg → 9.5%).
- Dyerberg 2010, *PLEFA* 83(3):137–41, PMID 20638827 — rTG 124% vs EE 73% vs natural TG 100%.
- Neubronner 2011, *EJCN* 65(2):247–54, PMID 21063431 — rTG > EE membrane incorporation, plateau ~6 mo.
- VITAL (PMID 30415637) / ASCEND (PMID 30146932) — null primary-prevention. REDUCE-IT (PMID 30415628, positive, EPA-only 4 g) vs STRENGTH (PMID 33190147, null + excess AFib).
- Liao 2019 meta, *Transl Psychiatry* 9:190, PMID 31383846 — EPA-predominant benefit on depression.
- Arterburn 2008, *J Am Diet Assoc* 108(7):1204–9, PMID 18589030 — algal DHA bioequivalent.
- **Safety:** Gencer 2021, *Circulation* 144(25):1981–90, PMID 34612056 — AFib HR 1.25, dose-dependent (>1 g/day HR 1.49). EFSA 2012 — bleeding safe to ~5 g/day.

**Selenium / iodine (thyroid)**
- Gärtner 2002, *JCEM* 87(4):1687–91, PMID 11932302 (selenite, TPOAb −36%); Duntas 2003, *Eur J Endocrinol* 148(4):389–95, PMID 12656658 (selenomethionine, TPOAb −55%).
- Winther/Wichman 2016 meta, *Thyroid* 26(12):1681–92, PMID 27702392; **CATALYST 2024, *Eur Thyroid J* 13(1):e230175 — null on quality-of-life (hard outcome)**.
- **Safety:** Stranges 2007, *Ann Intern Med* 147(4):217–23, PMID 17620655 — selenium 200 mcg diabetes HR 1.55; SELECT (PMID 19066370). Teng 2006, *NEJM* 354:2783–93, PMID 16807415 — iodine U-shaped harm curve (autoimmune flare).

**Homocysteine B-complex**
- HLTC 1998, *BMJ* 316:894–8, PMID 9569395 — folate −25%, B12 +7%, B6 ~nil.
- Hard-endpoint null: HOPE-2 (PMID 16612465), NORVIT (PMID 16531614), VISP (PMID 14762035), SEARCH (PMID 20571015), VITATOPS (PMID 20688574), Cochrane 2017 (CD006612).
- Subgroup positives: CSPPT 2015, *JAMA* 313:1325–35, PMID 25771069 (low-folate stroke); VITACOG (PMID 20838622) / FACIT (PMID 17240287) (high-homocysteine cognition).
- **Safety:** EFSA 2023 B6 UL lowered to 12 mg/day (PMID 37207271, neuropathy); Figueiredo 2009, *JNCI*, PMID 19276452 — folic acid **prostate-cancer** HR 2.63. 5-MTHF vs folic acid: PMC9380836 (equivalent tHcy, no UMFA).

> Cross-cutting caveat: many collagen/MSM/HA/UC-II/ashwagandha trials are small (n≈40–191), short (≤6 mo), and manufacturer-sponsored (UC-II = Lonza/InterHealth; collagen = Gelita; ashwagandha = Ixoreal/Arjuna). Interpret modest effects accordingly. Keep external claims to authorised EFSA structure-function wording only regardless of trial strength.
