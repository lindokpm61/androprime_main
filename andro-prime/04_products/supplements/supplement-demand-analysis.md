Source: DataForSEO `overview` + `suggest`, UK. **KD = DataForSEO scale** (not comparable to the
Semrush KDs in keywords.csv); volumes are provider-agnostic Google data.

**The lens that matters here:** an Andro Prime supplement needs three things, not one —
(1) real demand, (2) an **EFSA-approved claim** (or we hit the "ashwagandha trap" — can't say a
word about it), and (3) model fit (ideally a biomarker trigger from a kit). Demand alone is a trap.

## The strategic tension (the real takeaway)

Three of the four biggest supplement demands in the market are things we **can't capitalise on under our own rules**: ashwagandha (165k, silent), berberine (90.5k, non-authorised), electrolytes/protein (off-model commodity). That's not a failure — it's the cost of the EFSA-claimable, data-triggered thesis, and the thesis is sound (it's what keeps us ASA-safe and differentiated).

But two findings are genuine decisions for Keith:

1. **Creatine (201k) is the one large, EFSA-claimable, men's-core demand we're leaving on the table** — but it is structurally immune to our data model, not just missing a marker:
   - **No consumer biomarker exists.** What matters is muscle phosphocreatine *saturation*, measurable only by muscle biopsy or MR spectroscopy (research tools). Serum creatine swings with diet; serum **creatinine** is a kidney marker that actually *rises* with supplementation. No finger-prick test reflects creatine status.
   - **No baseline to personalise against.** Diet alone leaves most people ~60–80% saturated; a flat 3–5 g/day tops everyone to a ~100% ceiling (excess excreted). Everyone takes the same dose regardless of starting point — no threshold, no titration. The only "starts lower" signal is dietary (vegetarians/vegans), inferred not measured.
   - **So it can't carry our differentiator.** Unlike the Daily Stack (your dose follows your numbers), creatine would be an undifferentiated flat-dose commodity competing on price/brand. Our data moat can't touch it.
   - **Verdict:** if pursued, it's a pure demand play in a **separate performance line** (creatine ± the KD0 "creatine gummies" format), explicitly *not* a data product, kept clear of the test-triggered Daily Stack. Or hold the line and stay test-triggered only.
2. **Magnesium deserves a second look** — removed for thesis purity, but 60.5k demand and strong EFSA fatigue/sleep/recovery claims. Could return to the Stack or anchor a "recovery/sleep" angle.

## Recommendations

1. **Lean into collagen now (no new product).** It's our strongest-validated demand (74k/KD14) and the men's angle is wide open (`collagen for men` KD6, `joint supplement` 5,400/KD6). Build a collagen/joint content cluster routing to the existing Collagen product. Quick SEO win.
2. **Put creatine to Keith as a strategy decision** — demand-led men's performance line vs thesis purity. Don't build silently; it's a positioning choice.
3. **Re-evaluate magnesium** against demand, not just trigger logic.
4. **Do NOT build for marketing:** berberine, turmeric, tongkat ali, ashwagandha-as-hero — high demand, no EFSA claim, all repeats of the ashwagandha trap.
5. Seed the collagen-for-men + creatine (if greenlit) clusters into `keywords.csv` once direction is set.
