## 1. Why this kit (the strategic case)

UK private blood-test market gap analysis (Semrush sweep 2026-05-26) shows:

- **HbA1c blood test** — 12,100 vol/mo, KD 46. SERP dominated by diabetes charities + NHS regional pages. **Zero UK men-focused commercial specialist** currently ranks. Massive content + commercial gap.
- **ApoB test** — 590 vol/mo, KD **13**. Advanced cardiovascular marker the NHS does not measure. Almost no UK home-test competitor offers it. Genuine product differentiator.
- **Homocysteine test uk** — 260 vol/mo, KD **12**. Advanced cardio + cognitive marker. Same gap.
- **Cholesterol test at home** — 1,000 vol/mo, KD **15**. LP-grade commercial intent, low KD.
- **Fasting insulin test** — 480 vol/mo, KD **15**. Enables HOMA-IR insulin-resistance calculation. Predictive of pre-diabetes 5-10 years before HbA1c moves.
- **Private cholesterol test** — 320 vol/mo, KD **19**.
- **Lipid blood test** — 880 vol/mo, KD 48.
- **Fasting glucose test** — 720 vol/mo, KD 42.
- **Triglycerides test** — 210 vol/mo, KD 50.

**Combined targetable demand: ~18,000 vol/mo across the marker cluster.** Six entries clear the strict KD ≤ 20 gate.

**Brand fit:** Metabolic + cardiovascular risk is the #1 preventable health concern in Andro Prime's ICP (men 35-55). Aligns perfectly with V7 brand thesis ("data-first wellness, the markers your GP only runs when you're already sick"). The "find out before it's a problem" narrative is the brand's strongest angle and ApoB/Homocysteine are textbook examples of what the NHS misses.

**Cross-sell flywheel:**

- Existing Daily Stack customers with persistent fatigue could be insulin-resistant → Kit 4 upsell
- Kit 2 customers whose Vit D + B12 + Ferritin came back fine but still feel sluggish → Kit 4 as next layer
- Kit 1 customers with normal T who want to investigate "what else" → Kit 4

## 3. Provisional marker stack

| Marker | What it measures | NHS run for healthy adults? | Andro Prime differentiator |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HbA1c** | 3-month average blood sugar; pre-diabetes / diabetes risk | Sometimes (only if symptomatic) | Standard |
| **Fasting Insulin** | Insulin resistance (combined with glucose = HOMA-IR) | Rarely | YES — most home tests don't include |
| **Fasting Glucose** | Current blood sugar | Sometimes | Standard |
| **Total Cholesterol** | Cardiovascular risk | Sometimes (NHS Health Check 40+) | Standard |
| **HDL Cholesterol** | "Good" cholesterol | Sometimes | Standard |
| **LDL Cholesterol** | "Bad" cholesterol | Sometimes | Standard |
| **Triglycerides** | Fat in blood, metabolic syndrome | Sometimes | Standard |
| **ApoB** | The actual particle count carrying cholesterol — more predictive than LDL | **No, NHS does not test** | **STRONG DIFFERENTIATOR** |
| **Homocysteine** | Amino acid; elevated = cardiovascular + cognitive risk | **No, NHS does not test** | **STRONG DIFFERENTIATOR** |
| **hs-CRP** | (Already in Kit 2) — inflammation marker; cardiometabolic risk | No | Standard if added |

**Open question for Ewa:** Add **Lp(a)** (Lipoprotein-a)? Genetic CV risk marker, NHS doesn't test, very high E-E-A-T appeal for the "data-first" brand. May add cost.

**Open question for Ewa:** Add **Liver markers (ALT, AST, GGT)** to flag fatty liver (NAFLD), which is metabolic-syndrome adjacent? Would broaden the panel without huge cost addition.

## 5. Compliance gates (Ewa decisions required)

| Risk area | Issue | Resolution path |
|---|---|---|
| "Pre-diabetes" / "diabetes risk" framing | HbA1c result in 42-47 mmol/mol range is pre-diabetic per NICE — can we say "your result suggests pre-diabetic range"? | Ewa to draft EFSA-compliant + ASA-safe language |
| Cardiovascular risk framing | ApoB / Homocysteine elevated → "your cardiovascular risk markers are elevated" vs "you have heart disease" | Ewa to draft hedged language with GP-referral CTA |
| Cancer screening adjacency | Some HbA1c elevations correlate with pancreatic cancer risk — we MUST NOT mention | Pre-flight rule |
| Daily Stack cross-sell | Zinc / Vit D / B12 don't have approved EFSA claims for "metabolic health" — can we cross-sell on a Kit 4 result? | Likely NO direct cross-sell — Kit 4 results probably route to **lifestyle guidance + Daily Stack only via "general wellness support"** + new metabolic-specific supplement opportunity (future product) |
| TRT availability | Phase 0 boundary — metabolic kit must not imply TRT availability | Standard rule |
| Statin recommendation | LDL/ApoB elevated → cannot recommend statins (POM) | Hard rule — GP-referral only |
