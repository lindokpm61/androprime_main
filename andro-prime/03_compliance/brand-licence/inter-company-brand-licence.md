## BACKGROUND

**A.** The Licensor is the owner of the Andro Prime brand, including the trade name "Andro Prime", the "AndroPrime" wordmark, the "AP" logo mark, all associated design elements, brand guidelines, and any trade marks applied for or registered in connection with the same ("the Brand IP").

**B.** The Licensee is a company incorporated in England and Wales in which Keith Lindo holds 50% of the issued share capital and Dr Ewa Lindo holds 50% of the issued share capital. The Licensee intends to apply for CQC registration and, upon approval, operate a regulated clinical testosterone replacement service under the Andro Prime brand.

**C.** The Licensor wishes to grant, and the Licensee wishes to accept, a licence to use the Brand IP on the terms and conditions set out in this agreement.

**D.** Both entities are ultimately under the control of Keith Lindo, who is the sole director and 100% shareholder of the Licensor, and a 50% shareholder and director of the Licensee. Nothing in this agreement shall be construed as creating a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the parties.

## AGREED TERMS

---

### 1. Definitions

In this agreement:

**"Brand Guidelines"** means the document titled "Andro Prime Brand Guidelines" dated April 2026, as updated from time to time by the Licensor, a copy of which is held at `02_brand/brand-guidelines.md` in the Licensor's operating system.

**"Brand IP"** means the trade name "Andro Prime", the wordmark "AndroPrime", the "AP" logo mark, the colour palette, typography, tone of voice, and all other brand identity elements described in the Brand Guidelines, together with any trade mark applications or registrations subsisting in relation to the same.

**"CQC Registration"** means registration of the Licensee with the Care Quality Commission as a regulated provider of clinical services in England.

**"Licensed Activities"** means the operation of a regulated clinical testosterone replacement therapy service and associated clinical services under the Andro Prime brand, following CQC Registration.

**"Pre-CQC Period"** means the period from the date of this agreement until the date on which the Licensee obtains CQC Registration.

**"Territory"** means the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland.

---

### 2. Grant of Licence

**2.1** The Licensor grants to the Licensee a non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-free licence to use the Brand IP in the Territory solely for the purposes of the Licensed Activities, subject to the terms and conditions of this agreement.

**2.2** During the Pre-CQC Period, the Licensee may use the Brand IP solely for the purposes of:

  (a) incorporating as a company under the name "Andro Prime Ltd";

  (b) applying for CQC Registration;

  (c) preparing clinical protocols, consent documentation, and internal governance materials in anticipation of regulated operations;

  (d) appearing on the Licensor's website and marketing materials as the future clinical entity, provided all such references make clear that clinical services are not yet available and are subject to CQC Registration.

**2.3** For the avoidance of doubt, the licence granted in clause 2.1 does not extend to:

  (a) the sale of wellness products (blood test kits, nutritional supplements, founding member deposits), which shall remain exclusively within the operations of the Licensor;

  (b) any marketing activity directed at consumers in respect of services not yet available;

  (c) any use of the Brand IP outside the Territory without the prior written consent of the Licensor.

---

### 3. Quality Control and Brand Standards

**3.1** The Licensee shall at all times use the Brand IP strictly in accordance with the Brand Guidelines as provided by the Licensor from time to time.

**3.2** The Licensee shall not use the Brand IP in any manner that, in the reasonable opinion of the Licensor, is likely to:

  (a) bring the Licensor or the Andro Prime brand into disrepute;

  (b) mislead consumers as to the nature, availability, or regulatory status of the Licensee's services;

  (c) breach any applicable law, regulation, or regulatory guidance, including but not limited to CQC standards, GMC guidance, the Advertising Standards Code, and the Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008.

**3.3** The Licensor reserves the right to inspect any materials produced by the Licensee bearing the Brand IP and to require amendments within 14 days of written notice if those materials do not comply with the Brand Guidelines or clause 3.2.

**3.4** The Licensee shall not make any modification to the Brand IP without the prior written consent of the Licensor.

---

### 4. Intellectual Property Ownership

**4.1** The Licensee acknowledges that all rights, title, and interest in and to the Brand IP vest exclusively in the Licensor and that the Licensee acquires no ownership rights under this agreement.

**4.2** The Licensee shall not at any time do or omit to do anything that might damage, imperil, or challenge the Licensor's ownership of the Brand IP.

**4.3** The Licensee shall not apply for any trade mark, domain name, or other registration that incorporates the Brand IP or any mark confusingly similar to it without the prior written consent of the Licensor.

**4.4** If the Licensee becomes aware of any actual or threatened infringement of the Brand IP by a third party, it shall notify the Licensor promptly in writing. The Licensor shall have sole discretion as to whether and how to take action in respect of such infringement.

**4.5** Any goodwill generated through the Licensee's use of the Brand IP shall accrue solely to the benefit of the Licensor.

---

### 5. Consideration

**5.1** In consideration of the rights granted under this agreement, the Licensee shall pay to the Licensor a licence fee of **£1 per annum** (one pound sterling per year), payable on the anniversary of the date of this agreement.

**5.2** The parties acknowledge that the above consideration reflects the early-stage nature of the business and the common beneficial ownership of both entities. The parties agree to review the licence fee structure no later than 12 months after the Licensee obtains CQC Registration, at which point a commercial royalty arrangement may be negotiated in good faith.

**5.3** All amounts payable under this agreement are exclusive of VAT, which shall be added at the applicable rate where applicable.

---

### 6. Sublicensing and Assignment

**6.1** The Licensee shall not sublicense, assign, transfer, charge, or otherwise deal with the benefit or burden of this agreement or any of the rights granted under it without the prior written consent of the Licensor.

**7.1** The Licensor warrants that:

  (a) it is the owner of the Brand IP and has the right to grant the licence set out in this agreement;

  (b) to the best of its knowledge, the use of the Brand IP in accordance with this agreement will not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party.

**7.2** The Licensee warrants that:

  (a) it has full authority to enter into this agreement;

  (b) it will use the Brand IP only in accordance with the terms of this agreement and all applicable laws and regulations;

  (c) it will obtain and maintain all licences, registrations, and approvals required to carry on the Licensed Activities before commencing those activities.

### 8. Liability

**8.1** Neither party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, special, or consequential loss or damage arising out of or in connection with this agreement.

**8.2** Nothing in this agreement shall limit or exclude either party's liability for fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation, death or personal injury caused by negligence, or any other liability that cannot be excluded or limited by law.

---

### 9. Term and Termination

**9.1** This agreement shall commence on the date of execution and shall continue until terminated in accordance with this clause 9.

**9.2** The Licensor may terminate this agreement immediately by written notice to the Licensee if:

  (a) the Licensee commits a material breach of this agreement and (where the breach is capable of remedy) fails to remedy it within 30 days of written notice requiring it to do so;

  (b) the Licensee undergoes a change of control without the prior written consent of the Licensor;

  (c) the Licensee enters administration, liquidation, receivership, or any analogous process;

  (d) the Licensee's CQC Registration is revoked or suspended and not reinstated within 90 days;

  (e) the Licensee uses the Brand IP in a manner that in the Licensor's reasonable opinion materially damages the brand or brings either party into disrepute.

**9.3** Either party may terminate this agreement on 90 days' written notice.

**9.4** On termination for any reason:

  (a) all rights granted to the Licensee under this agreement shall immediately cease;

  (b) the Licensee shall immediately cease all use of the Brand IP;

  (c) the Licensee shall (at the Licensor's election) destroy or return all materials bearing the Brand IP;

  (d) the Licensee shall take all steps necessary to change its registered company name to a name that does not incorporate "Andro Prime" or any confusingly similar name within 60 days of termination.

**9.5** Termination of this agreement shall not affect any accrued rights or liabilities of either party.

### 10. Confidentiality

**10.1** Each party shall keep confidential all confidential information of the other party obtained in connection with this agreement and shall not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing party, except as required by law or regulation.

**10.2** This clause shall survive termination of this agreement for a period of 5 years.

**13.2** The parties irrevocably agree that the courts of England and Wales shall have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with this agreement.
