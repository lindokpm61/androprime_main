These are the compliance gates that determine the product's and content programme's commercial framing.

**(a) Pre-diabetes language on HbA1c results.** HbA1c 42-47 mmol/mol is the NICE-defined pre-diabetic range. If a customer's result comes back in this range, what is the safe customer-facing wording? My instinct is something like "your result is in the pre-diabetic range — recommended next step is a GP appointment to confirm and discuss next steps" with no "you have pre-diabetes" claim. **(b) ApoB / Homocysteine elevated wording.** Same question for the CV markers — "your cardiovascular risk markers are elevated, recommended next step is a GP appointment" vs "you are at risk of heart disease". I'd plan to use the former, but want your view on whether even "elevated CV risk markers" is too directional without a full clinical history.

**(c) Cancer-screening adjacency.** I'm aware that some HbA1c elevations have a correlation with pancreatic cancer risk. My default is **never mention cancer in any Kit 3 Plus copy** — flag elevated HbA1c, recommend GP, full stop. Confirm this is the right rule.

**(d) Daily Stack cross-sell from metabolic results.** Daily Stack's EFSA claims (zinc / testosterone, D3 / muscle function, Active B12 / energy metabolism) don't cover metabolic-syndrome support. I'm planning to **route Kit 3 Plus metabolic-out-of-range results to lifestyle guidance + GP referral, with NO Daily Stack CTA on those specific result paths**. The existing Daily Stack CTAs on the Kit 1 / Kit 2 / Kit 3 result paths (low T, low D, low B12) are unchanged. Does that match your read of the EFSA boundary, or would you split it differently?

**(e) Statin / POM territory.** Elevated LDL or ApoB obviously can't trigger a "you should consider statins" recommendation — that's POM territory. My default is **GP-referral CTA only**, no supplement suggestion, no lifestyle prescription beyond generic "diet + exercise + speak to your GP". Confirm.

**(f) The "Ewa-approved recommendation logic" framing.** Following the 2026-05-26 decision that we won't be doing per-customer GP interpretation, my plan for the Kit 3 Plus differentiator is the same framing we've moved Kit 3 to — "Ewa-approved recommendation logic" / "every threshold signed off by a GMC-registered GP" — not "personalised GP interpretation". Confirm that's the right line for the metabolic markers too, especially given the higher clinical stakes around CV / pre-diabetes results.

**(g) Pillar E (andropause / male menopause) content — what's the safe frame, and what CTA can we use?** Our SEO sweep flagged a high-intent UK demand cluster around `andropause` (5.4k vol/mo), `male menopause` (5.4k), and `hypogonadism` (5.4k) — combined ~10-15k/mo. These are exactly the men we'd want reading our content, but we can't sell TRT in Phase 0 so the obvious commercial path (FM-list opt-in framed as a TRT waiting list) carries real ASA / MHRA exposure: we'd be capturing demand for a regulated service we can't yet provide.

The version of this I think clears the line is **diagnostic-education content with a Kit 3 / Kit 3 Plus CTA, not an FM-list CTA**:

- Articles frame andropause as a *clinical concept* — what the term means, what the NHS does and doesn't do about it, what biomarkers tell you whether testosterone is part of your picture.
- No mention of TRT availability anywhere. No comparison to private TRT providers. No "join the list" language.
- CTA points at **Kit 3 (or Kit 3 Plus once it launches)** as the diagnostic step — "you can find out where your testosterone actually sits, plus the metabolic markers that often shift alongside it" — not the FM list.
- Pre-flight scan + your clinical sign-off on every article before publish, same process as the CIO sequences.

Two specific questions on this:

(g.1) **Is "diagnostic education + Kit 3/Plus CTA" a workable frame, in your view?** Or does even informational andropause content carry ASA risk for us if we also operate an FM list elsewhere on the site, even when the article itself doesn't link to it?

(g.2) **What words/concepts must not appear in Pillar E content at all?** I'm thinking: no "TRT", no "testosterone replacement", no "treatment", no comparison to NHS or private TRT providers, no implied prescribing pathway, no "low T diagnosis". Confirm or add to that list.

If the answer is "this frame works", the FM list stops being the Pillar E CTA and becomes a Kit-1-result mechanic only (unchanged from today). If the answer is "even this frame is too risky", we skip Pillar E entirely until post-CQC and accept the lost traffic.
