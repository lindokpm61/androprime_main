## 1. Why this pillar, and why it needs its own pack

The SEO sweep flagged a large, high-intent UK demand cluster the funnel currently names in product language the market doesn't use: `andropause` (5,400/mo, KD 42), `male menopause` (5,400, KD 55), `male menopause symptoms` (1,600, KD 48), `do men go through menopause` (1,600), `andropause treatment` (210, commercial), plus the adjacent `night sweats men` (8,100). Combined roughly 15-22k searches/month, with no UK men's-health specialist owning it. The GTM (v4 §12) names Pillar E the **highest-leverage narrative** in the plan and the strongest founder-journey hook.

It needs its own pack because andropause is the **single most compliance-sensitive topic** in the content programme. Three reasons:

1. **It sits right on the Phase 0 boundary.** The condition men are reading about is age-related low testosterone, and the obvious "treatment" they are searching toward is TRT, which we cannot sell until post-CQC. Capturing that demand badly (an FM-list "waiting list" CTA) would be recruiting for a regulated service we cannot yet provide. ASA + MHRA exposure.
2. **The term itself is medically loose.** "Male menopause" is a popular label, not a defined clinical event. That is a risk if we use it carelessly, and an *opportunity* if we are the honest voice that explains it (good for ASA, good for E-E-A-T).
3. **The symptoms are higher-stakes than vitamin D.** Low mood, loss of libido, and erectile dysfunction carry referral duties (mental health; ED as a cardiovascular flag) that ordinary marker content does not.

The pack defines the say / don't-say rules so every Pillar E asset can be pre-flighted and shipped without re-litigating the boundary each time.

## 2. The proposed frame (one line)

**Diagnostic education, not a treatment funnel.** Explain the concept honestly, point the reader at a blood test (Kit 1) to find out where their testosterone actually sits, and refer to the GP for anything clinical. No TRT language. No FM-list CTA. No implied prescribing pathway.

This is the same frame proposed in the draft email §(g); this pack supplies the actual wording.

## 3. The honesty angle (why being straight is also the safe play)

The NHS position is that "male menopause" is an unhelpful term, because unlike the female menopause it is not a universal, clearly-defined event, and most symptoms blamed on it have other causes (lifestyle, stress, poor sleep, or genuinely low testosterone). We adopt that position rather than fight it:

- It is true, so it clears ASA substantiation.
- It differentiates us from the "boost your T" crowd who oversell.
- It naturally leads to the one thing we *can* honestly offer: a test that shows you the actual number, so you stop guessing.

So the pillar's spine is: *"Male menopause is a fuzzy term for a real thing. Here's what's actually going on, and here's how you find out if testosterone is part of your picture."*

## 4. Proposed APPROVED language (Ewa to redline)

These are draft customer-facing lines. They follow the no-em-dash rule and the diagnostic-observer voice. Ewa edits or strikes any line; only signed lines may be used.

**Defining the term:**
- "Andropause, often called the male menopause, is a popular name for the cluster of symptoms some men notice as testosterone gradually declines with age."
- "Unlike the menopause, there's no single switch-off moment. The NHS points out the term can be misleading, because a lot of what gets blamed on it has other causes."

**The symptom-to-cause bridge (kept non-diagnostic):**
- "Low energy, low mood, poor sleep, a softer sex drive: these can be age-related testosterone change, or they can be sleep, stress, or lifestyle. A symptom list can't tell you which. A blood test can tell you where your testosterone actually sits."
- "Your results may show your testosterone is on the low side. That tells you there's a signal. It doesn't tell you, on its own, that it's the cause of how you feel. That's a conversation with your GP."

**What we offer (Phase 0 boundary intact):**
- "We test, we explain the number, and we tell you the next step, including when that next step is your GP. We don't diagnose and we don't prescribe."
- "You can find out where your testosterone actually sits with our [Hormone Health Check (Kit 1)]." (CTA to Kit 1, or Kit 3 as the broader hormone panel.)

**GP-referral lines (see §6 for triggers):**
- "If these symptoms are affecting your daily life, your relationship, or your mood, speak to your GP. That's not a wellness conversation."

**Forward-reference to TRT (the only permitted one):**
- Default: say nothing about TRT. If a forward-reference is unavoidable, the only permitted form is the existing Phase 0 line: "Be first to know when we launch our clinical service." No mention of TRT by name, no timeline, no "waiting list."

## 5. Proposed BANNED language (Ewa to confirm / extend)

Inherits the draft-email §(g.2) list and the CONTEXT.md red-flag table. Must not appear anywhere in Pillar E content (article, video, email, social, or affiliate derivative):

- "TRT" / "testosterone replacement therapy" / "testosterone therapy"
- "treatment" / "treat" / "cure" / "reverse" / "fix your testosterone" / "restore your levels"
- "diagnose" / "diagnosis" / "you have andropause" / "you have low T" / "low T diagnosis"
- "boost" / "optimise" your testosterone (also a CONTEXT.md / voice ban)
- Any comparison to NHS or private TRT providers (Numan, Manual, etc.) in a Pillar E context
- Any implied prescribing pathway, "get prescribed," "access treatment," "start therapy"
- "join the list" / "waiting list" / "register your interest" framed around TRT or a future clinical service
- The FM list, in any form (Pillar E routes to Kit 1/Kit 3 only; `feedback_fm_list_not_in_content`)
- "Ashwagandha" / KSM-66, anywhere, ever (silent-ingredient rule)
- Self-diagnosis testimonial from Keith ("I've got the male menopause") in the founder-journey episode (see §7)

## 6. GP-referral triggers specific to Pillar E (Ewa to set wording)

These symptoms carry a referral duty that plain marker content does not. Proposed triggers; Ewa sets the exact result/copy wording:

- **Erectile dysfunction.** Can be an early cardiovascular warning, not just a hormone issue. Proposed rule: any ED mention is paired with a GP-referral line, never with a product CTA alone.
- **Low mood / depressive symptoms.** Mental-health referral, not a supplement or test CTA. Proposed: "If your mood is low or you've lost interest in things you used to enjoy, please talk to your GP."
- **Symptoms affecting daily life / relationship.** General referral line (§4).
- **Under-40s presenting these symptoms.** Lower prior probability of age-related decline; likelier another cause. Proposed: stronger steer to GP.

## 7. The founder-journey andropause episode (specific rules)

Keith can use his own age-and-symptoms experience as the hook, but the line between *peer education* and *self-diagnosis testimonial* is the risk. Proposed rules (Ewa to confirm):

- Keith may describe **what he noticed** ("recovery slowed, energy dipped, sex drive isn't what it was") and that he **tested his own testosterone** to find out where he sits.
- Keith may **not** say or imply "I have andropause / the male menopause / low T," because a founder testimonial cannot state a claim the advertiser could not make directly, and a self-diagnosis reads as exactly that.
- No "and here's what I did about it" that implies a treatment, supplement fix, or TRT. The episode ends at the number and the next honest step, same retest framing as the rest of the series.
- The episode is gated on this pack being signed AND a per-script `compliance-preflight`.
