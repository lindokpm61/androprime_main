## Positioning statement

Go private the conventional way and the clinic holds your record. You see a number at an appointment and book a follow-up. Andro Prime inverts that: you see everything we see, your results sit in your own dashboard in plain English, they are tracked over time as you retest, and they are yours to keep whether or not you buy anything else. Underneath it is one method, applied every time: blood data before any recommendation.

The single sentence: **you're an adult, and you get to see your own data.**

## Brand pillars

Ordered. Lead with pillar 1. Pillars 2 to 4 support; never open on them.

### 1. Patient-owned data (lead)

- **Means:** The longitudinal record belongs to the customer, not the clinic. Andro Prime is the only UK men's health brand that hands you the data layer, not just a result.
- **Customer-facing translation:** "Your results, in your dashboard, tracked over time, and yours to keep." / "You see everything we see." / "Your data, not ours to sit on."
- **Do NOT say:** the word "patient" in customer-facing Phase 0 copy (Phase 0 has customers, not patients — wellness/clinical split). "Patient-owned data" is the internal pillar name only. Do not claim the dashboard interprets, scores, or advises (tracker is observation-only — see compliance posture below). Do not promise the longitudinal tracker as live before it ships (v1 is M3-M4 post-launch); "your dashboard" and "yours to keep" are true from day one, "tracked over time" lands as customers retest.

### 2. Comprehensive men's health

- **Means:** Andro Prime is open-ended men's health, not foreclosed as "TRT done properly." Leaves room for the Y2 verticals without committing Phase 0 to them.
- **Customer-facing translation:** "Men's health, measured properly" / "the conditions you don't have to just accept." Broad, not TRT-only.
- **Do NOT say:** anything that names or dates hair loss / GLP-1 / clinical verticals in Phase 0 copy. "Coming in Y2" is the ceiling, and only if asked.

### 3. Clinical credibility

- **Means:** Real doctor, accredited lab. Dr Ewa Lindo (GMC-registered, Harley Street TRT-trained) behind the model; UKAS ISO 15189 lab; results reviewed, not auto-generated.
- **Customer-facing translation:** "Real doctor. Accredited lab. Plain English." Use `trust-signals.md` for exact UKAS/GMC wording.
- **Do NOT say:** that clinical services (TRT, prescribing, monitoring) are available now. All clinical-tier language is future-conditional until CQC. Low-T routing (changed 2026-06-04): T < 12 (Kit 1/3) routes to a GP referral, no upsell, with consent-gated nurture only. The founding-member list is a dormant standalone non-cash opt-in, never a results CTA.

### 4. Premium positioning

- **Means:** Considered, direct, no wellness fluff. Not Numan, not Hims, not a Harley Street brochure. Price stated plainly, not hidden.
- **Customer-facing translation:** plain-English, data-first, pub test. "From £99" stated, not coy.
- **Do NOT say:** aspirational/spa/motivational language; medicalised jargon; anything that fails the pub test.

## The method line (always true, sits beneath the lead)

Blood data before any recommendation. Not a guess, not a generic age-and-symptoms protocol, your actual numbers. The full loop is the proof: test, interpret, recommend, supply, retest, and you keep the record at the end of it.

## Phase 0 wording rules (non-negotiable)

These are compliance and brand boundaries, not preferences. Full detail in `/03_compliance/CONTEXT.md` and the Creative Compliance Checklist in `CONTEXT.md`.

- Customers, not patients, in all Phase 0 external copy.
- Tracker/dashboard is observation-only: no "you are deficient", no "your supplement is working", no AI/insights labelling.
- No medicinal claims: never "diagnose", "treat", or "cure". The clinical TRT service is future-conditional until CQC; never imply it is live now.
- Low-T (T < 12, Kit 1/3) routes to a GP referral, no upsell (2026-06-04). The founding-member list is a dormant non-cash opt-in, never a results CTA.
- Supplement claims: EFSA-approved wording only. The Daily Stack's undisclosed botanical (Guardrail #3) is a silent ingredient: never named in any copy, anywhere.
- No em dashes. Pub test on every line.
