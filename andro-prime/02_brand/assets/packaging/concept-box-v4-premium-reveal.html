What the research changed (all 3 studies agreed)

  • Credentials promoted, not hidden. "UKAS-accredited UK laboratory" + the GMC-GP line are now a legible, designed block on the front. For a new health brand these are the #1 trust/conversion lever; burying them on the back (v3) was backwards.
  • Warm-white exterior, black reveal. White leads (clinical trust, not cold/ominous like a black-dominant health box); the premium "shout" happens on opening, via a black foil-and-deboss interior. Warm ivory (#F4F1EA), not surgical pure-white.
  • Welcome card does the reassurance. "You did the right thing" is the clinically-evidenced anxiety reducer for a man whose GP dismissed him. Plain "what happens next," GMC credential, human signatures.
  • Staged tray. In diagnostics, trust is built inside the box (the Thriva / Medichecks pattern), not on the front face.
  • Legibility for 40–55: body copy in Inter ≥14px; monospace used for data/labels only, never body.

Premium is carried by finish, not artwork (the print spec)

  • Heavy uncoated rigid board (32pt+) — weight is a felt premium cue; thin board is the fastest way to look cheap.
  • Soft-touch matte laminate — the cheapest single upgrade from "stark" to "premium"; warm to the touch.
  • Debossed AP monogram on the front (colourless ornament) + black / gunmetal foil monogram on the interior card.
  • None of this breaks the no-colour rule: black, warm-white, and finish only.

Brand-rule decisions this raises (for you / brand doc)

  • Warm-white (#F4F1EA) vs pure #FFFFFF — research strongly prefers warm-white to avoid clinical coldness. This is a small but real change to the strict palette.
  • Ink-black (#141414) vs pure #000 for large fields/body, for comfortable reading. Also a minor brand-purity relaxation.
  • Welcome-card copy is draft, pending Ewa / compliance sign-off before any print run.
