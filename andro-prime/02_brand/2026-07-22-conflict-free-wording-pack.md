## P. The governing principle (overrides the copy on every kit page)

The position is not testosterone-only. Kit 2 and Kit 3 must conform, so the principle is stated in a form that is true for all three kits, and every kit page inherits it. Two rules carry it:

> **1. Any result that needs a doctor goes to a GP, and earns us nothing.** True on every kit: low testosterone (Kit 1/3), low ferritin and other GP-tier results (Kit 2/3), anything the results engine routes to a GP.
>
> **2. No result changes what we offer or what it costs.** The same options at the same prices whether your numbers are flagged or fine (the locked Option 4 rule plus the all-clear maintenance offer make this substantiable). A flagged result never unlocks a sales pitch a fine result would not get.

The testosterone-specific line ("no reason to sell you testosterone") is the **spearhead**: it names the sharpest conflict in the market and stays in the press/GEO layer (section E). On-site copy uses the umbrella form so all kits conform. Scope note added to the decision doc.

## A. The standing claim

The paragraph that carries the position on About and How-it-works, and in shortened form in the footer trust block. This is the wording Ewa's principle-level agreement needs to land on.

**A1 (recommended, plain):**
> Testing and selling are kept apart at Andro Prime. You pay one price for the test. Any result that needs a doctor, low testosterone included, goes to a GP, and those results earn us nothing.

**A2 (shorter, footer-weight):**
> One price for the test. A result that needs a doctor goes to a GP, not a checkout, and earns us nothing.

**A3 (fuller, About page):**
> Most men's health brands make their money after a bad result. We built Andro Prime the other way round. You pay one price for the test. Any result that needs a doctor goes to a GP, and earns us nothing. And no result changes what we offer or what it costs. The report has no reason to lean.

*(Compliance F1, applied 2026-07-22: earlier drafts claimed "we earn the same whether your results are fine or not". That absolute is not substantiable once supplements attach post-result; the revenue actually skews in the customer's favour, so the claim above is both true and stronger: a low result earns us nothing.)*
