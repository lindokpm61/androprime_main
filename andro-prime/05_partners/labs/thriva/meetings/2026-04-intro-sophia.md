## Key Points

### Thriva's service model
- 360-degree service: kit assembly, distribution, lab analysis, API integration, clinical governance, account management
- Public API with sandbox environment
- Free API integration, but requires 200 tests/month within 3 months to justify engineering resource
- Account manager (Lissy) available for ongoing support
- Clinical team reviews panel selections for viability

### Pricing (ballpark, pre-quote)
- Tasso kit assembly: ~£32
- Fingerprick: at least £14 cheaper than Tasso
- Testosterone panel total (kit + postage + lab analysis): just under £60
- Clinical governance / doctor reporting: ~£8-9 per test (mandatory without CQC, removed once CQC obtained)
- Adding a biomarker to an existing panel (e.g. PSA): ~£8
- Separate kit for a single marker (e.g. PSA standalone): ~£56
- Conclusion: cheaper to add markers to one panel than run separate kits

### Biomarker constraints
- Magnesium, calcium, potassium: NOT viable for postal at-home testing. Biologically unstable in transit. Produces false elevated results, triggers clinical escalations, poor user experience. This is a biological limitation, not a Thriva limitation.
- Sophia suggested B vitamins and HbA1c as alternatives
- HbA1c: 3-month average blood sugar, relevant for weight loss and type 2 diabetes screening. Most people have poor levels. Could be a good supplement conversion marker.
- Vitamin B12: most people are deficient even when supplementing. Good supplement conversion pathway.
- Albumin: should be included in testosterone screening panel for proper TRT qualification

### Clinical governance
- Pre-CQC: Thriva's doctors review every result and write personalised reports. Mandatory. Not optional. Insurance and liability issue.
- Post-CQC: Andro Prime can bring clinical governance in-house, Thriva removes the £8-9 fee. They will advise on what the internal setup needs to look like.

- Three kit tiers: TRT screening, energy/recovery, full combined
- Go-to-market: affiliate programme (50-100 micro-influencers, 5k+ followers, fitness/wellness), Google and Facebook paid ads
