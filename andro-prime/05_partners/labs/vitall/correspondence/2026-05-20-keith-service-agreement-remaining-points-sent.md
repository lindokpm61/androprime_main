Hi Ben,

Thanks for sending the updated agreement through. It's close, and the earlier drafting gaps are sorted. I've been through it carefully and I'm ready to sign once we've cleared the points below. I've split them into quick corrections and a couple of more substantive ones so they're easy to work through.

**Quick corrections**

Energy & Metabolism panel, B12 assay. In the Schedule 1 pricing table, the Combo Test correctly lists Active B12, but the standalone Energy & Metabolism kit lists only Vitamin B12. You confirmed in writing on 30 April that our profile includes Active B12, and our results system is built specifically around Active B12 (HoloTC). Total serum B12 is a different test we can't use. Please correct the Energy kit line to Active B12 so the Schedule matches what we agreed.

Conflicting precedence clauses. The Order Form introduction says the Core Terms prevail over the Order Form, while Schedule 2 §7 says the Order Form prevails first. These contradict each other on which document wins in a dispute. Please align them into a single, consistent order-of-precedence clause.

**Two substantive points**

Data-protection roles (Order Form §6). When I raised this previously, I asked that we be set up as Controller (Andro Prime) and Processor (Vitall) for the testing data, with an Article 28 DPA. The Order Form instead makes both parties independent Data Controllers, with no processor DPA. I'm not against a controller-to-controller model if that's how Vitall operates, but I need to understand the rationale. We'll also need the data-protection wording clean and final, because our privacy documentation has to mirror it exactly. Can we agree the correct model and get the corresponding DPA or controller-to-controller terms finalised? This is probably best on a short call.

Liability cap and insurance (Clause 5; no insurance clause currently in the agreement). The cap has moved to £50,000 (£100,000 for data protection), and the indemnities sit under that cap. For a partnership handling special-category health data, that figure is still on the low side; a single data incident could exceed it. Two things: (a) can we revisit the cap, and (b) the agreement has no insurance provision at all. Please confirm in writing that Vitall maintains professional indemnity and product liability cover, and at what levels, and let's add a short insurance clause referencing it.

**Please also send over**

Sub-processor and laboratory list. The agreement authorises sub-processors by category but doesn't name them. Please provide the current list of laboratories and other sub-processors as a schedule. We need the labs named for our compliance file.

Approved customer-facing wording pack (Clause 3.14.9). Please send the baseline approved wording for sample types, turnaround times and result limitations so we can use it as-is on the site.

Turnaround confirmation (Clause 3.1(b)). As drafted, this reads as up to 48 hours to test plus 24 hours for results, on a reasonable-endeavours basis. Can you confirm the realistic typical end-to-end turnaround? We need our customer-facing claims to match your approved wording, so I want to set customer expectations correctly from day one.

Everything else looks good: pricing, the cancellation and rebooking schedule, and the exit and transition clause. Once the above are settled, I'll sign straight away. Happy to jump on a call this week if it's quicker for the data-protection and liability points.
