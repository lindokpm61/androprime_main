## Outreach Cadence

**Day 0:** Send all four Tier 1 cold emails on the same morning.
**Day 5:** If no reply from any of the four → send polite follow-up email.
**Day 10:** Escalate to phone call (numbers in shortlist) for any non-responsive Tier 1 contacts.
**Day 14:** If only one or two Tier 1 replies received → activate Tier 2 outreach.
**Day 21:** Decision point — do we have at least 2 viable manufacturers in active conversation?

> If yes: book calls with top 2, finalise commercial terms, decide. **Manufacturer locked by end of M1.**
> If no: rescope. Either accept higher MOQ (Tier 2), or split manufacturers (one for capsules, one for powder), or delay supplement launch by 4–6 weeks.

## Key Spec Points to Communicate (V7.2)

Every conversation must lead with these to avoid wasted time:

1. **Daily Stack is 4-ingredient capsule formulation** (Zinc Gluconate equivalent to 30mg elemental zinc, Vitamin D3 4,000 IU, Active B12 / Methylcobalamin 1,000mcg, Ashwagandha KSM-66 600mg)
2. **Joint & Recovery Collagen is 5-ingredient powder** (Hydrolysed Bovine Collagen 10g, UC-II 40mg, Vitamin C 80mg, MSM 500mg, Hyaluronic Acid 5mg)
3. **Initial Gate 0A volume:** 1,500 capsules + 750 sachets (or tub equivalent)
4. **Scaling target:** ~5,000 capsules/month + ~2,500 sachets/month by M6 if growth is on plan
5. **Open spec question:** Active B12 dose — 1,000mcg confirmed default. Asking manufacturer to quote 5,000mcg as alternative for upgrade decision.
6. **V7.2 stability testing requirement:** ~£750. New product from regulatory standpoint (V7.1 testing covered Magnesium, which is removed).

## Negotiation Levers (carry into every call)

Drawing from the lab partnership conversations and standard B2B supplement industry practice:

**Lever 1 — Volume commitment for low MOQ:**
"We can commit to X kits/month for first 6 months in exchange for low or no MOQ on first batch. Locks in your demand forecasting; lets us launch with manageable cash exposure."

**Lever 2 — Payment terms:**
"30-60 day net payment terms instead of prepay. You get your manufacturing cash; we get working capital cushion. Same total spend, better cash flow timing for both sides."

**Lever 3 — Co-marketing:**
"Andro Prime is a founder-visible brand with a compliance-led narrative. Happy to mention our manufacturing partner in PR if helpful."

**Lever 4 — Dual-product commitment:**
"We need both capsules (Daily Stack) AND powder (Collagen). One supplier handling both is preferable. If you can do both, we'd commit to both products even if your MOQ on one is slightly above our preference."

## Decision Criteria (after responses received)

Score each manufacturer on:

| Criterion | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| MOQ flexibility (can they do <5,000 first run?) | 25% | Hard requirement |
| Pricing per unit at 1,500 / 5,000 / 25,000 unit volumes | 20% | Need 3-tier quote |
| Certifications (GMP, BRC, NSF, MHRA) | 15% | BRC and MHRA prefered |
| Lead time (formulation → first batch delivered) | 10% | Target ≤6 weeks |
| Both capsule AND powder capability | 10% | Dual-product is preferred |
| Stability testing in-house or partner | 5% | Affects V7.2 testing cost |
| Payment terms flexibility | 5% | Net 30+ preferred |
| Communication quality on first contact | 5% | Subjective but important |
| Geographic proximity / UK base | 5% | Reduces logistics complexity |

**Top scorer wins.** Tie-breaker: BRC certification + UK base + dual-product capability.

## Risk Watch

- **Stability testing timing:** V7.2 stability testing takes 3-4 weeks. If we don't start it as part of manufacturer selection process, MOQ delivery could be delayed by 4 weeks waiting for testing.
- **MOQ inventory cash hit:** Even at favourable terms, 5,000-unit Daily Stack MOQ at ~£3/unit COGS = £15,000 cash hit. Must align with cash position at Gate 0A.
- **Single-source risk:** If we go with one manufacturer for both products, supply chain risk concentrates. Consider dual-source by Year 2.
- **Ashwagandha sourcing:** KSM-66 is a specific branded extract. Verify each manufacturer can source it specifically, not a generic ashwagandha extract. KSM-66 ≠ Sensoril ≠ generic.
