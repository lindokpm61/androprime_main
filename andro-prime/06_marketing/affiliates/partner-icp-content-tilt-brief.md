## Top 5 partner-vetting questions to answer ON THE PROFILE (kit-agnostic)

When a UK PT or micro-influencer clicks Keith's profile after the DM, they're running a 90-second silent vetting checklist. The pinned post + top 9 grid + bio must answer:

1. **"Is this a real person or a marketing department?"** — they want a face, a name, a why. If the bio is brand-voice-anonymous, they'll bin it. (Trigger: every "Take a hike with your bullshit" reaction in the partner ICPs.)
2. **"Is the product something a credible UK PT/creator would actually recommend, or is this another MyProtein-tier code-blaster?"** — needs visible product credibility (UKAS lab, GP-interpreted, named GP, proper pricing) within 2 scrolls. Needs to read for **both** Kit 1 audiences (A2) and Kit 2 audiences (A1) — the partner is silently asking "would the kit my audience needs be obvious from this profile?"
3. **"Will this brand pay me on time and not ghost me after 3 rounds of edits?"** — needs at least one post explicitly addressing "we pay on the 15th, no net-60, no chasing accounting." Build-in-public 20% bucket.
4. **"What is this brand's claim discipline? Will I get an ASA letter for posting their code?"** — needs visible compliance literacy (Keith mentions claim discipline, mentions Dr Ewa, mentions the "Your results indicate..." framing) without lecturing. Subtle. Critical for A2 candidates (testosterone-curious posts carry higher ASA exposure).
5. **"Who else has signed up? Do I want to be in this room?"** — flagship PT names, partner highlight, founding-member numbers (note: FM as paid deposit is shelved — see §7.5 of kit-strategy decision brief; FM may continue as a non-cash recognition tag, TBC). Social-proof grid should show **both** A1 and A2 partners once first wave is live.

If 3 out of 5 of these are unanswerable from the profile within 90 seconds, the DM goes nowhere.

**New under equal pacing:** The grid must show audience-fit signals for both tiers. If the top 9 grid is 100% recovery content, A2 candidates assume they're in the wrong room; if it's 100% testosterone content, A1 candidates assume the same. Aim for ~40% A1-coded, ~40% A2-coded, ~20% kit-agnostic build-in-public.

## Language to use (verbatim or near-verbatim)

Pulled from the ICP language banks. Mirror these phrases — UK PTs and creators recognise them as their own. **Reweighted for two-tier outreach:** A1 and A2 phrases tagged where the register splits.

**Kit-agnostic (use across both tiers):**

1. *"UKAS-accredited lab, GP-interpreted report"* (the four words that distinguish Andro Prime from Medichecks)
2. *"Your results indicate..."* (the compliant alternative to clinical statements)
3. *"Plain English, no jargon, no panic"* (the brand-voice differentiator vs lab-portal PDFs)
4. *"It makes my service better"* (PT-side motivation — say it out loud as why PTs join)
5. *"No obligation to post — honest feedback either way"* (creator-side trust signal)
6. *"We pay on the 15th of each month for the prior month. No net-60. No chasing accounting."* (the trust-buying line for both ICPs)
7. *"10% off — that's the partner code rate, doesn't go higher"* (the discount-honesty line)
8. *"Code stays live. We don't pull it on you."* (residual-income reassurance)
9. *"At-home finger-prick. 72 hours. Done."* (the experiential summary)
10. *"They don't just say 'normal' when stuff is out of range — they give you a doctor's summary in plain English."* (the recognised UK consumer differentiator — direct verbatim from r/AskUK SecretSquirrelSpot)

**A1 register (recovery / performance / Kit 2 audiences):**

1. *"Your programme isn't the problem. Your levels are."* (the recovery-side recruiting line)
2. *"Knackered all the time"* (UK PT vernacular for client-side fatigue complaint)
3. *"Sore for 3 days after a workout that used to take 1"* (recovery-tier symptom)
4. *"Sports hormone check"* (recognised UK consumer category — Reddit signal Theme 6)
5. *"50nmol is deficient in every other country besides the UK"* (Vit D / NHS-frame quote — Electronic-Yam-4054 r/VitaminD)

**A2 register (testosterone-curious / men's health / Kit 1 audiences):**

1. *"GP told me I was fine for 18 months. I wasn't."* (the founder-page hero line for A2)
2. *"My GP said I'm fine but I don't feel right"* (the symptom-recognition phrase)
3. *"NHS will tell you you're 'in range'. In range isn't the same as optimal."* (UK-specific framing — universal in this audience)
4. *"Borderline low — above the NHS threshold. Which was basically the end of the conversation."* (the r/trt verbatim line A2 PTs/creators recognise immediately)
5. *"Going private was the only route."* (NHS-frustration register, kit-agnostic but lands hardest in A2)

## Language / framing to AVOID (kit-agnostic — preserved verbatim from v1)

These trigger instant disqualify in the UK PT/creator mind. Almost all also overlap with compliance rules.

**Compliance-driven (also in `copy-content-context.md`):**

- "Diagnose," "diagnosis," "treat," "treatment," "cure" — hard ban
- "TRT now" / "TRT available" / any present-tense clinical claim — hard ban (Phase 0)
- "Ashwagandha" — never, ever, in any context — hard ban
- "15% off" / "20% off" / "25% off" / "biggest discount" / "exclusive deal" / "limited time" / "sale" / "half price" — hard ban (ASA exaggerated-savings rule, plus PT/creator credibility)
- "Clinically proven" without an RCT citation
- "You have low testosterone" — replace with "Your results indicate..."

**Tone / culture (binned by UK partners):**

- "Excited to launch," "thrilled to announce," "delighted to share" — corporate-press-release voice
- "Passive income," "financial freedom," "earn while you sleep" — guru voice
- "Limited spots remaining" / "first 25 only get..." (when not strictly true) — fake-urgency tells
- "Disrupt," "biohack" (as a verb), "optimise your hormones" — overclaimed and soft-clinical
- Bullet-point list reels with "5 signs of low T" — formulaic, ASA-adjacent
- Hashtag spam (>5 hashtags or "#fitness #health #motivation" stacks)
- "DM me my code" with no other context
- Transformation photos / before-and-after as primary visual format
- Anything that sounds American: "guys," "huge fan," "wellness journey," "$"-anywhere, "GP" → "doctor"
- "TRT-curious" / "TRT may be the answer" — even if technically compliant in Phase 0, signals to partners we'll cross the line later

**Equal-pacing-specific (new in v2):**

- Sequencing language that implies one tier is primary: "we're starting with recovery first" or "testosterone is where we're focused" — both signal the wrong half of the partner pool that they're a back-burner audience. Replace with "we promote both kits at equal pace because both audiences exist."
- Founding-member-as-paid-deposit framing — shelved per kit-strategy decision brief 2026-05-08 §7.5. If FM survives as a non-cash cohort tag (TBC), frame as recognition not fee. "Founding member, not subscriber" wording from v1 is on hold pending Keith's call.

The total grid stays 40% founder story / 40% domain expertise / 20% build-in-public. Inside the two 40% buckets, content splits 50/50 between A1 (Kit 2) and A2 (Kit 1) narratives. Build-in-public stays kit-agnostic.

## Founder-vetting questions partners now run (recalibrated)

Under equal pacing, the partner self-segments into A1 or A2 on the intake form *after* clicking through from Keith's profile. The profile therefore has to satisfy **both** the A1 and the A2 vetting checklist simultaneously, in the same 90 seconds. Practically:

- Within the top 9 grid: at least 3 A1-coded posts, at least 3 A2-coded posts, ~3 build-in-public.
- Within the bio: a single sentence that names both audiences without sequencing them. Draft: *"At-home blood tests, GP-interpreted in plain English. For men hitting training plateaus and men whose GP keeps telling them they're fine."*
- Within the pinned posts: two slots — one A1 founder reel, one A2 founder reel. If only one pin slot is available, rotate weekly.
- Within Stories Highlights: separate "Recovery (Kit 2)" and "Hormones (Kit 1)" highlights so audience-fit is obvious to scrolling partners. Plus the kit-agnostic "Partners" highlight (payment terms, brief reveal, equal-pacing post).

If a partner clicks through and sees only one narrative for 3 scrolls, half the partner pool walks. The grid composition is the single biggest equal-pacing risk on the founder page.
