## Section B — What you cannot say

Non-negotiable. The ASA, MHRA, and CAP enforce these. A breach by a PT is a breach by Andro Prime — the regulator does not draw the distinction. Three strikes and your code is permanently revoked. Some breaches result in immediate revocation (Section G).

**Words and phrases you must never use**
- "Diagnose", "diagnosis".
- "Treat", "treatment", "cure".
- "TRT is available now" or any framing implying a clinical-tier service is live.
- "You have low testosterone" — replace with "Your results indicate…"
- "Andro Prime cured my fatigue" or any equivalent cure claim.
- "Clinically proven" without a specific RCT citation.
- The name of any Daily Stack ingredient that is not on the approved-claims list in Section C — anywhere, ever (see Section C).
- "15% off", "20% off", "biggest discount available", "exclusive deal", "limited time", "half price" (see Section D).
- Any claim of personal experience if you haven't actually used the kit (standard PTs without free kits).

**Implications you must never make**
- That blood test results are diagnostic.
- That Andro Prime supplements treat any disease.
- That the kit replaces a GP consultation.
- That low results mean TRT is appropriate.
- That joining the founding-member list guarantees a future TRT prescription. (The founding-member list is a free, non-cash email opt-in. No payment is taken. It does not create a right to any future service.)

If you're unsure whether a phrase crosses the line, ask before you post. We'll always help. We won't help after the post is live.

## Section C — The silent-ingredient rule (absolute)

The Daily Stack contains an ingredient that has **no approved EFSA health claim**. Naming that ingredient, or making any statement about what it does, in any context — caption, story, voice note, DM, in-person client conversation, podcast, comment — is treated as a regulated health claim, even if you don't intend it as one.

**The rule:** You may only ever name or make claims about Daily Stack ingredients that appear on this approved-claims list, using only the approved wording:

| Ingredient | The only wording you may use |
|---|---|
| Zinc | "Contributes to the maintenance of normal testosterone levels" |
| Vitamin D3 | "Contributes to normal muscle function" |
| Active B12 (Methylcobalamin) | "Contributes to normal energy-yielding metabolism" / "Contributes to normal psychological function" |

> You may **not** name, list, or make any claim about any Daily Stack ingredient that is not in the table above — anywhere, ever. Doing so is a severe violation and results in immediate permanent revocation of your code. Zero tolerance.

**If a client asks "what's in the Daily Stack?"**
> Approved answer: *"Zinc, Vitamin D3, Active B12 (Methylcobalamin), and a few other ingredients. The full label is on the bottle and on andro-prime.com."*

**If a client asks about an ingredient not on the approved list**
> Approved answer: *"I'm not able to make claims about that ingredient because it doesn't have an approved EFSA health claim. The label has the full ingredient list."*

That answer is permitted because it makes no claim. Anything beyond it — what the ingredient does, what studies show, why we included it — is forbidden.

## Section D — The 10% discount language rule

The customer discount via your code is exactly 10%. Not approximately. Not "up to". Exactly 10%. The ASA polices exaggerated savings claims specifically — "biggest discount", "exclusive", or any percentage above 10% is a Section B violation and triggers strike-1 enforcement.

**You may say**
- "10% off."
- "Use my code, get 10% off."
- "It's £89.10 with my code (£99 RRP)." (use the price of the kit you're recommending)

**You may not say**
- Any percentage above 10% ("15% off", "20% off", "save 25%").
- "Biggest discount you'll find."
- "Exclusive deal" or "members only".
- "Limited time" (the discount is not time-limited).
- "Half price" or any equivalent inflation.
- "Sale" or any seasonal-promotion framing.

First violation: strike-1 written warning + post takedown within 48 hours. Persistent inflated discount claims after one warning = severe violation = permanent code revocation.

Every code-bearing PT is monitored — automated weekly content audit (Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok) plus manual review of top performers. The audit flags forbidden language, missing #ad/#gifted disclosure, and inflated discount claims.

### Three-strikes enforcement

| Strike | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | First flagged violation | Written email from Keith. Post removed within 48h. PT acknowledges by reply. Logged. |
| 2nd | Second flagged violation within 6 months | Code suspended 30 days. |
| 3rd | Third within 12 months, OR a severe violation | Code revoked permanently. |

### Severe violations (immediate permanent revocation)

- Naming, or making any claim about, a Daily Stack ingredient that is not on the Section C approved-claims list.
- "TRT is available now" or any equivalent.
- False medical diagnosis claim.
- False cure claim.
- Personal-experience claims without having tested the kit (standard PTs).
- Persistent inflated discount claims after one warning.

### If we receive a regulator query about your content

Within 4 hours: your code is paused, the offending post is taken down. Within 24 hours: incident logged. Within 48 hours: a written response is filed, in consultation with Dr Ewa Lindo if required. You're informed at every step.

### Where to ask before posting

If a phrasing feels close to the line, send it to keith@andro-prime.com or DM Keith before posting. Pre-clearance is always faster than enforcement.

> Compliance is shared between Andro Prime and you. Treat the Brief as a tool, not a constraint. The PTs who treat it as a tool are the ones whose codes outlast their first audit.
