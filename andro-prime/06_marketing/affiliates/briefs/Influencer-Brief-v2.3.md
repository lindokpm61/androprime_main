## Section B — What you cannot say

Non-negotiable. The ASA, MHRA, and CAP enforce these. A breach by you is a breach by Andro Prime. Three strikes and your code is permanently revoked; some breaches are immediate revocation (Section H).

**Words and phrases you must never use**
- "Diagnose", "diagnosis".
- "Treat", "treatment", "cure".
- "TRT is available now" or any framing implying a clinical-tier service is live.
- "You have low testosterone" — replace with "Your results indicate…"
- "Andro Prime cured my fatigue" or any equivalent cure claim.
- "Clinically proven" without a specific RCT citation.
- The name of any Daily Stack ingredient not on the Section C approved-claims list — anywhere, ever.
- "15% off", "20% off", "biggest discount available", "exclusive deal", "limited time", "half price" (Section D).
- Any claim of personal experience if you haven't actually used the kit.

**Implications you must never make**
- That blood test results are diagnostic.
- That Andro Prime supplements treat any disease.
- That the kit replaces a GP consultation.
- That low results mean TRT is appropriate.
- That joining the founding-member list guarantees a future TRT prescription. (The founding-member list is a free, non-cash email opt-in. No payment is taken; it creates no right to a future service.)

If you're unsure whether a phrasing crosses the line, ask before you post.

## Section C — The silent-ingredient rule (absolute)

The Daily Stack contains an ingredient that has **no approved EFSA health claim**. Naming that ingredient, or making any statement about what it does, in any context — caption, Reel voiceover, story, voice note, DM, podcast, comment thread — is treated as a regulated health claim, even if you don't intend it as one.

**The rule:** You may only ever name or make claims about Daily Stack ingredients on this approved-claims list, using only the approved wording:

| Ingredient | The only wording you may use |
|---|---|
| Zinc | "Contributes to the maintenance of normal testosterone levels" |
| Vitamin D3 | "Contributes to normal muscle function" |
| Active B12 (Methylcobalamin) | "Contributes to normal energy-yielding metabolism" / "Contributes to normal psychological function" |

> You may **not** name, list, or make any claim about any Daily Stack ingredient not in the table above — anywhere, ever. A single breach is a severe violation and results in immediate permanent revocation of your code. Zero tolerance.

**If a viewer asks "what's in the Daily Stack?"**
> *"Zinc, Vitamin D3, Active B12 (Methylcobalamin), and a few other ingredients. The full label is on the bottle and on andro-prime.com."*

**If asked about an ingredient not on the approved list**
> *"I'm not able to make claims about that ingredient because it doesn't have an approved EFSA health claim. The label has the full ingredient list."*

That answer is permitted because it makes no claim. Anything beyond it is forbidden.

## Section D — The 10% discount language rule

The customer discount via your code is exactly 10%. Not approximately. Not "up to". Exactly 10%.

**You may say:** "10% off." · "Use my code, get 10% off." · "It's £107.10 with my code (£119 RRP)." *(use the price of the kit you're recommending)*

**You may not say:** any percentage above 10% · "biggest discount you'll find" · "exclusive deal" / "members only" · "limited time" · "half price" · "sale" or seasonal-promotion framing.

First violation: strike-1 written warning + post takedown within 48 hours. Persistent inflated discount claims after one warning = severe violation = permanent revocation.

## Section E — ASA disclosure & authenticity rules

Influencer content is held to a higher disclosure bar than any other channel.

**Rule 1 — Visible disclosure on every post.** Use #ad or #gifted on every Andro Prime post, including Stories. It must be visible without expanding the caption. Do not edit it away later. On platforms without hashtags, put "[Sponsored by Andro Prime]" in the first sentence. Missing disclosure is a severe violation: first instance = strike-1 + takedown within 48h; editing the disclosure away after publication = immediate permanent revocation.

**Rule 2 — Actually use the kit before posting (free-kit creators).** If you received a free kit, take the test before posting about it. Posting promotional content without having taken the test = immediate permanent revocation. Honest neutral or critical reactions are explicitly permitted — and often more credible. What you cannot do is fake the experience.

**Rule 3 — No personal-experience pitch without the experience.** Didn't get a free kit and haven't bought one? Use the credibility-led framing in Section A.
