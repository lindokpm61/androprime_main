## 1. Why This Document Exists

Micro-influencers are the **secondary acquisition channel** in Phase 0 v2.3. PTs are the hero. Influencers are the awareness top-of-funnel that complements PT-driven conversion.

Master plan target: 8–12 active influencers at launch, 18–25 active by M3, 25+ by M6 — generating 15–25% of kit volume.

Influencers function differently from PTs:
- **One-shot exposure model:** A single Reel reaches their audience once with a spike in engagement. Compare to PTs who reinforce recommendation across weekly client touchpoints.
- **Awareness-led, not conversion-led:** Influencers create top-of-funnel demand. Many of their viewers don't convert immediately but do find Andro Prime later via direct or branded search.
- **Audience match is everything:** A 50,000-follower influencer with the wrong audience converts less than a 10,000-follower influencer with the right one.
- **Compliance friction is high:** Influencer content faces stricter ASA disclosure requirements (#ad/#gifted) and louder public scrutiny when claims slip.

## 2. Why Influencers (the structural case)

UK micro-influencers (10k–150k followers) are the right tier for Andro Prime because:

1. **Trust-to-reach ratio is highest at micro-tier.** A creator with 50k engaged followers produces materially more conversions than one with 500k less-engaged followers.
2. **Cost is right.** Free kit + £15/sale flat fee + 10% audience discount is a deal a micro-influencer takes seriously. Macro-influencers expect £5,000+ flat fees per post, which the model can't absorb.
3. **Audience trust at micro-tier mirrors the brand voice.** Education-led, plain-spoken, direct — the same voice as Keith and Andro Prime.
4. **Compliance is more manageable.** A 30-influencer cohort can be audited weekly. A 300-influencer programme cannot be effectively monitored by one founder.

## 3. Influencer Vetting Criteria

(Unchanged from v2.2.) Premium products require premium audience standards. At £99–£179 price points, audience match matters more than follower count.

### Hard requirements (auto-reject if missing)

- UK-based (geo-targeting matters for NHS messaging)
- Male audience ≥ 80% (verified via Modash, Heepsy, or manual scan of comments)
- Follower range: 10,000–150,000 (micro and mid-tier)
- Engagement rate ≥ 2% (likes + comments / followers, last 12 posts)
- Content category match: men's fitness, men's health, biohacking, recovery, longevity, performance, 40+ lifestyle
- Posts publicly ≥ 3× per week (last 30 days)
- No current sponsorship from direct competitors: Voy, MediChecks, Numan, Manual, Hims UK, Optimale, Balance My Hormones, MyProtein, Bulk
- Will sign written affiliate attestation before code issuance
- Proper #ad / #gifted disclosure on previous sponsored posts (ASA compliance demonstrated)

### Strong-fit signals (prioritise these)

- Has previously discussed: blood testing, biomarkers, hormones, recovery science, supplement education
- Audience comments demonstrate real engagement (questions, not just emojis)
- Education-led content style (explainers, breakdowns, science-led)
- Independent (not part of an MCN or talent agency demanding flat fees)
- Has worked with health/wellness brands before with positive outcomes
- Located in or content focused on London, Manchester, Birmingham, Bristol, Edinburgh (Andro Prime's first geographic concentrations)

### Auto-reject signals

- Bargain-led content style ("£10 deals," "code SAVE25" in every caption)
- Pseudo-clinical claims (already a regulatory red flag)
- Audience female-skewed or under-35
- Hard-sell content (transformation photos, before/after-centric)
- Has posted positively about TRT, peptides, or steroids
- Audience size <5,000 (too small to justify free kit) or >150,000 (macro tier — different commercial conversation)
- Recently called out for ASA violation

### Vetting process per applicant (~10 min each)

1. Scan top 12 most recent posts for content quality and claim style
2. Check engagement rate via tooling (Modash, Heepsy) or manual sample
3. Audience inspection: top engagers male UK 35–55?
4. Check for #ad/#gifted disclosure on prior sponsored content
5. Run ICP fit check: would Keith trust this creator's recommendation if he were a customer?
6. Decision: accept, reject, or hold for batch review

## 4. Recruitment

### Target volume

- **Pre-launch (Weeks -8 to -3):** 100–120 cold DMs sent. Target 25–30 confirmed creators (free kit recipients).
- **First month post-launch:** Continue to ~30 DMs/week. Target additional 8–12 active by M3.
- **M3+:** Steady 15 DMs/week. Replace churned creators, recruit emerging voices.

### Sourcing channels

- **Instagram search:** `#menshealthuk`, `#mensfitnessuk`, `#bloodtestuk`, `#biohackinguk`, `#over40fitness`, `#recoveryuk`, `#strengthuk`, `#testosteroneawareness`
- **YouTube:** search "testosterone UK," "men's health blood test," "vitamin D men UK," "recovery science men" — filter to UK creators with 5,000+ subs
- **Tools:** Modash, Heepsy, Phlanx (paid; nice-to-have, not required)
- **Manual prospecting:** scroll Instagram health/fitness hashtags, save promising profiles weekly
- **Reverse-search:** check tagged accounts on competitor brand posts (Voy, Numan, MediChecks) — find creators they HAVEN'T contracted yet

### Equal-pacing framing (v2.3)

Under Option 4 (kit-strategy decision 2026-05-08), Kit 1 (testosterone) and Kit 2 (energy/recovery) share the same architecture (test → result → Daily Stack subscription) and are promoted at equal pace. Influencer outreach reflects this: creators self-select based on audience fit.

- **Kit 2 / energy-recovery audience match:** lifters, strength-focused PTs, recovery science creators, biohackers — the "tired all the time / I don't recover like I used to" register.
- **Kit 1 / testosterone audience match:** men's health 35–55 creators, longevity creators, the "GP fobbed me off" register.

Most micro-influencers in this niche cover both. The DM template below leads with whichever kit fits the creator's content best, but the offer applies across the range.

### Outreach DM template — Instagram (v2.3)

> Hi [Name] — I've been watching your content for a while. Your post on [specific topic, last 14 days] was exactly the kind of thing my audience wishes more brands talked about.
>
> Quick context: I'm Keith, founder of Andro Prime. We do at-home blood tests for UK men 35–55 — vitamin D, Active B12, CRP, ferritin, testosterone (depending on which kit). Each comes back with a plain-English personalised report from a GP (Dr Ewa Lindo, Harley Street TRT-trained). Kit 1 is £99, Kit 2 is £119, Kit 3 is £179. We're positioned above the lab-portal brands but below private clinics.
>
> I'd love to send you one of our kits completely free — no obligation. Test it. Share what you learned, or don't. If you do post and your audience is interested, your code gives them 10% off and you earn £15 per referral plus bonuses (Kit 3 upsell £10, supplement conversion £10). Honest feedback either way is just as valuable.
>
> Interested?

(Pick the kit that matches the creator's content. Energy/recovery creators get Kit 2. Testosterone/men's-health creators get Kit 1. Premium-positioning creators get Kit 3.)

### Outreach DM template — YouTube/email

Same content as Instagram but signed:
> *"Keith Antony, Founder, Andro Prime"* + 60-second Loom video link.

### Initial qualifying questions (in second message)

1. What does your audience demographic look like — typical viewer age and gender?
2. Have you covered blood testing, biomarkers, or supplementation on your channel before?
3. What price points have your audience typically converted on for health/wellness products?
4. Are you currently in any exclusive partnerships that would conflict?

If answers are good fit, send the kit. If not, send a polite "no thanks, but appreciate the response" and tag the contact as "future considered" in the tracker.

### Free kit allocation

**First 25 confirmed influencers (Weeks -8 to -3) get a free Kit 2 (£63 COGS).** (Unchanged in v2.3.)

After Week -3: new influencers onboard with the affiliate code only — no free kit. They can buy a kit using their own code (10% off, £15 fee paid back to themselves) if they want product experience.

This mirrors the PT first-wave / standard cohort distinction. Same cost-control logic.

## 5. Onboarding

(Unchanged from v2.2.)

**Goal:** Free kit dispatched and code live within 7 days of confirmation.

### Onboarding flow

**Step 1: Confirmation email (Day 0)**
- Welcome message (60 words from Keith)
- Brief PDF link
- Free kit dispatch notification (if first-25)
- Calendar link for optional 15-min Zoom (most influencers don't take it; offered anyway)

**Step 2: Brief PDF**

Same brief as PTs (Section 6 of `pt-programme.md` v2.3) — but with influencer-specific clauses:
- ASA #ad/#gifted disclosure requirement
- Authentic experience requirement (must actually use the kit before posting)
- Photo/video usage rights
- Compliance audit notification

**Step 3: 15-minute Zoom (optional)**

If creator opts in:
- 5 min walk through brand, the bet
- 5 min walk through brief — claim restrictions, Ashwagandha rule, discount language
- 5 min Q&A

If creator declines: send a 7-min Loom recording covering same content. Require acknowledgment of receipt before code activation.

**Step 4: Compliance attestation signed via DocuSign**

**Step 5: Code activated in FirstPromoter**

**Step 6: First-content nudge (Day 14)**

If no content posted by Day 14, automated email:
> *"Hey [Name] — just checking in. Did the kit arrive? Any questions on the brief? No pressure to post — only if it feels right and your audience would care. Either way, happy to chat if helpful."*

If still no content by Day 30: code stays live but creator moves to "monitoring" status. No re-engagement push beyond Day 30.

## 6. Content Guidelines

(Unchanged from v2.2.)

### What influencers can post (ASA-compliant + Section A of brief)

**Personal kit experience format (Reel, 30–60 sec):**
> *"Just got my Andro Prime results back. [Visual: holding kit packaging or showing results dashboard]. Tested vitamin D, Active B12, CRP, ferritin. My [specific marker] was lower than I expected. Got a plain-English explanation from their GP and a specific supplement recommendation based on my numbers. £119 (£107 with my code [CODE]). UK-based, UKAS-accredited lab. Code in bio. #ad"*

**Story sequence (3–5 frames):**
1. Frame 1: Kit unboxing — "Just got this in the post"
2. Frame 2: Sample collection — "Took 5 minutes"
3. Frame 3: Posting the sample — "Lab in 48 hours" <!-- SLA note: 48h locked 2026-05-12 pending written confirmation from Vitall. If Vitall confirms 72h, revisit and resweep this file + pt-programme.md + partner-icp-content-tilt-brief.md. -->
4. Frame 4: Results dashboard reveal — "My results"
5. Frame 5: CTA — "Code [CODE] for 10% off, link in bio"

**YouTube long-form (8–15 min):**
- Walkthrough format: kit → sample → results → interpretation → recommendation
- Honest tone: include any concerns or caveats
- End with code + 10% off mention + Andro Prime link in description

**Caption template (Instagram post or YouTube description):**

Same as PT caption template (`pt-programme.md` v2.3 Section 6 — Asset Pack), with `#ad` or `#gifted` mandatory.

### What influencers cannot post (Section B of brief — same as PT)

- "Diagnose," "diagnosis," "treat," "treatment," "cure"
- "TRT is available now" or any equivalent
- "You have low testosterone" — replace with "Your results indicate..."
- "Andro Prime cured my fatigue" — any cure claim
- "Clinically proven" without RCT citation
- **"Ashwagandha" — anywhere, ever**
- **"15% off," "20% off," etc. — exact 10% only**
- "Limited time," "exclusive deal," "biggest discount" — exaggerated savings claims

### ASA-specific requirements (influencer-only)

- **#ad or #gifted on every Andro Prime post** — even Stories. ASA explicitly requires this.
- **Disclosure must be visible without expanding** — not buried in long captions
- **No editing the disclosure away** in subsequent edits to the post
- **If posting on a platform that doesn't support hashtags,** disclosure must be in the first sentence: "[Sponsored by Andro Prime]" or equivalent

### Authentic-experience requirement

For influencers who received a free kit:
- Must actually use the kit before posting about it
- Must post genuine reaction (positive, neutral, or critical — all permitted)
- Posting promotional content without having used the kit is a Section B violation

For influencers who did not receive a free kit (post Week -3):
- Cannot claim personal experience
- Must use credibility-led framing only ("I've vetted this for my audience")
- Same as standard-PT framing in `pt-programme.md` v2.3 Section 6

## 7. Activation Mechanics (v2.3)

Industry default activation rate for influencer programmes: 30–40% (lower than PTs because the "post once" model has higher friction). v2.2 leaned on a £10 first-month activation bonus to push this; v2.3 removes the bonus and re-baselines target to 35–40% within 30 days, supported by non-cash levers (priority newsletter feature for first-content influencers, named spotlight in Andro Prime social channels).

### Per-sale bonus stack (v2.3 — same as PT, cross-reference `commission-structure.md` v2.3)

| Bonus | Trigger | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Kit 3 upsell bonus | Influencer code used on Kit 3 purchase | +£10 |
| Supplement conversion bonus | Customer subscribes within 60 days | +£10 |

(v2.2 included a £10 first-month activation bonus, removed in v2.3.)

### No tier system (PT-only)

Unlike PTs, influencers don't have Bronze/Silver/Gold tiers and **don't earn tier graduation bonuses.** Influencer model is one-shot — not the repeated-touchpoint model that justifies tier mechanics. (This is unchanged from v2.2 — PTs are the only cohort with tier-linked compensation.)

Instead, influencer scaling logic is:
- Top 5 monthly performers get featured spotlight in Andro Prime social channels (if consented)
- Top quarterly performer gets co-branded content opportunity with Keith (podcast, LinkedIn)
- Best-performing influencer M6 gets reserved early-access TRT slot

### Contest mechanics (v2.3 — quarterly only)

v2.3 removes monthly contests across the whole affiliate stack. Influencer contests are now quarterly only.

| Prize | Eligibility | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| (Influencer-specific quarterly contest) | Harmonised into the unified PT/influencer quarterly pool — see `commission-structure.md` v2.3 §5 | n/a (no separate line) |

(v2.2 ran both a monthly £150 prize and a quarterly £400. Per Keith 2026-05-08, the **influencer £400 quarterly is harmonised into the unified PT/influencer quarterly contest pool** — top quarterly performer £750 + top newcomer £250 — applying across both PTs and influencers (best 90-day performer wins regardless of cohort). The monthly prize is dropped in v2.3 in line with the affiliate-wide right-sizing. Recognition for monthly top performers is now non-cash — newsletter feature + social spotlight — see scaling logic above.)

## 8. Compliance Moderation

(Unchanged from v2.2.)

### Pre-issuance: signed attestation

Mandatory before code goes live (same as PT — Section 6 of `pt-programme.md` v2.3).

### Weekly content audit

Same automated script as PT audit (`pt-programme.md` v2.3 Section 8.2). Scrapes Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn for handle mentions + flagged terms. Flags:
- "Ashwagandha"
- "TRT," "diagnose," "diagnosis," "treat," "cure"
- "Low testosterone" without "may indicate"
- Discount language above 10% ("15% off," "20% off," "save 25%," "exclusive deal," "limited time," "biggest discount")
- **Missing #ad/#gifted disclosure** (specific to influencer compliance)

Manual review: spot-check 5 random influencer posts/Stories per week beyond automated flags.

### Three-strikes enforcement

Same structure as PT (`pt-programme.md` v2.3 Section 8.3). 1st = written warning + post takedown within 48 hours. 2nd = code suspended 30 days. 3rd = permanent revocation.

**Influencer-specific severe violations (immediate permanent revocation):**
- Posting promotional content without #ad/#gifted disclosure
- Posting without having actually used the kit (claiming personal experience falsely)
- Editing a post after publication to remove the disclosure

### Kill switch

Same protocol as PT (`pt-programme.md` v2.3 Section 8.4). Within 4 hours of any ASA query: pause code, request post takedown, log incident.

## 11. Critical Counterpoints (sparring with this plan)

1. **"Influencer activation will be 35–40% without a first-month bonus."** v2.2 leaned on a £10 first-month bonus to push toward 40%+. v2.3 removes it and bets that priority feature in newsletter + named social spotlight + a clean compensation story is enough. If activation drops below 25% at M2, reintroduce a non-cash activation lever first (faster onboarding turnaround, dedicated WhatsApp Q&A line); reintroduce cash only if non-cash levers don't move it.

2. **"Free kit cohort closes at Week -3."** This may strand promising late-finds. Reserve 5-kit emergency allocation for high-fit creators discovered Weeks -3 to launch — Keith's discretion to grant case-by-case.

3. **"Audience match matters more than follower count."** True but hard to enforce without good tooling. Modash and Heepsy are imperfect. Manual scan of top engagers is slow but most reliable.

4. **"Influencers will follow disclosure rules."** Many won't reflexively. Audit aggressively. Strike-1 the first missed #ad. By second cohort wave (M2+), this should be muscle memory.

5. **"Compliance system scales to 50 influencers."** Possibly. At 100+, need VA. Use same tooling and staffing model as PT compliance audit.

6. **"YouTube content delivers higher LTV than Instagram."** Untested but likely. YouTube long-form (8–15 min walkthrough) is more conviction-building than a 30-second Reel. **Decision (Keith 2026-05-08): YouTube long-form creators do NOT receive flagship-tier per-piece content payments — affordability constraint.** YouTube creators receive the same standard influencer treatment as Instagram creators (£15 base + bonus stack + free Kit 2 if first-25 cohort). If conviction-building thesis holds at M3, revisit at v3 — but no per-piece-content programme for YouTube in Phase 0.

7. **"Premium pricing reduces influencer audience conversion."** Probably. Watch conversion rate from influencer-coded landing pages — if below 1.5%, consider topic-specific landing pages per influencer (e.g., recovery-focused for fitness creators, energy-focused for biohackers).

8. **"Equal-pacing Kit 1 + Kit 2 dilutes the influencer pitch."** Possible. Test M1–M2 — if creators consistently default to one kit despite the equal-pacing framing, follow the data and adjust the DM template. The architecture supports both; the audience picks.
