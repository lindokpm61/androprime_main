## 1. Why PTs (the structural case at premium pricing)

A vetted UK personal trainer is the highest-leverage referrer at £99–£179 price points:

1. **Premium products need credibility transfer, not discount transfer.** A PT recommendation carries the social weight of a coach to a client.
2. **Permission to discuss the symptoms.** A PT regularly hears "I'm shattered, my recovery's gone, I'm not sleeping right."
3. **Repeated exposure.** A PT sees a client weekly. A single recommendation gets reinforced.
4. **The product makes their service better.** A PT who knows their client's vitamin D, B12, ferritin, and CRP can write smarter training plans.
5. **Uncontested channel.** No direct competitor runs a structured UK PT affiliate programme.

**The economic case at premium pricing with hybrid model:**

| Per-kit unit economics (with 10% discount via PT code) | Kit 1 (£89.10) | Kit 2 (£107.10) | Kit 3 (£161.10) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Customer pays | £89.10 | £107.10 | £161.10 |
| Cost of 10% discount | £9.90 | £11.90 | £17.90 |
| PT base fee | £15 | £15 | £15 |
| PT Kit 3 bonus | — | — | +£10 |
| Stripe (2.5%) | £2.23 | £2.68 | £4.03 |
| COGS | £58.50 | £63.00 | £98.00 |
| **Net per kit (no supplement bonus paid)** | **£13.37** | **£26.42** | **£34.07** |
| Net per kit (with supplement subscriber within 60d) | £3.37 | £16.42 | £24.07 |

50 active PTs × 1.0 kits/month × £25 blended net = £1,250/month gross profit on kits alone. Plus supplement conversion, plus founding member deposits.

## 2. PT Vetting Criteria

(Unchanged from v2.1 — full criteria preserved.)

### Hard requirements (auto-reject if missing)

- UK-based
- Currently active as a PT
- Active client base ≥ 15 1-to-1 clients
- Posts publicly on Instagram or LinkedIn ≥ 2× per week

- Will complete onboarding (live call or async video walkthrough)

### Strong-fit signals (prioritise)

- Specialises in 35+ men, body recomp, sports performance, recovery, rehab
- Content is education-led
- Audience engagement rate > 2%
- Has previously mentioned blood testing, biomarkers, recovery science, supplementation
- Independent (freelance or small studio)
- 3+ years experience
- **Premium signal:** has previously sold or recommended high-ticket items to clients

### Auto-reject signals

- Heavy unbranded supplement stack promotion
- Pseudo-clinical claims
- Audience skewed female or under-35
- Hard-sell content style
- Posts about TRT, peptides, steroids in any positive context
- Recently called out for ASA violation
- Audience size <1,500 or >250,000
- **Premium reject:** content is bargain-led ("£10/session," constant discount codes)

### Vetting process per applicant

1. Spot-check 10 most recent posts
2. Profile check: bio, location, partner mentions
3. Audience inspection: top engagers male UK 35–55?
4. DM screening: 3 qualifying questions
5. Decision: accept, reject, or hold

Vetting time per applicant: 8–12 minutes.

## 5. The PT Brief (compliance-critical)

Every PT signs and acknowledges this before code issuance.

### Section A: What you can say

**Permitted personal experience claims (free-kit PTs):**
- "I tested my [vitamin D / energy markers / hormone markers] with Andro Prime."
- "Got my results in 3 days — my [specific marker] was lower than I expected."
- "It told me exactly what to do — [Daily Stack / nothing / GP letter for ferritin]."

**Permitted credibility-led framing (standard PTs without free kit):**
- "I've vetted Andro Prime — UKAS-accredited lab, GP interpretation, GMC-registered prescriber. I'd trust the result."
- "I've checked the protocols and I'm comfortable recommending it to clients."
- "If you've been wondering, this is your nudge."

**Permitted recommendation framing:**
- "If you're knackered all the time, recovering slowly, or your GP said 'normal' but you don't feel right — this is the test."
- "PTs can use the results to write a smarter plan."
- **"Use my code [PTNAME15] at checkout — 10% off."**

**Permitted product description:**
- "At-home blood test from a UKAS-accredited lab."
- "Results in 72 hours. Plain-English explanation."
- "Specific supplement recommendation based on your actual results."
- "GP-led — Dr Ewa Lindo signs off all the report copy."

**Premium-pricing language (use openly):**
- "It's £119 (£107 with my code). I think it's worth it."
- "Not the cheapest at-home test — and not trying to be."
- "A private clinic consultation alone is £200+. This is the test, the lab, and the interpretation for £119."
- "MediChecks gives you a number. Andro Prime gives you an answer."

**Permitted CTA:**
- "Use my code [PTNAME15] at andro-prime.com — 10% off"
- "DM me my code"
- "If you'd been wondering, this is your nudge."

### Section B: What you cannot say

**Never use these words/phrases:**
- "Diagnose," "diagnosis"
- "Treat," "treatment," "cure"
- "TRT is available now" or any framing implying clinical tier is live
- "You have low testosterone" — replace with "Your results indicate..."
- "Andro Prime cured my fatigue" or any equivalent
- "Clinically proven" without specific RCT citation
- "Ashwagandha" — anywhere, ever
- **"15% off," "20% off," "biggest discount available," "exclusive deal," or any framing that exaggerates the actual 10% discount**
- **Any claim that you've personally tested the kit if you haven't (standard PTs without free kits — DO NOT pretend personal experience)**

**Never make these implications:**
- That blood test results are diagnostic
- That Andro Prime supplements treat any disease
- That the kit replaces a GP consultation
- That low results = TRT is appropriate
- That the founding member deposit guarantees a TRT prescription

### Section C: The Ashwagandha rule (absolute)

Daily Stack contains 600mg Ashwagandha KSM-66. No approved EFSA health claim. **You may not mention ashwagandha by name in any context, ever.**

If a client asks "what's in the Daily Stack?" — answer: "Zinc, vitamin D3, Active B12 (Methylcobalamin), and a few other ingredients. Full label is on the bottle and on andro-prime.com."

If a client asks specifically about ashwagandha — answer: "I'm not allowed to make claims about ashwagandha because it doesn't have an approved EFSA health claim. Label has the full ingredient list."

**Severity:** First mention = permanent code revocation. Zero tolerance.

### Section D: The discount language rule (v2.2 — new)

The customer discount via your code is **exactly 10%, no more.** You may say:

- "10% off"
- "Use my code, get 10% off"
- "It's £107 with my code (£119 RRP)"

**You may not say:**

- Any percentage above 10% (15%, 20%, 25% — all forbidden)
- "Biggest discount you'll find"
- "Exclusive deal" or "members only"
- "Limited time" (the discount is not time-limited)
- "Half price" or any equivalent inflation
- "Sale" or any seasonal-promotion framing

**Why:** ASA polices "exaggerated savings claims" specifically. A PT saying "20% off" when it's 10% is a Section B violation. A PT saying "biggest discount" when it isn't is also a violation.

**Severity:** First violation = strike-1 written warning + post takedown within 48 hours.

If still zero sales at 90 days: code stays live; PT moves to quarterly contact cadence.

## 7. Compliance Moderation System

### 7.1 Pre-issuance: written attestation (Section 5)

Mandatory before code goes live.

### 7.2 Weekly content audit

**Automated layer:** Python script (or Zapier) runs weekly:
1. Pulls Instagram posts/Stories tagged with @andro.prime or any registered PT code
2. Scrapes captions for forbidden words: "ashwagandha," "TRT," "diagnose," "diagnosis," "treat," "cure," "low testosterone" (without "may indicate"), **"15% off," "20% off," "25% off," "30% off," "biggest discount," "exclusive deal," "limited time," "half price"**
3. Outputs flagged list to Google Sheet

**Manual layer:** Keith reviews flagged list (~30 min/week at 50 PT scale). Spot-check 5 random Top-tier PT posts/Stories.

**At 100+ PT scale (M3+):**
- VA for tier-1 review (4 hours/week, ~£60/week)
- Keith reviews escalated cases only
- Total Keith time: ≤ 1 hour/week

### 7.3 Three-strikes enforcement

| Strike | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | First flagged violation | Written email from Keith. Post removed within 48h. PT acknowledges by reply. Logged. |
| 2nd | Second flagged violation within 6 months | Code suspended 30 days. |
| 3rd | Third within 12 months OR severe violation | Code revoked permanently. |

**Severe violations (zero tolerance, immediate permanent revocation):**
- Ashwagandha mentioned by name
- "TRT is available now" or any equivalent
- False medical diagnosis claim
- False cure claim
- **Persistent inflated discount claims after one warning** (single violation is strike-1; pattern of repeat violations after warning is severe)

### 7.4 Kill switch protocol

If Andro Prime receives:
- ASA query
- DM from CAP, MHRA, or another regulator
- Consumer complaint citing a PT's claim
- Journalist enquiry about a PT's content

Protocol:
1. **Within 4 hours:** Pause the PT's code. Take down the offending post.
2. **Within 24 hours:** Internal incident log filed in `03_compliance/content-approval/incident-[date].md`.
3. **Within 48 hours:** Written response to regulator/complainant if required, in consultation with Ewa.
4. **Within 7 days:** Post-mortem note added to Section 7.5.

(Empty at launch. Each incident adds a line.)

## 10. Critical Counterpoints (sparring with this plan)

1. **"PT activation will be 65% at premium pricing."** Industry data is thin. Could be 40%. If so, volume targets need 1.5x more onboarded PTs. Operationally manageable.

2. **"PTs will follow the brief."** The "exact 10% only" rule is harder to enforce than the Ashwagandha rule because "save 15%" is muscle memory. Audit script flags it; first violation is strike-1.

3. **"Standard PTs without free kits will perform 70-80% as well as free-kit PTs."** Untested assumption. The hybrid model bets that the 10% discount substitutes adequately for personal-experience pitch. Watch the free-kit vs standard PT comparison from M2 onward — if standard PTs underperform by >40%, the model needs revision.

4. **"Vitall can fulfil 100–150/month."** Untested. Confirm Week -8.

5. **"PT clients will accept £107 (£119 with 10% off) without further haggling."** Some will. Some won't. Premium-pricing FAQ in Section 5 gives PTs scripts.

6. **"The £15 flat fee + bonus stack + tier retention will be enough motivation."** Possibly insufficient for top performers. Watch defection. Be ready to add custom flagship-style deals for any sub-flagship PT doing 30+ kits/month.

7. **"Bonus stack won't get gamed."** Watch patterns: PT with 10 sales all to friends/family (low-conversion supplements), PT hoarding sales to hit Silver in single push.

8. **"Keith can run this solo through Week 2."** 1,500 cold DMs + 50 onboarding calls + audits + LinkedIn = 50+ hours/week. **VA hire should be Week 2, not Week 6.** Budget for it.

9. **"Cash flow timing is sustainable."** Tighter than v2.1 was. Bonus payouts accumulate from M1; supplement MRR doesn't ramp until M5. Cash floor mechanism (Section 6 of master plan) defers retention bonuses if monthly cash drops below £3,000.
