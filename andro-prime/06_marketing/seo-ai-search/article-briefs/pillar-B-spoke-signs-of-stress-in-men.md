## 2. The article's job (one sentence)

Help a stressed, wired 35–50yo man recognise the **physical** signals his body is under load, understand in
plain English what's happening (cortisol / fight-or-flight), give him levers to lower the load this week,
and draw the honest line for when it's more than everyday stress — without diagnosing, treating, or drifting
into mental-health territory.

## 3. Target reader

Men 35–50, running hot — work pressure, poor sleep, "wired but tired", noticing body symptoms (gut, chest
tightness, tension, skin, energy) and wondering if stress is behind them. Searched in plain English. Wants
practical, non-preachy, non-medical answers and a no-panic signal for when to act.

## 4. Search-intent decoded

Informational, "is this stress / what's it doing to me / what do I do" intent. They want: (a) recognition
("yes, that's stress, not something scarier"), (b) the body mechanism in plain terms, (c) concrete levers,
(d) a clear boundary for when to see someone. NOT looking to buy — so no product pitch; newsletter is the
earned next step.

## 9. Section-by-section content brief

- **What stress does:** cortisol + sympathetic "fight-or-flight" explained simply; acute vs chronic load.
  Behavioural/physiological, NO thresholds, NO "you have high cortisol", no claims.
- **Physical signs (core):** one tight block per symptom — what it feels like, why stress causes it
  (plain mechanism), reassurance it's common. Gut section anchors the `stomach cramps when stressed` win.
  **Chest section MUST open with the red-flag** (see §16) before any "it's often stress" reassurance.
- **What to lower the load this week:** 4–6 concrete behavioural levers (sleep window, caffeine/alcohol
  trim, daily walk / breathing down-regulation, boundaries on work, daylight + movement). Shareable core.
  Behaviour only — no supplement efficacy, **no ashwagandha**, no "adaptogens".
- **When it's more than stress (boundary, Ewa sight):** see your GP if stress is persistent, affecting daily
  life/relationships/work, with low mood that won't lift, or physical symptoms that don't settle — and the
  hard line: **sudden/severe chest pain, or chest pain with breathlessness/sweating → urgent care / 999**,
  don't wait. Signpost NHS / Mind for the mental-health side rather than addressing it here.
- **Where a check fits:** soft, honest, kit-less — "if the load's been high for a long time, a check can
  show things stress touches over time (the kind a GP looks at); we'll add men's-health checks like this as
  we launch — join the list to be first." Non-diagnostic, no product on sale, routes to newsletter. Mention
  cortisol/testosterone only as "things stress can affect," never as a result or a buyable test.
