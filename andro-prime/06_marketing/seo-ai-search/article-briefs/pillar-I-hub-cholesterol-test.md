## 1. Why this article ships

`cholesterol test` at 9,900 vol / **DFS KD 26** (Semrush had 48) is a high-intent cardiometabolic entry point
with no current Andro Prime pillar. The strategy: capture the cholesterol-test volume, then **out-position the
whole SERP on ApoB** — the marker NHS lipid panels and most private tests don't report, which better predicts
cardiovascular risk than LDL. That's the on-brand "better than the standard test / optimisation" wedge, and the
ApoB SERP (`apob test`, KD 13) is wide open (US clinical + lab product pages, zero UK men-specialist editorial).
Seeds authority + email capture ahead of the Kit 3 Plus launch.

## 2. The article's job (one sentence)

Turn the man who Googled "cholesterol test" — because a GP printout flagged his cholesterol, a panel returned
numbers he can't interpret, or he's heart-health-aware and wants a proper baseline — into a **waitlist signup**
(and, on launch, a Kit 3 Plus buyer) by giving him the most useful UK men-specific lipid explainer on the SERP,
introducing ApoB as the number he's been missing, and earning AI citations.

## 3. Target reader

**ICP 2 (active 35–50) — primary.** Landed here because: a GP printout flagged total/LDL cholesterol; a private
panel returned a lipid profile he doesn't understand; or he's read that "cholesterol is more complicated than one
number" and wants the proper version. **ICP 3 (preventative 40+) — secondary:** family history of heart disease,
running a comprehensive screen, wants the full lipid + ApoB picture before booking.

He is NOT: a clinician; a patient with diagnosed cardiovascular disease or on statins seeking dosing advice
(compliance — route to GP); a worried-well googler convinced one high LDL means a heart attack is imminent
(defuse — Section 16).

## 4. Search-intent decoded

`cholesterol test` reads commercial-with-informational intent. The reader wants to understand his numbers and
often to test/retest. The article must: answer the literal question (what a cholesterol test measures — total,
LDL, HDL, triglycerides, non-HDL — and what's healthy) in the opening block; **introduce ApoB as the better
risk marker** (the differentiator that reframes the whole topic); resolve the job (interpret numbers, decide
lifestyle vs GP, decide retest); earn the waitlist signup via baseline→retest logic.

## 9. Section-by-section content brief

- **What a cholesterol test measures** — total/LDL/HDL/triglycerides/non-HDL in plain English. Concrete opener.
- **What counts as "healthy" — the UK targets** — most-cited section. UK lipid targets each sourced (NHS / HEART
  UK). Note "targets vary by personal CV risk" + the population-band-vs-optimum point (link Pillar C
  `myth-of-normal-range`).
- **ApoB — the number your standard cholesterol test misses** — the hero. ApoB counts the actual number of
  atherogenic particles; predicts CV risk better than LDL-C, especially when triglycerides are high or LDL looks
  "normal". Cite the evidence (Sniderman et al.; Harvard Health). Explain plainly why NHS panels don't include
  it (cost/policy, not lack of value). **This is the differentiator no UK competitor leads with.**
- **The wider cardiometabolic picture (blood sugar, briefly)** — name HbA1c/fasting glucose as the blood-sugar
  half of cardiometabolic health, link to a future blood-sugar spoke. **Do NOT pivot into the diabetes SERP** or
  make diabetes claims — one paragraph, link out.
- **What raised numbers usually mean in active men** — diet pattern, body composition, alcohol, genetics
  (familial hypercholesterolaemia — name it, route to GP), low activity. Honest, no shaming, no statin advice.
- **What a single result does NOT tell you** — defuse; lipids fluctuate; one high LDL isn't a diagnosis; CV risk
  is a pattern (lipids + ApoB + BP + family history), not one number.
- **Baseline → retest** — the loop in lipid terms (retest 8–12 weeks after a diet/activity change). Link Pillar F.
- **When to see your GP, not us** — non-negotiable. Very high total/LDL (possible FH), known heart disease,
  chest pain, family history of early heart disease → GP. Enabling framing.
- **How Andro Prime will measure this — waitlist** — a lipid + ApoB panel is on the roadmap (Kit 3 Plus); join
  the waitlist; no fake "buy now". Emphasise **ApoB included** as the differentiator vs standard tests.
