## 9. Section-by-section content brief

### What a B12 blood test actually measures

- Open with the document/body-feel cue (the panel number, the GP mention, the fatigue/brain-fog that sent him looking).
- Plain-English: B12 is a vitamin your body can't make, gets from animal foods (note the relevance for lower-meat diets, factually, no preaching), and needs for energy metabolism, nerve function and red-cell production. Frame with EFSA-safe wording (Section 16). A blood test measures how much is circulating.

### Total B12 vs Active B12 — the difference that matters

- **The article's centrepiece (~250 words + a clean compare block).** Total B12 = all the B12 bound in your blood, most of it bound to a protein (haptocorrin) your cells can't use. **Active B12 (holotranscobalamin)** = the fraction bound to transcobalamin, the part actually delivered to cells. Because total includes the unusable majority, a "normal" total B12 can sit over a genuinely low active level — which is why symptoms sometimes appear while a standard test reads normal.
- Compare block (Total B12 / Active B12: what it measures / why it can mislead / which Andro Prime runs). Keep it sourced and clean for AI extraction.
- Compliance: this is a measurement-precision point, not a claim that Active B12 "diagnoses" anything better — frame as "measures the usable fraction directly," not "more accurate diagnosis."

### Why your GP ordered it — or why your panel includes it

- ~120 words. Common triggers: fatigue work-up, tingling/numbness investigation, anaemia follow-up (macrocytosis on an FBC — link to the FBC spoke once live), lower-meat-diet review, "general check."

### What counts as low, normal, and low-normal — the UK numbers

- Reference ranges (writer confirms exact UK figures at draft from NHS / NICE CKS "B12 and folate deficiency"): the low cutoff, the "low-normal grey zone" where symptoms can precede a frank flag, and the Active B12 thresholds Kit 2 uses (from the [Kit 2 spec](../../04_products/kits/kit-2-energy-recovery-check.md): Active B12 < 35 pmol/L low, 35-70 borderline, > 70 optimal). The pillar's recurring "normal vs optimal" move applies — link to `myth-of-normal-range` (live).

### What a single B12 reading does NOT tell you

- ~150 words. Defuse-the-worry. A single reading doesn't diagnose; recent supplements or injections distort it; it doesn't on its own explain a cause (low intake vs absorption are different problems); tingling/fatigue have many causes. An out-of-range value is a prompt to look further with a GP, not a verdict.

### When to see your GP, not us

- **Non-negotiable. ~150 words.** Direct, enabling. GP, not us, if: B12 low **with** neurological signs (tingling, numbness, balance or memory change) — these need prompt GP review, never self-treatment; suspected absorption problem / pernicious anaemia / past gastric surgery; on metformin or long-term acid-suppressants (absorption effect); vegan/low-B12 diet with symptoms. State plainly: **a genuine B12 deficiency — especially with nerve symptoms — is a GP diagnosis and may need injections, not an off-the-shelf supplement.**

### How Andro Prime measures B12 — Active B12 in Kit 2

- **One paragraph.** Kit 2 measures **Active B12 (holotranscobalamin)** — the usable fraction — alongside ferritin, Vitamin D and hs-CRP. Finger-prick at home, UKAS ISO 15189-accredited lab, results 2-5 working days, reviewed by Dr Ewa Lindo. Links to `/kits/energy-recovery` once inline. Note honestly: a low Active B12 with nerve symptoms routes to the GP, not to a supplement upsell.
