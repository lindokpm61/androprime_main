## 1. Why this article ships now

Large, winnable, compliance-light. `brain fog causes` is KD 8 and the whole cluster is plain-English
lifestyle search Andro Prime can own with no clinical cycle. It is the **Mind door** of the wellness
flywheel: the honest answer to "what causes brain fog" *is* a short list of lifestyle inputs plus a few
**testable** contributors (B12, iron, vitamin D, inflammation), which routes naturally into the Kit 2
(Energy & Recovery) orbit. Nobody UK-men-specific owns it.

## 2. The article's job (one sentence)

Help a foggy, unfocused 30–50yo man understand the **lifestyle** reasons his thinking feels slow, give
him things to change this week, and draw the honest line for when brain fog is worth a check or a GP
visit — without diagnosing anything.

## 3. Target reader

Men 30–50, "can't think straight / foggy / forgetful", everything "looks fine", not yet thinking about
blood tests. Arrived from a plain-English Google search ("brain fog", "what causes brain fog"). Wants
practical, non-preachy answers and a no-panic signal for when it's more than lifestyle.

## 4. Search-intent decoded

Informational, reassurance + self-help. They want: (a) "what is this / is it normal", (b) concrete causes
they can fix, (c) when to act. NOT looking to buy a test yet — product tie must be soft and earned.

## 7. Opening block (AI-snippet target)

2–3 sentences answering "what causes brain fog" head-on: most everyday brain fog in otherwise-healthy men
traces to a short list of inputs (poor sleep, stress load, blood-sugar swings, alcohol, and a few
correctable things a blood test can show — like low B12, iron or vitamin D), not a brain problem — and
the fix usually starts there. Sets up the "and here's when it's more than that" promise.

## 8. Heading scaffold (H2 / key H3)

- What causes brain fog *(primary keyword in this H2)*
  - Sleep you think is fine but isn't
  - Stress and the "wired but foggy" loop
  - Blood-sugar swings, alcohol, caffeine timing
  - The things you can't feel but a test can show (B12, iron, vitamin D, low-grade inflammation)
- How to get rid of brain fog — what to change this week
- When brain fog is more than lifestyle (the honest boundary) — GP/SystemAlert
- Where a check fits in (soft Kit 2 tie — non-diagnostic)
- Your next move

## 9. Section-by-section content brief

- **Causes:** practical, evidence-aware, NO claims/thresholds. Sleep (consistency > hours), stress
  (behavioural, not biochemical), blood sugar/alcohol/caffeine (levers). The "testable contributors"
  subsection names B12, iron/ferritin, vitamin D, inflammation as **"things a test looks at,"** never
  "you have a deficiency." This is the bridge to Kit 2, kept honest.
- **How to get rid of it (this week):** 4–6 concrete actions (sleep window, caffeine cut-off, protein+fibre
  breakfast to flatten blood sugar, alcohol trim, a walk/movement break, hydration). Shareable core.
- **When it's more than lifestyle (boundary):** see your GP if fog is sudden/severe, with persistent low
  mood, marked memory loss, neurological symptoms, or won't lift despite weeks of genuine change. The
  section Ewa sight-checks. (Note thyroid sits behind a GP/Kit 3 conversation, not Kit 2 — don't imply
  Kit 2 covers it.)
- **Where a check fits:** soft, honest — "if you've changed the inputs and still feel foggy, a check can
  show whether something measurable (e.g. B12, iron, vitamin D) is contributing; that's a GP conversation,
  or a check like our Energy & Recovery Check." No thresholds, no diagnosis.
