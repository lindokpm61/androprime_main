Help a 30–50yo man who wants more testosterone understand the **lifestyle levers that genuinely move it**,

## 9. Section-by-section content brief

- **Levers:** evidence-aware, ranked by real impact. Body fat (adiposity ↔ testosterone), resistance
  training, sleep, alcohol moderation, stress. Behavioural framing, NO biochemical promises, NO
  thresholds, NO "this will raise your T by X".
- **"Quickly":** answer the query honestly — meaningful change takes weeks-to-months of consistent
  lifestyle change; there is no safe overnight fix. Kills the quick-fix expectation without a claim.
- **Boosters/supplements (hype check):** the most claim-risky section — keep it sceptical and EFSA-clean.
  Permitted: zinc "contributes to the maintenance of normal testosterone levels" (EFSA). Do NOT promise
  any supplement *raises* testosterone. **Never name ashwagandha.** Steer to "test first, then address
  any actual gap with your GP / data-led choices."
- **Boundary:** see your GP for a proper assessment if you have persistent symptoms (low drive, fatigue,
  mood, etc.) — frame symptoms as "reasons to get checked," never as "you have low testosterone." Ewa
  reviews this whole section.
- **Measure first (Kit 1 tie):** soft, honest — "before you change everything or buy anything, it's worth
  knowing your actual level: our Testosterone Check (Kit 1) measures it." Non-diagnostic. Kit 1 is
  testosterone-only — do NOT frame it as explaining general fatigue/energy (that's Kit 2/3 per CONTEXT).
