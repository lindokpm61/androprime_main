# Partner-Proxy Buyer Persona — "She buys it for him"

**Created:** 2026-05-31 | **Owner:** Keith | **Status:** Validated with live UK data (DataForSEO + research). Companion to `portfolio-demand-gap-map.md` and `discovery-symptom-first.md`.

> **Verdict in one line:** Women searching/buying men's-health solutions for their partner is **real and measurable**, but it is **a buyer persona + a paid/gifting angle, NOT an SEO pillar.** The search volume is small and emotional; the value is in *channel and positioning*, where keyword tools structurally undercount her.

---

## The hypothesis (Keith, 2026-05-31)
"Women may be searching for solutions for their husbands." Correct — and worth acting on, but not where you'd first expect.

## What the data shows

### A. Partner-framed *symptom* queries — real but tiny + compliance-hot
UK monthly volume (DataForSEO, Labs scale), combined per cluster:

| Cluster | Example queries | ~Combined vol/mo | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sex drive | `husband has no sex drive` 70, `low sex drive husband` 50, `my husband has no sex drive` 40 | **~400–500** | ASA sexual-function surface |
| Libido | `low libido husband` 50, `husband with low libido` 50, `husband with no libido` 30 | **~150–200** | ASA sexual-function surface |
| Low mood | `is my husband depressed` 140, `my husband is depressed` 140 | **~280** | Clinical-adjacent |
| Energy/tired | `husband always tired` 30, `my husband has no energy` 10 | **~40** | **Compliance-safe** |
| Testosterone (direct) | `husband with low testosterone` 10, `how to deal with a husband with low testosterone` 10 | **~80** | Tiny |

- **Total genuinely-relevant partner-symptom cluster ≈ 1,000–1,300/mo**, fragmented across dozens of long-tails — roughly **1/10–1/20 of the man-facing terms** (`testosterone supplement` 14,800; `why am I always tired` 12,100). Not a pillar.
- **Intent is emotional, not commercial** (`reddit`, `how to deal with`, `trying to conceive`, `love my husband but…`) — reassurance-seeking, low buy-intent.
- **The big "husband" head terms are pop-culture noise** (`where is my husband` 18,100 = a RAYE song; `marry my husband` 8,100 = a K-drama; `why is my husband yelling at me` 9,900). Broad "husband" targeting is a trap.
- **Compliance:** the largest clusters (libido, sex drive, ED `why is my husband not getting erect with me` 140) sit on the **Pillar-E / ASA sexual-function surface → HOLD for Ewa.** Only the **energy / low-mood** framing is safe to use unguarded.

### B. Health-GIFT queries — the clean, winnable slice
This is the *buying-mode* version of the same person, and it's better on every axis:

| Query | Vol | KD | Intent |

| `health gifts for men` / `gifts for mens health` | 210 / 210 | 0 | high commercial |
| `men's health gift` | 210 | 0 | high commercial |
| `wellness gifts for men` / `wellness gift for men` | 170 / 170 | 0 | high commercial |
| `gift for health-conscious man` | 90 | 0 | transactional |
| `mens wellness gift` | 90 | 0 | informational |
| `self care gifts for men` | 70 | 0 | high commercial |

- **Men's-health-gift cluster ≈ 1,000/mo, KD 0–10, HIGH commercial intent, low compliance risk.** Better than the symptom queries: buying mode, winnable difficulty, care-framed not symptom-framed.
- **`gift health check` / `blood test gift` / `health gift voucher` all NULL** — nobody serves this well = an open gap (and a missing product mechanic, see below).

### C. Generic gifting — enormous, seasonal, NOT an SEO target
`gifts for men` **74,000** (KD6, transactional); `christmas gifts for men` 22,200; `birthday gifts for men` 14,800; `valentines gifts for men` 14,800; `experience gifts for him` 3,600. Unwinnable on SEO (Amazon/Etsy own it) — but it proves the gift-for-a-man market is huge and **seasonal**. These are **paid + seasonal-campaign hooks**, never rank targets.

## Why search undercounts her (the real point)
When the worried partner is ready to *buy*, she searches the **neutral** term (`at home testosterone test`) or arrives via **social/gifting**, not `…for my husband`. So her true economic weight is invisible to keyword tools. Supporting research: women do ~82% of household health research; men avoid GPs; GPs report men present "after they see results in their wives" ([Men's Health Forum](https://www.menshealthforum.org.uk/key-data-understanding-health-and-access-services), [Baker 2024](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/tre.950)).

## What to do (channel-split, in priority order)
1. **Paid social (Meta) — the primary play.** Target women 35–55 with partner-framed, concern-led creative ("Get him checked without the GP fight"). This is where her undercounted volume actually lives. Beats chasing 50-volume keywords.
2. **A gift-purchase mechanic — the product enabler.** "Gift a kit / health-check gift voucher." Checkout is self-purchase only today; `health gift voucher` demand exists and is **unserved**. Needed for both the gift-content spoke and the seasonal campaigns.
3. **One compliance-safe content spoke.** "Is your partner exhausted all the time?" framed on **energy / low mood** (NOT libido/ED), doubling as a paid landing page. Plus a **health-gift page** targeting the ~1,000/mo KD-0–10 gift cluster. Both small spokes, not pillars.
4. **Seasonal gifting campaigns (paid).** Ride Father's Day (June), Christmas, Valentine's, birthday peaks with the kit-as-gift. Capture a slice of the 74k generic gifting demand via paid + the gift mechanic.

## Compliance line (non-negotiable)
- **Safe to use openly:** energy, tiredness, low mood, general "health check", gifting/care framing.
- **HOLD for Ewa (Pillar E / ASA):** anything libido / sex drive / erectile / sexual-function partner framing. Do not ship partner copy on those surfaces without sign-off.

## Product gap surfaced
**No gift-purchase flow exists** (self-purchase checkout only). A "gift a kit" + voucher mechanic is the enabler for the entire partner-as-buyer opportunity and the seasonal campaigns. Flag for the product backlog.
