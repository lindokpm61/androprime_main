# Competitor Organic Teardown (Semrush, UK, May 2026)

One-time competitive intelligence pulled during the Semrush One trial. Source report:
`domain_organic_unique` (top organic pages by traffic), UK database. Snapshot ~May 2026.
This is banked because Semrush's full competitor content map can't be cheaply rebuilt via
DataForSEO once the trial lapses. Ongoing spoke volume/KD checks still go to DataForSEO.

---

## The headline finding

**The biomarker-explainer library is the dominant organic engine for every diagnostics
competitor.** Medichecks, Thriva and Forth each rank with deep libraries of "what is [marker]"
pages, and those pages — not their product pages — drive the bulk of their organic traffic.
This is hard validation that **Pillar D (markers explained) is the highest-ROI content play**,
and that our single CRP article is the first brick of what should be a full UK-male-focused
biomarker glossary.

Off-strategy for us (do NOT chase): Numan has pivoted to GLP-1/weight-loss (Mounjaro is their
single biggest page); Manual's engine is sexual-health/ED + a TRT subdomain. Neither is a
Phase 0 Andro Prime lane.

## What this changes / confirms

1. **Build the biomarker glossary (Pillar D expansion).** A UK-male-framed "what is [marker]"
   set is the proven engine. Priority markers (appear across ≥2 competitors AND map to our kits):
   testosterone (total/free), SHBG, LH, FSH, ferritin, vitamin D, HbA1c, ALT, GGT, ALP, cortisol,
   TSH. Each is a low-KD explainer with durable traffic. Gate marker selection to our actual kit
   panels + Ewa claim sign-off.
2. **Testosterone-education spokes are validated** beyond the C-spoke we have: "normal T level for
   age" (Medichecks 3.6k), "signs of low T" (599), "signs of high T" (Manual 923), "total vs free T"
   (551). Feeds the Pillar C hub buildout.
3. **Kit 5 (thyroid) + Kit 6 (cortisol) demand re-confirmed** — thyroid results chart (Numan 1.5k),
   high-cortisol-symptoms (Thriva 944), cortisol product pages (Medichecks, Forth). See
   [[project-future-kit-opportunities]].
4. **UK-statistics linkbait is a backlink tactic worth copying** — Forth's "UK testosterone/cholesterol
   statistics" pages. Original UK male-health data → earns links (ties to the Tier-1 backlink workstream).
5. **Avoid:** GLP-1/weight-loss and ED/sexual-health content — competitors own it and it's off our
   Phase 0 positioning.

## Link-building model (from competitor referring-domain profiles)

Top referring domains (by authority) for Medichecks/Forth aren't niche health blogs — they're
**UK national press, regional press, and lifestyle magazines**. The winning link pattern in this
space is **digital PR**: original UK health data/surveys pitched to journalists, who cite the source.
Forth's "UK testosterone statistics" / "UK cholesterol statistics" pages are the bait; the press
coverage is the payoff. Medichecks has notably more of this coverage than Forth.

**Target publications already covering this space (our outreach list):**
- National: The Guardian, The Independent, Telegraph, Daily Mail, The Sun, Mirror, Express, Evening Standard, Metro
- Regional: WalesOnline, Manchester Evening News, Liverpool Echo, Daily Record
- Health/lifestyle: **Men's Health**, Women's Health, Cosmopolitan, Elle
- Other: MoneySavingExpert, Boots (retail/health), BBC Good Food

**Action (post-launch):** produce one original UK male-health data piece (e.g. an Andro Prime
testosterone/vitamin-D survey or anonymised aggregate of our own result data once we have volume),
then pitch it to the above. This is the highest-leverage link play and it doubles as Pillar F
(original-data) E-E-A-T signal. Not urgent pre-launch — banked as the link strategy.
