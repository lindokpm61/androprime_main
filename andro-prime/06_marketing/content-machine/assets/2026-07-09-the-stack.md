## Chosen hook

Spoken: "It's rarely one big cause. It's five small ones you stopped noticing."
On-screen text (frame 1, muted-legible): EIGHT HOURS IN BED. STILL FLAT BY TWO.

## Script

> A mate told me he was tired all the time. Not sleepy. Flat.
>
> So I asked what had changed.
>
> Nothing, he said. Same job. Same training. Same sleep, roughly.
>
> That "roughly" is where these things live.
>
> It's rarely one big cause. It's five small ones you stopped noticing. Broken sleep. Stress that never switches off. Training that's outrun recovery.
>
> Add a drink most evenings and a breakfast that drops you at two.
>
> He didn't have one big problem. He had a stack, and no way to see it.
>
> Change two of them for a fortnight. If your energy moves, you've found it.
>
> If it doesn't move, that's when a blood test earns its place. Not before.
>
> Education, not medical advice.
>
> Which two have you been calling "roughly the same"?
