## A. Ideal Viewer Avatar: Mark, 44

Operations manager, two kids, mortgage, decent-not-great shape. He used to be the one with the energy. Now he naps in the car at lunch, needs a second coffee to get through the afternoon, and can't work out why a workout that used to cost him one day of soreness now costs three. His drive is down and he's noticed; so has his wife, and neither says much about it.

He went to his GP because he did the responsible thing. The GP ran a basic panel, told him he was "normal," and suggested more sleep and less stress. Mark left both relieved and quietly furious, because "normal" didn't explain why he feels like this. He's not ill. He's just not himself, and being told he's fine when he knows he isn't is its own specific insult.

So he did what men like him do: went online. He's got a cupboard of supplements built from Reddit threads and a YouTube video (vitamin D, magnesium, a B complex, fish oil, maybe an ashwagandha) and no idea whether any of it is doing anything. He half-suspects he's flushing money away. He's read enough to be dangerous and not enough to be sure. He's circled the TRT world and it scares him a bit: the needles, the "is this cheating," the men who clearly overdid it.

What he actually wants is someone to **look at his numbers and tell him the truth in plain English.** Not sell him a pill. Not gatekeep him behind a £200 consultation. Not make him feel like a hypochondriac gym-bro. He'd pay for a real answer tomorrow if someone credible offered one without the lecture.

**Quick profile:** UK, 35 to 55. Time-poor, not cash-poor. Trusts data and a straight-talking real person over a brand or an influencer. Reads reviews. Suspicious of supplement marketing and of clinics that feel like a sales funnel. Brand-affine to plain-English, no-hype, "I've been through it too." Buying trigger: a specific, believable explanation for how he feels.

*(This is the Symptomatic Achiever made into one man, cross-bred with the "two years of guessing" wound. It is also, not by accident, close to Keith's own story, which is why Keith can voice him. Founder-as-product-feature.)*

## B. Dream Outcome

**To finally know, for certain, what's actually going on inside him, and what to do about it, without begging a GP or gambling on supplements.**

Not "get on TRT." Not even "have more energy" in the abstract. The dream is **certainty replacing the guessing.** The end of "maybe it's my sleep, maybe it's my age, maybe it's stress." A number, an explanation he can act on, and the quiet confidence that he's addressing the real thing instead of supplementing around a problem he can't see.

## What this makes us make, and avoid

A sharp avatar's real payoff is telling us what NOT to make.

**Make:** fatigue explained by real markers (iron, D, B12, hs-CRP); "normal vs optimal"; "what your GP won't test"; "supplements you're taking on faith"; Keith's own before/after numbers.

**Avoid. This is the compliance line, not a taste call:**

- Anything that promises TRT or implies we provide it. **The audience's gravity pulls hard toward TRT** (every testosterone thread resolves to "go to a clinic / self-prescribe / buy UGL"); that is the one place we cannot follow Mark in Phase 0. The avatar lets us sit in the same room as that man without following him into what we can't sell. Low-T results route to GP referral plus optional consent-gated education nurture, never a TRT pitch.
- Andropause / libido angles until Ewa signs **Pillar E**.
- Any "this fixes your fatigue" cure-claim. Education side of the line only; every script runs `/compliance-preflight` against `03_compliance/CONTEXT.md`.
- **Ashwagandha by name, ever**, even though it sits in Mark's own cupboard (root guardrail 3, silent-ingredient rule).
- Markers we don't yet sell a kit for (thyroid, cortisol, metabolic): usable as content, but never implied as something we currently test.
