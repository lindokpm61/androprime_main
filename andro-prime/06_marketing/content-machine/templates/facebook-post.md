# Template — Facebook Post (older segment, native)

Native informational post or short native video for the older ~45-70 segment. **Native upload, not a lazy Instagram cross-post** (cross-posting can strip audio or misroute to Feed). Original framing, no engagement-bait CTA, own audio. Run through `sops/sop-compliance-route.md`.

---

## Fill-in structure

- **Pillar / marker:** (must map to a live kit)
- **Feeling-first opening line:** ______
- **The plain-English explainer (2-4 short paragraphs, define the marker in-sentence):** ______
- **The compliance hedge (signal not cause; GP not supplement; "we test, explain, and tell you the next step, including when that's your GP"):** ______
- **Link:** to the pillar or the router (the post must stand on its own value; do not link-dump). UTM-tagged.
- **CTA target:** Kit ___ / email rung (never FM list).
- **Native video?** if yes, upload native + use Facebook's own audio library; thumbnail per `sops/sop-thumbnail.md`.

---

## Worked example (Pillar B — fatigue → Kit 2)

> Eight hours' sleep and still flat by mid-afternoon? A lot of men my age put that down to getting older and leave it there.
>
> Sometimes it is age. Sometimes it's something you can actually measure. Four of the usual suspects for low energy show up in a blood test: ferritin (your iron stores), Active B12, vitamin D, and hs-CRP (a marker your liver releases when your immune system is irritated).
>
> A blood test doesn't diagnose why you're tired. It shows you which of those numbers is off, so you know what to ask your GP about instead of guessing.
>
> We test, explain the number, and tell you the next step, including when that step is your GP.
>
> Education, not medical advice. Find out what your blood says: [link]

*Why this passes: feeling-first; native framing; defines each marker; hedges to the GP; no diagnose/treat/cure; markers are live-kit (Kit 2); no FM CTA; no em dashes; stands on its own value before the link.*
