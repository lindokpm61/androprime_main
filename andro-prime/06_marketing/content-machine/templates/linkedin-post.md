## Worked example (Pillar C — the normal range → Kit 1)

> A bloke my age showed me his GP results last week. Total testosterone: 12. "Within normal range," they'd told him.
>
> So I asked the one thing his GP hadn't. What is the range?
>
> Eight to twenty-nine.
>
> Most men assume that band means healthy. It doesn't. It's a statistical spread: the blood of every adult man from eighteen to seventy, athletes and sedentary alike, drawn at the 2.5th and 97.5th percentile.
>
> The range was built to spot illness. It was never built to tell you how you feel.
>
> He didn't have a normal-range problem. He had a question-mismatch problem. His GP answered "are you ill." He was asking "are you well." Two different questions. One test.
>
> This doesn't diagnose anything, and it isn't a reason to panic. It's a reason to know your number and what it actually means, and to take that to your GP if it warrants it.
>
> What number did your GP actually give you, and did anyone tell you the range?

*Why this passes: hook on line 1; diagnostic-question arc; reframe; no bullet list; no diagnose/treat/cure; hedges to the GP; no FM CTA; no em dashes; ends on a question.*
