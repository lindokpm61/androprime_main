# Hook Playbook (Spine B founder content)

**Owner:** Keith Antony | **Status:** Craft reference v1, 2026-07-09 | **Read first:** `avatar-mark.md`, `founder-content-system.md`

The repeatable craft for writing scroll-stopping hooks for Keith's short-form and YouTube. Synthesised from three studied hook breakdowns (desire-based hooking; the four hook mistakes; the six hook archetypes) and filtered through the Andro Prime compliance rails. Write every hook to **Mark** (`avatar-mark.md`) and in the **Peer / Storyteller / Documentarian** voice, never the Authority posture.

**The one rule above all others:** a hook exists to open a curiosity loop. It does that through **contrast**: the viewer believes A, you show B, and the gap between them is the pull. Bigger contrast, deeper hook. You have about five seconds.

---

## 1. Pick the key visual FIRST

The single most important production rule, and the one that changes how we shoot.

The viewer takes a video in as **visual → spoken → visual**. Eyes process far faster than ears, so they see the frame, then hear you, then look back at the frame to confirm what they heard. The visual leads. It is not what you say; it is what they see.

**A talking head is the weakest possible opening visual.** If Keith opens on his face, there is nothing to stop the scroll.

**Our key visual is the blood.** The lancet, the drop, the kit, the results dashboard, the real number filling the screen. Nobody else in men's health opens on real founder blood data. That is our built-in scroll-stop and it is un-fakeable.

So the order of writing is always:

1. What is the key visual I actually have for the first 3 to 5 seconds? (Default: the blood, the kit, or the number.)
2. Which archetype does that visual best support?
3. Only then write the words.

If there is no strong visual and no honest way to manufacture one, **do not make the video.** There is always another idea.

## 2. The six hook archetypes

Any one story can be framed through any of the six. Choose the one that fits the key visual and creates the most contrast. Ferritin examples below are illustrative only; every line still goes through `/compliance-preflight` and the rails in §4.

| Archetype | The contrast it builds | Ferritin-story example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Fortune Teller** | present vs future | "In five years, no man will accept 'your bloods are normal' as an answer." |
| **Experimenter** | old way vs a method I just tried (peer to peer) | "I tested my own blood on camera. One number explained a year of feeling flat." |
| **Teacher** | you're failing at X, here's the lesson | "If you're tired all the time, here's the one marker most men never think to check." |
| **Magician** | pure visual stun (stacks on any other) | Open cold on the lancet and blood drop: "Watch this," then into the line. |
| **Investigator** | you don't know the secret, now you do | "There's a number your GP usually doesn't run, and for a lot of tired men it's the whole story." |
| **Contrarian** | consensus vs what I believe | "'Normal' on a blood test doesn't mean healthy. It means average." |

**Our natural home** is Experimenter and Investigator (both lead on the blood, both are Peer voice), with Magician stacked on top as the cold-open stun. Fortune Teller and Teacher are fine occasional variety.

**The Contrarian is our most powerful and most dangerous.** The wedge ("normal is not the same as healthy") is inherently contrarian and it performs. But aim it at **the reference range / the system**, never at **the profession**. "Normal isn't healthy" is fine. "Your GP is lying to you" is a brand and compliance landmine. Scalpel, not hammer.

## 3. Desire-based framing: dream outcome + relatable character

A buyer scrolls with a latent unsolved problem. When they hear the **dream outcome** they want, their attention locks. Then they instantly judge whether the **character** who got it is relatable with no unfair advantage. Relatable plus believable equals hooked.

**Our dream outcome is CERTAINTY, not a cure.** This is the compliance key to the entire framework. "I felt better" or "this fixed my fatigue" is a treatment claim we cannot make. "I finally found out what was actually wrong" is compliant, and it is a stronger hook anyway (an answer is more intriguing than relief). Always frame the outcome as **the answer**, never the cure.

**Our relatability trap is Keith's founder status.** Owning a blood-testing company is the ultimate unfair advantage and a disclosure we are legally required to make. So: **lead with the tired man, reveal the founder as the turn.** The hook is "I felt like garbage for a year and my GP said I was fine." The "so I built a company to fix that" comes after relatability is earned, never in the hook. Front-loading "I'm a founder, buy my test" is both unrelatable and salesy.

Five sentence-level templates for the outcome+character move:

1. **About me** (backward): "I found the real reason for X using [relatable, condition-free method]."
2. **If I** (forward): "If I had X problem, the first thing I'd check is Y."
3. **To you**: "If you're dealing with X, here's the one thing worth checking."
4. **Can you?**: "Can a grown man actually be X? My bloods said [real number]."
5. **He / she just did**: "This guy did X for two years. Turns out he only ever needed one thing."

## 4. Compliance rails (non-negotiable, every hook)

Hooks are advertising. A founder account is not a loophole. Every hook obeys `03_compliance/CONTEXT.md` and runs `/compliance-preflight`.

- **Dream outcome = the answer / certainty, never a cure or symptom fix.** No "this fixes your fatigue," no diagnose / treat / cure.
- **No TRT promise or implication.** The audience's gravity pulls to TRT; we cannot follow. Low-T routes to GP referral plus optional consent-gated nurture, never a pitch. (See the make/avoid list in `avatar-mark.md`.)
- **Real numbers only.** Any figure on screen ("ferritin was 14") must be Keith's genuine result. Do not fabricate bloodwork. This is why the Ep 0 baseline shoot comes before these can be filmed.
- **Every hook maps to a currently-available marker** (testosterone; Vit D, B12, ferritin, hs-CRP). Thyroid, cortisol, metabolic: usable as content, never implied as something we test yet.
- **Ashwagandha silent, always** (root guardrail 3), even when it sits in the story.
- **Andropause / libido angles wait for Pillar E sign-off** (Ewa).
- **Founder disclosure** stays present in the video even though it is not in the hook.
- **Contrarian energy on the system, never the profession.**

## 5. The seven-step write (checklist)

Run this for every hook. If it fails the gut check, rewrite or bin the idea.

1. **Key visual.** What do I actually have for the first 3 to 5 seconds? (Blood / kit / number by default.)
2. **Biggest contrast.** What is the most surprising true angle in this story? That decides the archetype.
3. **Spoken hook**, two to four short lines, fifth-grade vocabulary, active voice, no wasted words. Dream outcome = the answer; character relatable; founder reveal held back.
4. **Text hook** on screen that supports and reconfirms the visual (short, obvious, first two seconds).
5. **Audio hook** aligned (sound / music matches the tone, does not fight it).
6. **Alignment audit.** Do the spoken, visual, text and audio hooks all mean the *same one thing*? Misalignment kills comprehension, and no comprehension means no hook.
7. **Gut check.** Does the hook plant one unmistakable subject and one single question in every viewer's head? Would Mark lift his thumb, and does it make him trust us more? If not, back to step 3, or bin it.

---

## 6. Don't guess, model winners

Do not invent hooks from a blank page. Study proven hooks from **small and mid-size** creators in adjacent lanes (the "normal range" critique, fatigue / deficiency, blood-test explainers), where a breakout proves the *structure*, not just fame. Mine our own winners once we have them. The voice-of-customer sweeps (Reddit, 2026-07-09; verbatim pain in `avatar-mark.md` §C) are the raw material: real sentences beat invented copy every time. Keep adding winning structures and painful real lines to the bank below.
