# Long-Form (YouTube) Script Playbook (Spine B founder content)

**Owner:** Keith Antony | **Status:** Craft reference v1, 2026-07-09 | **Read first:** `../content/youtube-founder-journey-strategy.md` (the authority), `avatar-mark.md`, `hook-playbook.md`

The repeatable craft for writing a **long-form YouTube script** (a 6-to-12-minute explainer or founder-journey episode). This doc owns the *scriptwriting craft* only. Scope, the two video lines, CTA routing, and the compliance rails are owned by `../content/youtube-founder-journey-strategy.md`, and it wins wherever the two touch. Adapted from a studied five-step long-form framework and gated on those rails.

Long-form is a different craft from short-form (`script-playbook.md`). Short-form stops a scroll in three seconds; long-form earns a click with a title, then holds attention for minutes.

---

## Which line are you writing?

Two lines, from the strategy doc, and they source their content differently:

- **Line 1, Explainer.** Atomised from a published, Ewa-signed blog asset, in content-calendar order. **It may not make a claim the article does not already make.** The article is the substantiation record; the video inherits it. Your "unique points" are reshapings of that article, not new claims.
- **Line 2, Founder journey.** Keith's own kits, real numbers, real timeline. Governed by the founder-testimonial limits: he shows his data and journey but may not assert a supplement caused a change. "How my levels have changed", never "what fixed them."

Everything below applies to both; where they differ, it is called out.

---

## Step 0. The governing principle: reality beats expectations

A viewer stays while reality is better than they expected and leaves the moment it is worse. Every choice below serves that gap. For us, "reality" is the genuinely useful, true, compliant insight (what a basic GP panel and the big testing brands skip on this marker), never a bolder health claim. We beat expectations with usefulness and honesty, not with promises.

---

## Step 1. Packaging (idea + title + thumbnail)

Do this before writing a line.

- **Idea:** one sentence naming Mark's pain point (from `avatar-mark.md`), the rabbit hole the video goes down.
- **Title (must be set first):** plain English, in the symptom language men search ("always tired", "brain fog"), never the clinical term. The marker name is the **answer, not the hook** (strategy doc section 4). Curiosity comes from a myth, a number, or a question. **Never from a cure or a claim** ("This blood test fixed my energy" is clickbait and a breach). The title sets the expectation the intro must then confirm.
- **Thumbnail:** a loose idea is fine; do not let thumbnail iteration block writing.
- **Click confirmation:** the first two or three lines of the intro must confirm, and ideally beat, the title's promise.

---

## Step 2. Outline before intro (the uniqueness gate)

Write a bulleted outline first, and gut-check it: is there anything genuinely useful and non-obvious here, or is it a summary of what everyone already says? If it is not unique, do not make the video; go back to the source.

- **Line 1:** the outline is pulled from the Ewa-signed article. Uniqueness is the *angle and distillation* ("what your GP and the big brands skip"), not new facts. Do not exceed the article's claims.
- **Line 2:** the outline is Keith's real journey and numbers.

For each point, layer **what** it is, **why** it matters to a tired man, and **how** it fits the whole story. Measure twice, cut once: idea, title, and outline are all done before you write the script.

---

## Step 3. The intro (five parts, builds the curiosity loop)

1. **Immediate context.** State plainly what the video is about, confirming the click.
2. **Common belief.** Name what Mark already believes ("your GP said your bloods are normal, so you must be fine"). This builds common ground and sets up the contrast.
3. **Contrarian take.** Contradict the belief ("normal means average, not healthy"). This is the stun. **Aim it at the reference range and the system, never at the GP or the profession.**
4. and 5. (either order) **Proof** and **plan.** Proof = why trust this (Keith's own story; the Ewa-approved recommendation logic; the article, attributed per the rail below). Plan = the ordered list of what the video will cover.

---

## Step 4. The body (2-1-3-4, value loop, re-hook)

**Order the points 2-1-3-4:** second-best point first, best point second, then third, fourth, and so on. A rising pattern makes the viewer stay; a falling one trains them to leave.

**Each point runs the value loop:**

1. **Context** (what it is, as simple as possible).
2. **Application** (how it works / what to do, with a concrete example).
3. **Framing** (why it matters to a tired man and how it fits the whole).

**Re-hook between points:** a mini-hook at the seam ("that matters, but on its own it is only half the story") so the viewer carries into the next point.

Compliance holds on every point: within the article's claims (Line 1) or the testimonial limits (Line 2); no cure / treat / diagnose / fix; "how my levels changed", never "what fixed them"; Kit 1 is testosterone only, never framed as general fatigue; ashwagandha never named or shown.

---

## Step 5. The outro (fortune cookie)

Summarise the points, remind the viewer of the pain you solved (the end of guessing), and leave on a high note. This is where likes, shares, and rewatches come from, so do not treat it as an afterthought.

---

## The CTA (native embed, routed our way)

Weave the CTA into the point it naturally solves, as the answer to a pain already raised, so it does not feel like an ad break. But route it per the strategy doc section 7, not Kallaway's generic advice:

- **Cold viewers to the free email rung first** (symptom guide / quiz to email to nurture), not straight to checkout.
- **Intent-match to the best live product** (inflammation/CRP to Kit 2 now; liver to email capture / waitlist, never to a hormone kit).
- **Never route into the Founding-Member list** (CQC/ASA).
- **Founder and brand disclosure on screen** (it is advertising).
- **Attribution (strategy doc section 8):** a claim-free derivative uses "Based on our article [title], clinically reviewed by Dr Ewa Lindo, GMC #4758565." Only a script Ewa actually reviewed (any net-new claim) may say "Clinically reviewed by Dr Ewa Lindo" directly. If your script introduces a net-new claim, it does not ship until Ewa signs it.

---

## The finish checklist

1. Does the intro confirm the title within three lines?
2. Is the first body point the second-best (the rising pattern)?
3. Does every point run the value loop and re-hook into the next?
4. Does the outro leave a high note and restate the "stop guessing" solve?
5. Real numbers are Keith's own (Ep 0); nothing fabricated.
6. **Compliance gate:** run `/compliance-preflight`. Line-1 claims stay inside the Ewa-signed article; Line-2 respects testimonial limits; no TRT, no cure, ashwagandha silent, Kit 1 scoped to testosterone, attribution correct, no FM-list routing.
