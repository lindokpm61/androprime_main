## 1. Executive summary

- **Two acquisition engines, not one.** **Engine A (Affiliate)** = rented, warm, fast, narrow — PTs/influencers/gyms (unchanged from v2.3). **Engine B (Owned content/DTC)** = earned, cold, slow-compounding, broad — SEO + YouTube + organic social + email. Both are co-primary. Both run from pre-launch. Still **zero paid media.**
- **Two audiences, not one.** "30-70" splits into a **younger ~30-45** segment (social/influencer/affiliate, more willing to pay) and an **older ~45-70** segment (search/YouTube/Facebook, trust-driven, price-sensitive, only reachable via owned DTC).
- **The affiliate engine has a demographic ceiling.** PTs/gyms reach the younger, fitness-adjacent man. The older man — arguably the highest clinical-need, best TRT-pipeline group — is reachable **only** via owned search + YouTube + trust. So DTC is not optional; it is the only route to roughly half the market.
- **One content spine feeds everything.** A symptom pillar becomes blog + short-form social + affiliate content kit + email. Create once, atomise everywhere (build spec: the content-atomisation model, next doc).
- **Premium pricing holds.** £99/£119/£179, above MediChecks, justified by interpretation + the loop + clinical pipeline. No cheap entry kit. Entry *friction* is lowered with a free content rung, not price.
- **Trust at scale:** Keith on camera (peer + founder), Dr Ewa Lindo as clinical authority via attribution + a consented short-form digital twin. No unconsented synthetic.
- **Content starts now**, routed to the best-matched live product, redirected to Kit 3 Plus / Liver as they launch.

## 2. The strategic bet (and what would disprove it)

**The bet:** at premium pricing, a new men's-health brand wins by combining *borrowed* trust (affiliates, fast warm start) with *earned* trust (owned symptom content that compounds and is defensible), targeting the underserved gaps the funded competitors don't own — and converting on **data-led clinical credibility**, not on out-spending or out-ranking them on contested head terms.

**Why now / why this:** the demand is symptom-shaped and 2-3× larger than the product-anchored view; the strongest gaps (inflammation/CRP, liver, metabolic, thyroid) have **no UK men's-health specialist**; and the moat is the closed loop + AI-citation (Pillar F) + Ewa's E-E-A-T + the founder brand — none of which a competitor can buy quickly.

**What would prove it wrong:**
- Owned content fails to rank/convert after a fair compounding window (≈2 quarters) with the engine properly resourced.
- The affiliate engine alone hits its ceiling *and* DTC doesn't pick up the older segment.
- Premium pricing suppresses conversion below the per-kit net floor even with the trust assets in place.
- Compliance drag (ASA on symptom/affiliate content) throttles the content engine faster than it compounds.

## 3. The two-segment ICP

"30 up to 65/70" is too wide to be one ICP. It splits behaviourally (evidence: §9 of the reframe doc):

| | **Younger ~30-45** | **Older ~45-70** |
|---|---|---|
| Where online | Instagram, some TikTok/Reddit, YouTube | **YouTube + Google search + Facebook** |
| Behaviour | Feed-discovery, follows influencers, short-form | **Searches/researches, long-form, does NOT buy off a feed** |
| Willingness to pay | Higher (more likely to go private) | Lower / price-sensitive (in commodities; less so for trust purchases) |
| Reached by | Engine A (affiliate) + short-form social | **Engine B (SEO + YouTube + Ewa-attributed trust)** |
| Clinical value | Earlier-stage | **Higher clinical need; best TRT pipeline** |

The whole plan is organised around this split. Same content spine; different atomisation and channel weighting per segment.

## 5. Channel architecture

### Engine A — Affiliate (warm-start, younger-segment-weighted)
PT affiliate (hero), micro-influencer, gym partnerships. Economics, commission stack, recruitment funnel, and the two-stage gate are **unchanged** — see `affiliates/pt-programme.md` (v2.3), `affiliates/commission-structure.md` (v2.3), and the acquisition-strategy v3 timeline. **One change:** the PT/influencer asset pack is rebuilt around **symptom hooks** (from the content spine), not just personal-story/discount framing — this also fixes the recurring "what % of PTs actually posted" activation risk, which is a content problem in disguise.

### Engine B — Owned content / DTC (co-primary, compounding, older-segment-weighted)
The part the locked plan lacked. Runs from pre-launch.
- **SEO / blog** — symptom hub-and-spoke; the underserved clusters first (inflammation/CRP, liver, metabolic, thyroid). Intent capture.
- **YouTube (organic)** — the missing central channel for the older segment (85% of 50-64s use it; bridges search + discovery). Keith-presented long-form explainers + founder-journey series + Ewa-twin short segments. *Organic only; YouTube ads stay excluded.*
- **Organic social** — IG Reels / Shorts / TikTok for the younger segment; Facebook (informational/community) for the older. **The v2.3 organic-TikTok exclusion is reversed** — split organic (adopt) from paid (still excluded).
- **Email** — the conversion spine for cold traffic: free entry rung (symptom guide/quiz) → nurture → kit. Routes to Kit 1/3/3 Plus only, **never the FM list** (CQC/ASA; see `feedback_fm_list_not_in_content`).
- **GEO / AI-citation (Pillar F)** — named as a channel and KPI, not just an SEO factor; the V7 patient-owned-data defensibility bet, won differently from Google rank.

### Supporting
Keith's LinkedIn (founder authority, B2B/press halo), Reddit (younger researchers), earned media/PR (slow burn).

### Excluded (unchanged)
Google Search ads, Meta (cold + retargeting), paid YouTube/TikTok/LinkedIn, podcast sponsorship. Re-evaluate at M3. **Paid stays off; organic everything is in.**

## 8. Pricing principle (decided) + product/content sequencing (decided)

**Pricing — hold premium above competitors, like-for-like.** Never enter the commodity floor; lower entry *friction* with a free rung, not price. Premium varies in absolute terms by scope but every rung sits above the equivalent competitor product. No cheap entry kit. (Evidence + the liver reprice to a ~£59-69 *test target* are in the reframe doc §2.9; the direction is evidence-backed, exact numbers are to be validated by live A/B or a Van Westendorp survey.)

**Sequencing — option (b), build content now, redirect later.** Start the content engine now; route by best-matched *live* product; redirect/insert to Kit 3 Plus and Liver as they launch on Keith's timeline.
- Content is **evergreen/topic-based**, never hard-wired to a kit — only the CTA target changes when the product does.
- CTAs route through **one central component/config** — redirect is a single change, not a per-article rewrite.
- **Intent-match to the best live product:** inflammation/CRP → **Kit 2 now** (carries hs-CRP), upsell/redirect to **Kit 3 Plus** when live; liver → **email capture/waitlist now**, point to the **Liver kit** when launched (don't route liver intent to a hormone kit).

## 9. The funnel (two paths into one loop)

**Path A (warm / affiliate):** trusted intermediary (PT/influencer/gym code) → LP variant → kit → result → supplement subscription → (post-CQC) TRT.

**Path B (cold / owned DTC):** symptom search or feed → content (blog/YouTube/social) → **free entry rung** (guide/quiz → email) → nurture (Ewa-validated) → kit → result → supplement subscription → (post-CQC) TRT.

Both converge on the same **test → result → supplement loop** (the LTV engine and the CQC cohort pre-qualifier). Path B is what reaches the older half and what compounds.

## 10. Phasing

- **Pre-launch:** stand up Engine B alongside the existing Engine A pre-launch build. Begin the content engine (symptom hubs for the underserved clusters), Keith's founder-journey baseline, YouTube channel, email capture rung. Affiliate recruitment per v3 timeline.
- **Launch → M3:** both engines live. Affiliate carries early volume (fast); content begins compounding (slow). Content routed to live kits (b). Two-stage gate (Week 3 pulse, Week 6 go/no-go) still governs the affiliate engine.
- **M3+:** content/DTC share of acquisition rising; redirect content to Kit 3 Plus/Liver as they launch; re-evaluate paid stance with a real retargeting audience built from owned traffic.

(Week-by-week affiliate actions: inherit the acquisition-strategy v3 timeline. Content cadence: the content-atomisation model + content-calendar.)

## 12. Compliance posture (Guardrail #1)

Symptom-based *health* content, especially short-form and especially when posted by *affiliates*, is the **highest-ASA-risk surface** the brand has — complaints land on Andro Prime, not the PT. Therefore:
- Pre-cleared **symptom-hook library** (Ewa-signed) is non-optional; the affiliate content kit is built from it so PTs never improvise claims. The weekly content audit becomes load-bearing.
- **Pillar E (andropause / male-menopause umbrella)** — the highest-leverage narrative (~10-15k, low KD) — is ASA/Ewa-gated. Unlocking Ewa sign-off is now a top priority, not a someday item.
- Read `03_compliance/CONTEXT.md` before any external copy. Compliance overrides persuasion.
