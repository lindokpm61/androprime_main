## 2. The Strategic Bet (and what would prove it wrong)

### The bet

PTs are the highest-leverage single channel for Andro Prime at premium pricing because:

1. They have permission to discuss energy, recovery, and performance with their clients — and a £119 home test fits naturally into a "let's actually find out what's going on" conversation that an Instagram ad cannot replicate.
2. The recommendation is a warm referral by definition.
3. Repeated exposure across multiple training sessions compounds conversion.
4. The product makes their service better — a PT who knows their client's vitamin D, B12, ferritin, and CRP writes smarter training plans.
5. Premium price points actually *favour* PT recommendation over cold ads, because trust friction matters more at £119 than at £29.
6. UK PTs are not currently a target of any direct competitor's affiliate programme.

### What would prove the bet wrong

**Two-stage decision gate** (this is the primary risk-management mechanism):

**Week 3 pulse check (early warning, not kill decision):**

| Signal | Healthy | Yellow flag | Red flag (consider cut) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cold DM reply rate (cumulative) | ≥6% | 3–5% | <2% |
| % of onboarded PTs who've posted any content | ≥40% | 20–39% | <15% |
| Total kit sales | ≥10 | 5–9 | <3 |
| PT-sourced sales as % of total | ≥35% | 20–34% | <10% |
| Compliance violations | 0–1 | 2 | 3+ |

**At Week 3:** Multiple yellow flags = restructure. Multiple red flags = serious review. **Don't cut on a single Week 3 signal — they're noisy.**

**Week 6 real go/no-go (formal Gate 0A) [v2.3 — FM deposit row removed]:**

| Signal | Continue | Restructure | Cut PT engine, pivot |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cumulative kit sales | ≥40 | 25–39 | <20 |
| Active PTs (sold in last 30d) | ≥15 | 8–14 | <5 |
| PT-sourced sales | ≥35% of total | 20–34% | <15% |
| Active Daily Stack subscribers (warm-pipeline proxy) | ≥8 | 3–7 | <2 |
| Avg sales per active PT (30 days) | ≥0.8 | 0.4–0.7 | <0.3 |

**At Week 6:** Three or more "Cut" signals → cut PT engine as primary channel, pivot to paid media + organic. (FM deposit count is no longer a Phase 0 economic gate per v2.3 — see Section 0. Daily Stack subscriber count substitutes as the warm-pipeline proxy.)

The plan does not assume the PT engine will work. It builds in a structured exit if it doesn't.

### Where the premium price specifically makes things harder

A £107 kit (after 10% PT discount) is still not an impulse buy. Customer journey from PT recommendation to purchase is 3–7 days, sometimes longer. The plan accommodates by:

- Lower productivity assumptions (1.0–1.2 kits/active PT/month)
- Longer "first sale" window (45 days post-onboarding)
- Stronger email nurture sequence (4 emails over 5 days)

## 3. Compliance Posture (Read First)

Aggressive volume + affiliate trainers + men's hormones + premium price = the highest-ASA-risk channel mix Andro Prime has. The compliance system (full detail in `affiliates/pt-programme.md` v2.3 Section 8):

| Layer | What it does | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-issuance brief | Every PT signs a written attestation before code issuance covering: no medical claims, no TRT availability claims, no Ashwagandha mentions, no diagnostic language, no implied product cure of any condition, **and exact discount language (10% only — never imply more)** | Keith |
| Asset pack | Pre-approved Story scripts, Reel scripts, post captions, client conversation Q&A — built around credibility-led personal-story framing for free-kit PTs and value-led 10% discount framing for the rest | Keith + Ewa |
| Weekly content audit | Automated scrape of Instagram and LinkedIn for handle mentions + manual review of top 20 PTs each week | Keith (later: VA) |
| 3-strikes enforcement | 1st: written warning + correction. 2nd: code paused 30 days. 3rd: code revoked. | Keith |
| Kill switch | Any ASA query or regulator DM: code revoked same day, post takedown requested, incident logged | Keith |

**Absolute rules (zero tolerance, immediate revocation):**
- Ashwagandha mentioned by name
- "TRT is available now" or any equivalent
- A false medical diagnosis claim
- A false cure claim

**Hybrid-model-specific compliance rule:** PTs may say "use my code, get 10% off" — they may NOT say "20% off," "biggest discount available," "exclusive deal," or other inflated framing. The ASA polices "exaggerated savings claims" specifically. First violation = strike-1.

## 4. Brand Positioning & Voice

### Positioning Statement

> Andro Prime is the men's health company that finds out what's actually wrong before telling you what to do about it. Built by a man who went through it. Guided by a GP who treats it. Sold without jargon — and without apology for what it costs.

### Voice Test
Would Keith say this to a friend in a pub? If not, rewrite it. (Full voice rules: `06_marketing/positioning/product-marketing-context.md`)

### Premium price + 10% discount — how to talk about it

- **Don't apologise.** "It's £119 (or £107 with my code)" — said directly.
- **Anchor against the alternative.** "Private clinic consultation alone: £200+. NHS GP refusal of test: free but irrelevant."
- **Lead with what the price buys.** UKAS-accredited lab. Clinical sign-off (Dr Ewa Lindo). Plain-English personalised report. Specific recommendation.
- **The 10% discount is a signal, not a sales lever.** Don't lead with it. PTs use it as the closing detail, not the headline.
- **Don't compete with MediChecks on price.** MediChecks is a lab portal. Andro Prime is an answer.

### What "premium with discount" rules out

- Discount-led promotion. The 10% is a token of credibility ("your PT vouched for you"), not a sales tactic.
- Hard-sell language. "Limited time only" — banned.
- Stock photo people. Real Keith. Real Ewa. Real customers (when consented).
