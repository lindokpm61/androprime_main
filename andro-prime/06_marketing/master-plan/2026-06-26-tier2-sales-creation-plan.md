Answers the question the strategy docs stopped short of: **how do we actually create sales in the next 90 days?**

> **Positioning note (2026-07-22):** this plan predates the **conflict-free positioning** Keith adopted 2026-07-22 (`../../01_strategy/2026-07-22-conflict-free-positioning-decision.md`; approved wording **CA-026**). ## Constraints this plan is built around (decided 2026-06-26)

- **Budget: ~£250–500** for paid, over the quarter. One validation test, not a channel. (Cold paid is unprofitable on first-order CAC: ~£180–220 CAC vs ~£45–85 gross profit. Paid is for *learning + list-building*, not profit, yet.)
- **Founder mode: creator, not closer.** Keith on camera in **short-form video (Instagram Reels + YouTube Shorts)**, curiosity/feeling angle ("interesting things about blood"). **Not** a LinkedIn founder-launch, not direct DM-selling.
- **Affiliate/PT (Engine A): frozen.** Solicitor block (CA-001/002 parked). Not relied on in this plan.
- **Near-zero audience:** 23 users, 3 marketing-consented. All sales must come from *new* traffic.
- **Supplements (Phase 0b) target: 6–8 weeks** (lands ~week 6–8, *inside* this quarter). This pulls the subscription profit engine into the plan — but only if the manufacturer RFQ + MOQ + capital are committed in the next ~1–2 weeks (see critical path under Play 5). It also means **fronting the ~£5,950 (phased) supplement capital now**, not self-financing it from kit profits.
- **Fulfilment pipe is proven** (Experiment 0 PASS, 2026-06-25): a real live purchase auto-dispatches to Vitall. Nothing technical blocks a sale.

## The honest truth up front

**There is no fast + cheap + profitable + scalable sales channel available to us right now.** Affiliate is off, we're not doing founder-direct selling, we have £250–500 and no audience. So 90 days realistically delivers: **the first real sales, a seeded audience/list, and a running engine — not volume.** Volume comes later, from the engine compounding (6+ months), the affiliate unfreezing, or more budget. Anyone promising big Q1 sales under these constraints is guessing. This plan is built to be honest about that and to make the constraints compound in our favour.

## "Create sales" decomposes into three jobs

| Job | What it produces | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| **1. First real sales now** | proof strangers will buy + testimonials + first results data | weeks 1–6 |
| **2. Build the owned audience/list** | the asset that makes *future* sales cheap (we have ~0 today) | months 1–3+ |
| **3. Make each customer profitable** | the subscription LTV that justifies any CAC | Phase 0b onward |

The plays below each do one of these jobs. None does all three.

## The plays

### Play 1 — Founder short-form video *(lead fast-lever, ~free)* — Job 1 + 2
Keith on camera, **IG Reels + YouTube Shorts**, 3–5/week. Curiosity/feeling hooks, not clinical lectures: "the number on your blood test that lies on its own", "why you're tired even though your GP said you're fine", "what your ferritin actually is". Pull hooks straight from the **feeling keyword set** we just staged (`tools/staging-feeling-first/`). Every video ends one place: the 2-minute quiz / site.
- **Why it fits:** matches GTM v4 (Keith on-camera, organic social for the younger segment) and the feeling-first doctrine (lead with the feeling). It's demand *creation*, which is exactly right given the thesis has near-zero search demand.
- **Its role: the de-facto accelerant** now that affiliate (Engine A) is frozen. It's owned media that can borrow *rented* algorithmic reach — so unlike blog/SEO (3–6 months to rank), a Short can spike traffic in 48 hours. **But it's an accelerant you can't dial:** post and you might get 200 views or 200k. Treat it as a slot machine, not a throttle — chase the breakout (post often, test hooks hard, and when one hits, feed it: more of that angle, pin it, push it to the quiz). Don't forecast sales as if you can turn it up on demand.
- **Compliance:** health short-form carries ASA/claims risk. Keep it **educational/informational** (how the body/marker works), never treatment/diagnosis/supplement-efficacy claims. Run anything claim-adjacent through the compliance pre-flight; Ewa for anything clinical. The "interesting things about blood" framing is the safe lane.
- **Highest-leverage version:** anchor it on **Keith's own test data over time** (real numbers, retested). Per the flywheel, that single move makes the flytrap content and the proof the *same content* — partially closing the bottom loop before Phase 0b, using your retest as the proof while customers' aren't available yet. Competitors selling pills-on-a-guess can't tell that story.

### Play 2 — Blog/SEO content engine *(compounding, ~free, already live)* — Job 2
Already running (12 articles live, more scheduled). Feeling-first hubs, newsletter capture on every piece. Next: build the missing **feeling doors** (weight/belly, stress, sleep, low mood) per the content-calendar overlay. Slow (3–6 mo to traffic), but it's the only *profitable* acquisition we have, and it compounds. Short-form (Play 1) and blog cross-feed: a Reel becomes a hook for an article and vice versa.

### Play 3 — Capture + nurture *(converts the traffic)* — Job 1 + 2
Quiz + newsletter capture on the back of every Play 1/2 touch. The (now feeling-first) email sequences nurture cold/warm leads toward a first purchase. This is what turns audience into a list, and a list into sales over weeks. The list is the asset that makes the *next* quarter cheaper.

### Play 4 — Google Search validation test (£250, locked) *(the dependable read, runs alongside Play 1)* — Job 1
**Google Search, high-intent test-category terms** ("at home blood test", "testosterone test uk", "mens health blood test", "vitamin d test"). This runs **in parallel with the short-form (Play 1)**, not instead of it. Its job is to be the one lever you *can* dial: with no founder-direct selling and affiliate off, this is the dependable way to read *real purchase intent* (these people buy in-session). Expect **~1–3 sales** for £250 — not profitable, but it (a) proves strangers buy, (b) seeds a **retargeting pixel** that later compounds with the short-form viewer audience, (c) gives a clean conversion read on the live funnel.
- **Tension to manage:** these are commodity, NHS-shadowed terms (price-compressed). The landing page must **reframe from "a blood test" to the feeling/outcome** so we don't anchor to the £30 competitor. Harvest the test-aware buyer; sell them the answer, not the test.
- **Budget: locked at £250.** Short-form (free) carries the reach; the £250 is the dependable read beside it, not the volume play.

### The two front-of-funnel levers, together
Plays 1 and 4 are a **pair**, not alternatives:

| | Play 1 — Short-form | Play 4 — Google Search |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | free | £250 (locked) |
| Speed | fast *spikes*, unpredictable | steady, in-session |
| Control | unbuyable (slot machine) | dialable (throttle) |
| Job | reach + audience + demand creation | dependable real-sales read |
| Reinforces | seeds a viewer audience to retarget | seeds a pixel to retarget |

Same quiz/feeling-first landing, same tracking, two pools you can later retarget together. Short-form chases the breakout; Search guarantees you *some* dependable read while you wait for it.

### Play 5 — Subscription launch at week 6–8 *(the profit engine)* — Job 3
Every kit buyer → results → data-matched supplement → subscription → retest loop. This is what makes *any* customer profitable (a 6-month subscriber is worth ~£165 vs ~£45 for a one-off kit). With supplements landing ~week 6–8, this is no longer "later" — it's mid-quarter, and it changes the back half of the plan.

**The warm-backlog play (don't miss it):** every kit buyer from weeks 0–8 is a **warm subscription cohort**. Launch supplements *into* them — email the matched supplement offer the day it's live — so the subscription starts with instant attach, not a cold start. Weeks 0–8 you're not just selling kits, you're stockpiling a warm list to convert at week 8.

**Two halves switch on at different times:**
- **Money-half (~week 8):** subscription LTV + MRR go live. Every attaching customer flips from ~£45 to ~£165; CAC on Plays 1/4 becomes justifiable; recurring revenue starts stacking. The funnel stops bleeding.
- **Proof-half (~week 16–20, past the quarter):** Vit D / B12 take 8–12 weeks to move, so the first *customer* retests (the referral/word-of-mouth fuel) land ~5–8 weeks after the quarter. Keith's own-retest content (Play 1) bridges this gap in the meantime.

**Critical path — fast via stock/private-label, not custom.** The realistic 6–8 week route is **private-labelling a manufacturer's closest stock men's formulation** (with minor tweaks toward the clean 4-active spec), NOT developing a bespoke V7.2 line. Stock formulas are already stability-tested, which removes the 3-month stability wall and the MOQ/tooling cost — that's what makes the timeline achievable. Full-custom V7.2 is deferred to a later run once volume justifies it. **Guardrail:** "closest stock" must stay clean and close to the 4 targeted actives (Zinc, D3, Methyl-B12, KSM-66), not a generic 20-ingredient multi — that would contradict the data-matched thesis and multiply the compliance surface. Two gating actions in the next ~1–2 weeks: (1) send the 4 ready Tier-1 emails reframed stock-first (`05_partners/manufacturers/`); (2) commit the ~£5,950 phased capital (breaks the self-financing principle — flagged for Keith). **This, not the marketing, is the real gate on the flywheel switching on inside the quarter.** This capital commit *is* **Gate 0A** as restated 2026-07-09 (canonical in `01_strategy/CONTEXT.md` → Gates Reference): a capped-downside **spend authorisation** — stock private-label only, exposure capped at the phased ~£5,950, MOQ write-off survivable, clean 4-active spec held — explicitly a founder bet, not earned demand. The supplement-waitlist opt-in rate is a directional read only, never a threshold.

Back half judged on **attach rate + retention** (the flywheel's leading indicators), not kit count.

## 90-day sequence

**Weeks 0–2 — stand up**
- Launch the short-form cadence; ship the first 5 videos (hooks from the feeling set).
- Build + launch the £250–500 Google Search test; confirm the landing reframes test→feeling.
- Confirm quiz + newsletter capture live on all content; sequences in feeling-first voice (done).
- Verify GA4 + first-party event tracking end-to-end (visitor → quiz → purchase) so we can actually *read* what works.

**Weeks 2–6 — run + read**
- Search test reads real sales; short-form builds; nurture converts the trickle.
- Weekly: which hook/format/term converts? Double down, cut the rest.

**Weeks 6–12 — compound + decide**
- Read results against **the locked bars** — i.e. **Gate 0B** (the unit-economics gate; canonical definition in `01_strategy/CONTEXT.md` → Gates Reference). This week-6–12 decision point **is when Gate 0B is read**; it is not a separate gate. Concretely, the bars are:
  - **Stage 1 (pre-supplement) — hard bar: CPA < kit gross contribution:** £38 (Kit 1) / £53 (Kit 2) / £77 (Kit 3), direct, per the 2026-06-26 LTV:CAC model. A kit sale must pay for its own acquisition before spend scales.
  - **Stage 2 (post-supplement, once attach is observed — not readable before ~week 8) — hard bar: CPA < blended LTV** (a 6-month subscriber ≈ £165).
  - **Soft signals (tie-breakers at low n, not substitutes):** CTR, quiz-starts, initiate-checkout, email captures. At this volume (~5–20 sales) they break ties between options; they never clear the gate on their own.
- Clearing Stage-1 (or Stage-2, once live) is what authorises **scaling paid spend beyond the £250–500 Search test**. Then revisit the three levers that unlock *volume* — **more budget, unfreeze affiliate, accelerate Phase 0b.**

## What 90 days realistically produces

- **Sales:** low double digits at best (~5–20), dominated by the Search test plus any short-form breakout and organic trickle. **Not volume.** Say this out loud so the result isn't misread as failure.
- **The real deliverable:** proof strangers buy, the first testimonials + results data, a seeded email list + retargeting audience, and a running content+video engine. Those are the inputs that make Q2 cheap.

## Compliance + brand guardrails (carry into every play)

- Short-form health video → educational only; no diagnosis/treatment/efficacy claims; pre-flight claim-adjacent copy; Ewa for clinical.
- Feeling-first hooks everywhere; premium positioning held. Only the Search-harvest landing flirts with commodity framing, and even there the page reframes to the outcome.
- Newsletter/content never drives to the FM list (CQC/ASA); CTAs go to Kit 1/2/3.
