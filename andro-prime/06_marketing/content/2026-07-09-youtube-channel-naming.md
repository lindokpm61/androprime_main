## What it does *not* change

The brand-architecture reasoning in `youtube-founder-journey-strategy.md` §2 survives intact, because the Brand Account setup satisfies it:

- **Channel equity stays a company asset.** A Brand Account is transferable and manager-addable, so the subscriber base, search rank, and E-E-A-T moat belong to Andro Prime, not to Keith personally. This was the whole objection to a personal health channel, and it is answered by the account *type*, not by the account *name*.
- **Ages fine as other faces come on camera.** Ewa and any future presenter appear as guests on Keith's founder channel, which is the normal shape for a founder-fronted brand.
- **Still not Keith's existing business-advisory personal brand.** That audience is founders and operators; this one is men searching about their own bloods. Separate accounts, separate analytics.
- **Cross-promotion stays one-way.** LinkedIn (Keith's personal profile) is the founder halo and feeds into the brand channel. The audiences never merge.
- **A founder account is not a compliance loophole.** Every caption and script runs `/compliance-preflight`. ASA complaints land on Andro Prime regardless of whose name is on the handle.

## Why founder-branded wins

The name carries the performance edge (a face and a name out-performs a logo account in both feeds), while the Brand Account type carries the ownership. Naming it after the founder does not leak equity out of the company; hosting it on a personal account would have. The 2026-06-08 decision conflated the two.
